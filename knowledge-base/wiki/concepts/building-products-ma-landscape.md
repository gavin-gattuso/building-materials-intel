# Building Products M&A Landscape 2025-2026

## Overview
The building products M&A market is undergoing a strategic shift from pure scale consolidation toward scope and capability acquisitions. North America is leading global activity with a 33% increase in deal value, while EMEA and APAC regions have declined sharply.

## Key Strategic Shift: Scale to Scope
The traditional playbook of buying competitors for market share is giving way to capability-driven deals. Companies are acquiring to expand product categories, enter new channels (especially pro/professional), and gain technology capabilities.

## Major Transactions (2025-2026)
| Deal | Value | Strategic Rationale |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| QXO → Beacon Roofing Supply | $11.0B | Roofing distribution platform |
| Lowe's → Foundation Building Materials | $8.8B | Pro customer reach |
| QXO → Kodiak Building Partners | $2.25B | Lumber/structural distribution |
| CRH → Eco Material Technologies | $2.1B | Fly ash/pozzolans scale |
| Home Depot → GMS | Undisclosed | Pro channel expansion |

## Consolidation Landscape
- Cement: Highly consolidated (top 6 = 65-70% US capacity)
- Sanitary/plumbing: Fragmented (top 5 = ~25% European share)
- Distribution: Rapidly consolidating via QXO, SRS/Home Depot, Foundation/Lowe's

## Performance Data
- Frequent acquirers: 9.6% total shareholder returns
- Inactive companies: 2.7% TSR
- Due diligence focus shifting to revenue synergies

## Key Players
- **QXO (Brad Jacobs)**: Targeting $50B revenue via roll-up strategy
- **CRH**: Expanding into sustainability-adjacent materials
- **Lowe's / Home Depot**: Racing for pro channel dominance
