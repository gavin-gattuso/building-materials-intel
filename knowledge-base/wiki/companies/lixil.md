# LIXIL Corporation (5938.T)

## Company Overview
LIXIL Corporation is a Japan-headquartered global leader in water technology and housing technology products. The company operates two core business segments: the Water Technology business (including the GROHE, American Standard, and INAX brands) and the Housing Technology business (building materials, housing components, and kitchen systems for the Japanese market). LIXIL is listed on the Tokyo Stock Exchange and has a global presence spanning Asia, Europe, the Americas, and the Middle East.

LIXIL's Water Technology segment is its primary growth driver, with the GROHE brand (premium European faucets and showers) and American Standard serving both residential and commercial plumbing markets worldwide. The Housing Technology segment is heavily focused on Japan's domestic renovation and new housing market, including sashes, doors, kitchens, and bathroom products.

The company has been executing a multi-year structural reform program to improve profitability, particularly in its international water business where European operations have undergone significant restructuring. Recent results show this strategy is bearing fruit, with core earnings surging 60% in H1 FYE2026.

### Acquisitions
LIXIL has historically been an active acquirer with deals including Grohe (2014) and American Standard (2013). Recent M&A focus has been on select bolt-on acquisitions to accelerate technology and market entry, particularly in renovation and water-saving solutions.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **European Restructuring:** Multi-year program to improve profitability in the European water business through plant rationalization, SKU reduction, and cost optimization. Results are showing strong improvement.
- **Renovation Market Focus:** Targeting energy and water-saving renovation solutions in Japan and Europe.
- **Middle East & India Expansion:** Capitalizing on strong construction demand in high-growth emerging markets.
- **Portfolio Simplification:** Exiting non-core businesses (U.S. bathing, ceramic siding) to focus on water technology and core housing products.
- **Automation & Digitalization:** Investing in manufacturing automation and digital customer engagement.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

LIXIL faces significant currency exposure (JPY vs. EUR, USD, CNY) given its global operations. The weak yen has been a mixed factor: supporting export competitiveness but inflating imported raw material costs. Tariff risk centers on cross-border flows between manufacturing hubs in Japan, China, Thailand, and Europe.
