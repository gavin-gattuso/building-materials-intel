# Canfor Corporation (CFP.TO)

## Company Overview

Canfor Corporation is one of the world's largest producers of sustainable lumber and pulp products, headquartered in Vancouver, British Columbia. The company operates sawmills and pulp operations across western Canada, the US South, and Sweden. Canfor's product portfolio includes dimension lumber, specialty wood products, and pulp and paper through its subsidiary Canfor Pulp Products Inc.

Canfor has been undergoing a significant strategic transformation in response to persistent timber supply challenges in British Columbia, weak lumber demand, and escalating US trade duties. The company has closed multiple high-cost mills in Canada and the US Southeast, while shifting investment toward lower-cost operations in the US South and Sweden. Canfor completed the acquisition of the remaining minority stake in Canfor Pulp Products ahead of its expected TSX delisting in March 2026.

The company is one of the Canadian lumber producers most heavily impacted by US softwood lumber duties and the newer Section 232 tariffs, which together create substantial cost headwinds. Canfor recorded a $320.4 million asset write-down and impairment charge in Q4 2025, reflecting the severity of market conditions.

## Recent Earnings

**Q4 2025 Results:**
- Operating loss of $415.9 million and net loss of $390.5 million ($3.35/share)
- Adjusted operating loss of C$145 million versus C$82 million loss in prior-year quarter
- Asset write-down and impairment charge of approximately $320.4 million:
  - $213.9 million related to lumber segment
  - $106.5 million related to pulp and paper segment
  - Includes $52.5 million write-off of previously recognized deferred tax asset
- Results significantly missed analyst expectations; stock fell 6.11% post-earnings

**Key Drivers of Losses:**
- Persistently weak North American lumber demand
- Elevated softwood lumber duties (antidumping + countervailing duties exceeded 35%)
- Section 232 tariffs adding additional 10% on imports from Canada
- Timber supply challenges in northern British Columbia
- Low pulp prices impacting pulp segment

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Geographic Shift:** Moving investment from high-cost British Columbia operations toward US South and Sweden
- **Mill Rationalization:** Permanent closure of uneconomic mills to reduce cost structure (Estill, Darlington, Polar, others)
- **Canfor Pulp Consolidation:** Acquired remaining minority stake; TSX delisting planned for March 2026
- **Capital Discipline:** Reduced 2026 capital spending plan to CAD 175M (lumber) + CAD 35M (pulp)
- **US South Investment:** Focus on lower-cost, duty-free lumber production in the US Southeast
- **Sweden Operations:** Maintaining and potentially expanding European lumber operations via Vida subsidiary

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Canfor is among the most tariff-exposed lumber companies due to its heavy Canadian production base:
- **Softwood Lumber Duties:** Combined antidumping and countervailing duties on Canadian lumber shipments rose from 14.4% to over 35% in 2025
- **Section 232 Tariffs:** Additional 10% ad valorem duty on softwood timber and lumber imports effective October 14, 2025
- **Cumulative Impact:** Duties represent a massive cost burden on approximately 25% or more of Canfor's production
- **Mitigation Strategy:** Shift production to US South (duty-free) and Sweden (different trade relationship)
- **US South mills:** Provide natural hedge as domestic US production avoids import duties
- **Sweden operations:** Subject to different EU-US trade dynamics
