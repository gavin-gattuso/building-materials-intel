# RPM International Inc.

## Company Overview

RPM International Inc. (NYSE: RPM) is a $7.4 billion multinational holding company headquartered in Medina, Ohio, whose subsidiaries manufacture and market specialty coatings, sealants, building materials, and related products for both industrial/commercial and consumer markets. Founded in 1947 and incorporated in Delaware, RPM employs approximately 17,200 people and markets products in roughly 163 countries and territories through manufacturing operations across approximately 118 locations worldwide. The company is consistently ranked among the top five paint and coating companies globally and holds the No. 5 position in the PCI Global Top 10.

RPM operates through four reportable business segments. The **Construction Products Group (CPG)** is the largest segment, accounting for approximately 39% of net sales, and serves commercial and industrial construction markets with roofing systems, wall systems, concrete admixtures and repair products, building envelope solutions, flooring systems, and weatherproofing technologies under brands including Tremco, Dryvit, Nudura, and Euclid Chemical. The **Performance Coatings Group (PCG)**, generating approximately 27% of revenue, supplies high-performance protective coatings, passive fire protection, and corrosion-resistant linings for industrial, infrastructure, and energy markets through brands such as Carboline, Stonhard, and Day-Glo. The **Consumer Group** (~33% of revenue, $2.4B in FY2025) produces professional and DIY products for residential home improvement and leisure markets, featuring flagship brands Rust-Oleum, DAP, Zinsser, and Varathane. The **Specialty Products Group** is a smaller segment covering niche markets including OEM coatings, powder coatings, and fluorescent pigments.

RPM's brand portfolio of over 100 product lines and hundreds of market-leading brands underpins its competitive position across multiple channels. DAP holds the No. 1 brand recognition and market share in U.S. and Canadian caulk and sealant residential markets. Rust-Oleum is among the most recognized consumer coatings brands in North America. Carboline and Tremco serve as anchor industrial brands for corrosion protection and construction waterproofing, respectively. This breadth across consumer and industrial end markets provides meaningful revenue diversification and reduces cyclical concentration, a key structural advantage relative to peers more dependent on single channels.

The company has a long track record of disciplined acquisition-led growth, having completed more than 150 acquisitions since its founding. RPM's fiscal year ends May 31. Its MAP 2025 operational improvement initiative, launched in 2019, concluded at the close of FY2025, driving significant margin expansion, working capital improvements, and record financial results over its six-year run. RPM entered FY2026 positioned as a leaner, higher-margin business with an active acquisition pipeline targeting building envelope and high-performance coatings adjacencies.

## Strategic Positioning

RPM's competitive moat rests on its multi-segment brand portfolio, technical formulation expertise, and deep channel relationships across both specification-driven commercial construction and retail consumer channels. In the industrial/commercial segment, Tremco and Carboline benefit from specification pull — architects, engineers, and building owners specify these products by brand, reducing price sensitivity and creating sticky recurring demand. Stonhard's flooring systems are similarly entrenched in institutional and industrial facilities. In the consumer channel, Rust-Oleum and DAP dominate shelf placement through long-standing retailer relationships with Home Depot and Lowe's. While RPM faces larger peers in Sherwin-Williams and PPG Industries — which possess greater scale and vertical integration — RPM differentiates through niche leadership in specialty categories where proprietary formulations and regulatory approvals create barriers to entry.

The MAP 2025 initiative (Manufacturing, Administration, and Procurement 2025) was a six-year operational transformation launched in 2019 targeting $290M+ in cumulative cost savings through manufacturing footprint rationalization, procurement consolidation, and SG&A discipline. The program concluded at FY2025 year-end having delivered record adjusted EBIT margins every year of its run. Post-MAP 2025, RPM has pivoted toward an "engineered solutions for high-performance buildings" growth narrative, focusing organic and inorganic investment on higher-margin commercial construction products, passive fire protection, and building envelope systems. The company's Asia-Pacific expansion — including a new production facility in Serendah, Malaysia opened in FY2025 — reflects efforts to capture share in high-growth emerging construction markets. With its largest-ever M&A year complete in FY2025, RPM entered FY2026 with a strong balance sheet and active deal pipeline.

## Recent Developments

- **Q3 FY2026 Record Results (April 8, 2026):** RPM reported record Q3 sales of $1.61B (+~9% YoY) and adjusted EPS of $0.57, beating consensus by over 50%. Adjusted EBIT rose approximately 50% to a record, driven by volume gains, SG&A optimization, and fixed cost leverage. CPG and PCG both posted record sales and EBIT. Consumer Group remained soft (-2.4% organic) due to continued DIY demand weakness and retailer cautiousness. Stock surged ~15% on the report.
- **Kalzip GmbH Acquisition Completed (March 31, 2026):** RPM's Tremco CPG completed the acquisition of Kalzip GmbH, a Koblenz, Germany-based global leader in aluminum-based roofing and facade systems (landmark projects include El Prat Airport, Barcelona and Mercedes-Benz Arena, Berlin). Kalzip generated ~€75M in 2024 revenue. The acquisition expands Tremco CPG's aluminum envelope systems offering beyond Europe and India into North America.
- **MAP 2025 Initiative Completed (FY2025):** RPM successfully concluded its six-year MAP 2025 operational improvement program, having delivered record adjusted EBIT margins, improved working capital, and record financial results every year of the program. The company exited MAP 2025 at a 13.2% adjusted EBIT margin — a structural step-change from pre-initiative levels.
- **FY2025 Record Acquisition Spending:** Fiscal 2025 was RPM's largest-ever year of acquisition investment. Notable deals included the acquisition of **Star Brands Group** (The Pink Stuff) for the Rust-Oleum/Consumer segment, **TMP Convert SAS** (decking and landscaping products), and **Ready Seal Inc.** Additional bolt-on acquisitions expanded footprint across CPG and PCG.
- **Malaysia Manufacturing Expansion (FY2025):** Opened new state-of-the-art production facility in Serendah, Malaysia to serve the Asia-Pacific construction and coatings markets, advancing the company's emerging markets growth strategy.
- **Dividend History:** RPM is a Dividend King with over 50 consecutive years of dividend increases. The MAP 2025 strategy supported a 5.9% dividend increase during its run.
- **Q4 FY2026 Outlook:** Management guided for mid-single-digit consolidated sales growth and low- to high-single-digit adjusted EBIT growth versus prior-year record Q4, signaling continued momentum heading into fiscal year-end despite macroeconomic uncertainty.
