# Carrier Global Corporation (CARR)

## Company Overview
Carrier Global Corporation is a global leader in intelligent climate and energy solutions, providing HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning), refrigeration, and fire & security products and services. The company was spun off from United Technologies in 2020 and has since undergone a dramatic portfolio transformation to become a pure-play climate and HVAC company.

The transformational acquisition of Viessmann Climate Solutions for EUR 12B (completed January 2024) made Carrier the leading provider of heating solutions in Europe, combining Viessmann's heat pump and boiler technology with Carrier's global HVAC platform. Simultaneously, Carrier divested its fire & security businesses and commercial refrigeration units to streamline its focus on climate solutions.

With approximately $21.75B in annual revenue, Carrier now operates primarily in commercial HVAC (including the growing data center cooling segment), residential HVAC and heating (strengthened by Viessmann), and transport refrigeration. The company is benefiting from strong secular demand for energy-efficient cooling, decarbonization solutions, and data center thermal management.

### Viessmann Integration Progress
The Viessmann acquisition has moved from being modestly dilutive to EPS in 2024 to accretive in 2025, with management targeting increasing accretion in 2026 and beyond. The European heat pump market has been recovering slowly but the long-term regulatory tailwind (EU Green Deal) remains intact.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Data Center Cooling:** Targeting sixth consecutive year of double-digit commercial HVAC growth in 2026, driven by hyperscale and colocation data center builds. Q4 2025 orders up ~50%.
- **Viessmann Synergies:** Accelerating integration to capture procurement, manufacturing, and commercial synergies from the EUR 12B acquisition.
- **Decarbonization Solutions:** Positioning as a leader in the transition from fossil-fuel heating to heat pumps and electrified HVAC in Europe and North America.
- **Aftermarket & Services:** Building recurring revenue streams through service contracts, parts, and digital building management.
- **Capital Return:** Plans to repurchase $1.5B in shares during 2026.
- **Portfolio Simplification:** Completing remaining divestitures (Riello) to achieve pure-play climate positioning.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Carrier faces moderate tariff exposure across its global operations:
- European manufacturing (Viessmann) could be impacted by U.S.-EU trade tensions
- Chinese HVAC operations and component sourcing exposed to U.S.-China tariffs
- Refrigerant supply chains may be affected by trade policy on specialty chemicals
- The company's global manufacturing footprint (North America, Europe, Asia) provides some production flexibility
