# Carlisle Companies (CSL)

## Company Overview
Carlisle Companies is a leading diversified manufacturer of premium building products, primarily focused on commercial roofing, waterproofing, and insulation through its two main segments: Carlisle Construction Materials (CCM) and Carlisle Weatherproofing Technologies (CWT). The company serves the non-residential construction market with a strong emphasis on energy-efficient building envelope solutions.

Carlisle has undergone a significant portfolio transformation over the past several years, divesting non-core businesses (including its interconnect technologies segment) to focus purely on building products. The company's Vision 2030 strategy targets best-in-class EBITDA margins, return on invested capital, and disciplined capital allocation across M&A and shareholder returns.

With approximately $5.0B in annual revenue, Carlisle holds leading market positions in single-ply roofing membranes (TPO/EPDM), spray polyurethane foam insulation, and commercial waterproofing systems. The company benefits from structural tailwinds including the aging U.S. commercial roofing installed base, energy efficiency mandates, and growing demand for sustainable building materials.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

Carlisle has a stated capital allocation target of deploying $4B into acquisitions between 2024-2026, though actual spending has lagged:

| Year | M&A Spending | Notable Deals |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| 2021 | ~$1.6B | Henry Company (largest deal to date) |
| 2025 | $110M | Bonded Logic / Phoenix Fibers |
| 2024-2026 Target | $4.0B | Pipeline remains active |

**Bonded Logic (May 2025):** Acquired select assets of Bonded Logic, Inc. and Phoenix Fibers, LLC, a U.S. manufacturer of sustainable thermal and acoustical insulation products with facilities in Arizona and Georgia. This acquisition expands Carlisle's sustainable insulation offerings.

The M&A environment in 2025 proved challenging (elevated valuations, limited quality targets), leading Carlisle to redirect capital toward share repurchases. Management expects to continue pursuing value-enhancing acquisitions in 2026.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Vision 2030:** Framework targeting best-in-class margins, ROIC, and revenue growth through organic investment and M&A.
- **Sustainable Building Solutions:** Expanding energy-efficient roofing, insulation, and waterproofing product lines aligned with building codes and ESG mandates.
- **Pricing Discipline:** Maintaining price leadership in premium commercial roofing categories.
- **Capital Return:** Aggressive shareholder return program with plans to repurchase up to $1B of shares in 2026.
- **Acquisition Pipeline:** Targeting $4B deployment across 2024-2026 in building envelope adjacencies.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Carlisle's manufacturing footprint is predominantly U.S.-based, providing substantial insulation from tariff risk. However, certain raw materials (petrochemicals, specialty chemicals) may be subject to tariff-driven cost inflation. The company's pricing power and market-leading positions have historically enabled pass-through of input cost increases.
