# Steel Dynamics Inc. (STLD)

## Company Overview

Steel Dynamics, Inc. is one of the largest domestic steel producers and metals recyclers in the United States, headquartered in Fort Wayne, Indiana. The company operates through three segments: Steel Operations, Metals Recycling, and Steel Fabrication. Steel Dynamics also has a new Aluminum Operations segment following the commissioning of its Columbus, Mississippi flat-rolled aluminum mill.

Steel Dynamics operates electric arc furnace (EAF) steel mills across the United States, producing sheet steel, structural steel, rail, bar, and specialty shapes. The company's state-of-the-art Sinton, Texas flat-roll mill (commissioned 2022) is one of the most advanced steel mills in the world. The Metals Recycling segment operates one of the largest ferrous and nonferrous metals recycling operations in North America, providing internal scrap supply to its steelmaking operations.

In 2025, Steel Dynamics achieved record annual steel shipments of 13.7 million tons and generated $18.2 billion in net sales. The company's entry into flat-rolled aluminum represents a significant strategic diversification, with the Columbus mill expected to deliver $650-700 million in through-cycle EBITDA. Management raised the expected aluminum mill utilization target to approaching 90% by end of 2026, up from the prior 75% expectation.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

- **Major Section 232 Beneficiary:** As a large domestic steel producer, STLD benefits significantly from 50% steel tariffs
- **Aluminum Tariffs:** Aluminum operations also benefit from Section 232 aluminum tariffs (50%)
- **Domestic Production:** All operations US-based, fully insulated from import duties
- **Scrap-Based Production:** EAF technology uses domestic scrap, limiting raw material tariff exposure
- **Derivative Products:** Expanded Section 232 coverage of steel/aluminum derivative products further protects downstream fabrication
- **Competitive Advantage:** Tariffs support domestic pricing, utilization rates, and investment returns
- **Net Assessment:** Strongly net positive; dual benefit from both steel and aluminum tariffs
