# West Fraser Timber (WFG)

## Company Overview

West Fraser Timber Co. Ltd. is one of the largest lumber producers in North America, headquartered in Vancouver, British Columbia. The company produces lumber, panels (plywood and OSB), pulp, and other wood products across a diversified network of operations in western Canada, the US South, the US Northwest, and Europe (UK operations). West Fraser is dual-listed on the TSX and NYSE.

West Fraser's product portfolio includes SPF (spruce-pine-fir) lumber, SYP (southern yellow pine) lumber, plywood, oriented strand board (OSB), newsprint, and kraft pulp. The company operates more than 30 sawmills, panel plants, and pulp mills. West Fraser has historically been one of the most profitable lumber producers, but 2025 proved to be an exceptionally challenging year with nearly $1 billion in net losses driven by restructuring charges, impairments, and weak market conditions.

The company announced permanent closures of multiple mills in 2025, reducing production capacity by approximately 300 million board feet. West Fraser recorded $712 million in restructuring and impairment charges tied to mill closures and goodwill write-downs, reflecting the severity of the downturn in lumber markets and the structural challenges in British Columbia's timber supply.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Mill Rationalization:** Permanent closure of four mills (Augusta, 100 Mile House, Huttig, Lake Butler) to reduce high-cost capacity
- **Capacity Reduction:** Approximately 300 MMBF of production capacity permanently removed
- **Cost Structure Optimization:** Focusing on lowest-cost operations in US South and remaining efficient Canadian mills
- **Capital Preservation:** Reduced capital spending to preserve balance sheet during downturn
- **Operational Efficiency:** Continuing to invest in technology and upgrades at remaining mills to improve productivity

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

West Fraser faces significant tariff headwinds on its Canadian operations:
- **Softwood Lumber Duties:** Combined AD/CVD duties on Canadian lumber exceeding 35%
- **Section 232 Tariffs:** Additional 10% duty effective October 2025
- **BC Exposure:** Canadian operations face both duty burden and timber supply constraints
- **US South Operations:** Provide duty-free production, representing a growing share of the portfolio
- **European Operations:** UK operations subject to different trade dynamics
- **Panel Products:** OSB and plywood may face different tariff treatment than dimension lumber
- **Impact Magnitude:** Duties and tariffs are a primary driver of mill closures and financial losses
