# CRH plc (CRH)

## Company Overview

CRH plc is the largest building materials company in North America and among the largest globally, with operations spanning 28 countries. Headquartered in Dublin, Ireland, and listed on the NYSE following its 2023 primary listing move from the London Stock Exchange, CRH produces and distributes cement, aggregates, ready-mixed concrete, asphalt, and a broad range of building products. The company was added to the S&P 500 index in 2023, marking a transformative milestone in its capital markets profile.

CRH operates through two primary segments: Americas Materials Solutions and Building Solutions, and International Materials Solutions and Products. The Americas segment is the dominant earnings driver, benefiting from robust U.S. infrastructure spending underpinned by the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) and state-level highway programs. Internationally, CRH maintains significant positions in Europe, including cement and aggregates operations across the UK, Ireland, continental Europe, and emerging markets.

With a market capitalization exceeding $60 billion, CRH is the clear scale leader in North American heavy-side building materials. Its vertically integrated model -- controlling quarries, cement plants, ready-mix operations, and asphalt paving -- provides significant competitive moats through logistics advantages and permitting barriers. The company's disciplined M&A program has been a hallmark of its growth strategy for decades.

## Recent Earnings

**Q4 and Full-Year 2025 (reported February 18, 2026):**

CRH delivered record full-year results in 2025. Total revenues of $37.4 billion were 5% ahead of the prior year, driven by favorable end-market demand, disciplined commercial execution, and contributions from acquisitions. Adjusted EBITDA of $7.7 billion increased 11%, reflecting continued pricing momentum, acquisition contributions, and disciplined cost control. Net income of $3.8 billion was 8% ahead of the prior year.

Both net income margin (10.1%) and Adjusted EBITDA margin (20.5%) expanded year-over-year, demonstrating operational leverage and the accretive nature of recent acquisitions. The stock declined modestly on a slight EPS miss relative to consensus despite otherwise strong results.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

CRH deployed $5.8 billion in value-accretive growth investments during 2025, completing 38 acquisitions.

**Key Transactions:**

- **Eco Material Technologies ($2.1B, completed September 2025):** North America's leading supplier of Supplementary Cementitious Materials (SCMs), including fly ash and natural pozzolans. This positions CRH to capitalize on the growing demand for low-carbon concrete.
- **North American Aggregates (December 2025):** Aggregates assets acquisition expanding CRH's footprint; sold Minnesota aggregates and FOB asphalt assets to Martin Marietta.
- **27+ bolt-on acquisitions through Q3 2025:** Smaller deals across cement, aggregates, and building products in key U.S. and European markets.

**2026 M&A Outlook:**

At its September 2025 Investor Day, CRH outlined $40 billion of financial capacity over 2026-2030 (after maintenance capex), signaling aggressive acquisition ambitions will continue. Management has historically averaged $3-5 billion in annual M&A spending and is expected to maintain or accelerate this pace.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **2030 Financial Targets (announced September 2025 Investor Day):** 7-9% annual revenue growth (organic + inorganic), Adjusted EBITDA margins of 22-24%, and adjusted free cash flow conversion exceeding 100%.
- **S&P 500 membership:** Continues to drive index fund inflows and broaden the U.S. investor base.
- **Infrastructure positioning:** CRH is the single largest beneficiary of U.S. federal and state infrastructure spending among publicly traded building materials companies, with leading positions in highway paving, bridges, and utility construction.
- **Sustainability:** Eco Material Technologies acquisition positions CRH as a leader in supplementary cementitious materials, supporting the industry transition to lower-carbon concrete.
- **Vertical integration:** Continued emphasis on controlling the full value chain from quarry to final placement, creating logistics moats and pricing power.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

CRH is among the least exposed building materials companies to tariff risk, as the vast majority of its products (aggregates, cement, ready-mix concrete, asphalt) are produced and consumed domestically. Key considerations:

- **Minimal import exposure:** Aggregates and cement are heavy, low-value-per-ton commodities that are overwhelmingly sourced locally. CRH's U.S. operations are primarily supplied from domestic quarries and cement plants.
- **Indirect benefit:** Tariffs on imported steel (50%) and aluminum (50%) increase costs for competing building materials (steel structures), making concrete and asphalt-based solutions relatively more attractive.
- **Input cost exposure:** Some equipment, spare parts, and specialty materials may face modest tariff impacts, but these are a small share of total costs.
- **Infrastructure spending risk:** The IIJA is scheduled to expire in October 2026, and broader federal spending cuts could slow future project flow, which would be a larger risk than tariffs for CRH.
