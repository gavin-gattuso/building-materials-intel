# Taiheiyo Cement (5233.T)

## Company Overview

Taiheiyo Cement Corporation is Japan's largest cement manufacturer and one of the largest in the Asia-Pacific region, headquartered in Tokyo, Japan. The company produces and sells cement, ready-mixed concrete, aggregates, and related construction materials across Japan, the United States, China, Vietnam, the Philippines, and Papua New Guinea. Its U.S. operations are conducted through its wholly-owned subsidiary CalPortland Company, one of the largest cement and construction materials producers in the western United States.

Taiheiyo Cement was formed through the 1998 merger of Nihon Cement, Chichibu Onoda Cement, and other entities, creating a dominant domestic position. The company holds approximately 35% of Japan's domestic cement market, but faces structural headwinds from Japan's declining construction volumes due to demographic contraction. This has driven the company to aggressively expand internationally, particularly in the United States through CalPortland.

With fiscal year 2024-25 (ending March 2025) revenue of JPY 896.3 billion (~$5.7 billion), Taiheiyo is a mid-size global player. The company is navigating a complex period, with strong prior-year results giving way to a challenging FY2025-26 marked by profit declines domestically, even as it pursues transformative acquisitions in the U.S. market.

## Recent Earnings

**FY2024-25 Full Year (ended March 2025):**

Taiheiyo delivered strong results with net sales increasing 1.1% to JPY 896.3 billion. Operating profit surged 37.7% to JPY 77.8 billion, driven by domestic price revisions, stabilizing coal costs, and continued strength in the U.S. business through CalPortland. Operating margins improved dramatically from 6.4% to 8.7%.

**FY2025-26 Interim Results (current fiscal year):**

The current fiscal year has been markedly weaker:
- Q1 (ended June 2025): Net sales up 1.1% but operating profit down 8.9% and net income down 49.6%
- H1 (ended September 2025): Net sales down 1.2% to JPY 438.1B, operating profit down 9.7%, net income down 18.9%
- 9M (ended December 2025): Net sales JPY 671.3B (-1.6%), net income collapsed to JPY 17.8B from JPY 52.4B (-66.1%)

The sharp profit decline reflects weak domestic cement volumes, higher input costs, and impairment or one-time charges. The U.S. business through CalPortland has remained a relative bright spot.

**Full-Year FY2025-26 Forecast:**

Taiheiyo projects net sales of JPY 906.0 billion (+1.1% YoY) and net income attributable to owners of JPY 45.0 billion (-21.6% YoY) for the full year ending March 2026.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

**CalPortland Acquisition of Vulcan Materials' California Ready-Mix (~$712M):**

Taiheiyo Cement's U.S. subsidiary CalPortland entered into an agreement to acquire Vulcan Materials' ready-mixed concrete business in California for approximately $712 million. The assets comprise 41 concrete plants and two cementitious material terminals in San Diego and the San Francisco Bay Area. The deal was initially expected to close by December 2025 but has been delayed to H1 2026 after the DOJ issued a Second Request under the Hart-Scott-Rodino Act.

This acquisition would give CalPortland an unrivaled position in coastal California ready-mix, with leading positions in San Diego, Los Angeles, and the San Francisco Bay Area. The Northern California assets provide exposure to data center construction driven by AI demand.

**Tokuyama Cement Sales Business (~JPY 37B / ~$250M):**

Tokuyama Corporation will transfer its domestic cement and solidification agent sales business to Taiheiyo Cement for approximately JPY 37 billion. Subject to regulatory approvals, completion is targeted for October 2026. This would further consolidate Taiheiyo's dominant position in the shrinking Japanese domestic cement market.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **U.S. expansion through CalPortland:** CalPortland is the primary growth vehicle, with the Vulcan ready-mix acquisition representing a major step up in California market position. CalPortland operates cement plants, ready-mix concrete, and aggregates across the western U.S.
- **Domestic consolidation:** The Tokuyama deal exemplifies Taiheiyo's strategy of consolidating Japan's declining cement market to optimize capacity utilization and pricing.
- **Carbon-neutral strategy (C2SP / "Apollo Project"):** Taiheiyo is accelerating development of its carbon-capturing kiln technology, internally referred to as the "Apollo Project" due to its scale and technological ambition. This represents a long-term bet on decarbonized cement production.
- **Activist pressure:** Palliser Capital published a value enhancement plan for Taiheiyo Cement in October 2025, pushing for improved capital allocation, higher shareholder returns, and strategic actions to close the valuation discount versus global peers.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Taiheiyo has complex tariff exposure across its Japan-U.S. operations:

- **U.S. operations (CalPortland):** CalPortland's U.S. cement and aggregates operations are predominantly domestic, with limited tariff exposure. The Vulcan ready-mix acquisition would further localize the California supply chain.
- **Japan-U.S. cement trade:** Taiheiyo exports some cement clinker from Japan to the U.S. West Coast. U.S. tariffs on Japanese imports (10% baseline global tariff, potential for higher rates) could increase costs for this trade route.
- **Japan domestic:** Japan's cement market is essentially self-sufficient, with minimal import competition and no significant tariff impacts.
- **Asian operations:** Vietnam, Philippines, and Papua New Guinea operations serve local markets with limited tariff complications.
- **Input costs:** Coal and petroleum coke (primary kiln fuels) are globally traded commodities subject to price volatility but generally not tariff-affected within existing trade relationships.
