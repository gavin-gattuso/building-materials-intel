# UFP Industries (UFPI)

## Company Overview

UFP Industries, Inc. is a leading manufacturer of wood and wood-alternative products, headquartered in Grand Rapids, Michigan. The company serves three primary end markets: Retail (consumer lumber and outdoor living products), Packaging (industrial packaging and pallets), and Construction (factory-built housing components, structural lumber, and engineered wood products). UFP Industries is one of the largest purchasers and processors of lumber in the United States.

The company has evolved from a traditional lumber distributor into a value-added manufacturer, with a growing portfolio of wood-alternative and composite products. UFP Industries operates over 200 manufacturing, distribution, and sales facilities across North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia. The company's three business segments -- UFP Retail Solutions, UFP Packaging, and UFP Construction -- each target specific market needs with specialized product offerings.

UFP Industries maintains a strong balance sheet with approximately $2.2 billion in liquidity and has an aggressive growth strategy targeting 7-10% annual unit sales growth (including bolt-on acquisitions) with at least 10% of sales from new products. The company announced up to $1 billion in capital investments through 2028 for automation, technology upgrades, geographic expansion, and capacity increases.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

- **Lumber Cost Impact:** As one of the largest US lumber purchasers, UFP is exposed to lumber price volatility driven by tariffs
- **Section 232 Tariffs:** 10% duty on imported softwood lumber effective October 2025 increases input costs
- **Softwood Lumber Duties:** AD/CVD duties on Canadian lumber affect UFP's raw material costs
- **Mitigation:** Strong domestic sourcing capabilities; ability to pass through cost increases to customers with a lag
- **Wood-Alternative Products:** Growing portfolio of composite/alternative products provides some insulation from lumber tariff impacts
- **International Operations:** European, Asian, and Australian operations have separate trade dynamics
