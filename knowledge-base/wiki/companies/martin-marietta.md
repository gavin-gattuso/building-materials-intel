# Martin Marietta Materials (MLM)

## Company Overview

Martin Marietta Materials, Inc. is one of the largest producers of construction aggregates (crushed stone, sand, and gravel) in the United States, headquartered in Raleigh, North Carolina. The company also produces cement, ready-mixed concrete, asphalt, and magnesia-based products. Martin Marietta is listed on the NYSE and is a component of the S&P 500 index.

The company operates primarily through two segments: Building Materials (aggregates, cement, ready-mix, asphalt) and Magnesia Specialties (lime and magnesia-based chemicals and minerals). Martin Marietta holds leading aggregates positions in Texas, the Carolinas, Georgia, Colorado, Indiana, Iowa, and other high-growth U.S. markets. Its reserves of approximately 15+ billion tons of permitted aggregates provide decades of supply and represent an irreplaceable competitive asset.

Following the completion of a transformative asset exchange with Quikrete Holdings in February 2026, Martin Marietta has significantly sharpened its aggregates-led strategy, divesting cyclical cement and ready-mix assets in exchange for pure-play aggregates operations. With FY 2025 revenues of $6.15 billion and Adjusted EBITDA of $2.07 billion, Martin Marietta is positioned as the second-largest U.S. aggregates producer behind Vulcan Materials.

## Recent Earnings

**Q4 and Full-Year 2025 (reported February 11, 2026):**

Full-year revenues from continuing operations rose to $6.15 billion from $5.66 billion, with gross profit increasing to $1.89 billion. Adjusted EBITDA from continuing operations grew to $2.07 billion from $1.77 billion, representing 17% growth. Aggregates shipments rose to 198.5 million tons with average selling price up to $23.30 per ton, lifting aggregates gross profit per ton by 12% year-over-year.

Net earnings from continuing operations declined to $990 million from $1.82 billion, but this was entirely due to a nonrecurring divestiture gain in the prior year rather than weaker operations.

Management stated: "2025 was another year of strong growth for Martin Marietta. Our aggregates business once again delivered record profitability and meaningful margin expansion, reflecting strong strategic and commercial discipline."

The stock saw pressure as Q4 earnings missed analyst forecasts despite the strong full-year performance.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

**Quikrete Holdings Asset Exchange (completed February 23, 2026):**

Martin Marietta completed a landmark tax-efficient asset exchange with Quikrete Holdings, the largest aggregates acquisition in company history. Martin Marietta received:
- Aggregates operations producing approximately 20 million tons annually in Virginia, Missouri, Kansas, and Vancouver, British Columbia
- $450 million in cash

In return, Martin Marietta transferred:
- Midlothian, Texas cement plant and related cement terminals
- Texas ready-mix concrete operations
- Certain nonoperating land

This deal is the capstone of the company's SOAR 2025 strategic plan and decisively pivots Martin Marietta toward a pure-play aggregates model.

**Minnesota Aggregates Acquisition from CRH (December 19, 2025):**

Acquired aggregates and FOB asphalt assets in Minnesota from CRH, expanding operations in the Twin Cities and St. Cloud markets. The assets include approximately 40 million tons of aggregate reserves.

**Premier Magnesia Acquisition (completed July 25, 2025):**

Acquired Premier Magnesia, a privately-owned producer of magnesia-based products with operations in Nevada, North Carolina, Indiana, and Pennsylvania. This bolsters the Magnesia Specialties segment.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **SOAR 2030:** The successor to SOAR 2025, launching with the Quikrete exchange as its foundation. Focus on aggregates-led growth, margin expansion, and strategic M&A.
- **Aggregates-led strategy:** The Quikrete exchange demonstrates Martin Marietta's conviction that aggregates offer the best risk-adjusted returns in building materials, with superior pricing power, margins, and reserve longevity.
- **Geographic diversification:** The Quikrete exchange and CRH Minnesota deal expand Martin Marietta's presence into Virginia, Missouri, Kansas, British Columbia, and Minnesota -- markets with attractive growth dynamics.
- **Pricing discipline:** Average selling price growth of 4-6% guided for 2026, reflecting continued discipline and the favorable supply-demand balance in U.S. aggregates.
- **Reserve management:** The company controls billions of tons of permitted reserves, creating multi-decade visibility on supply and significant barriers to entry.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Martin Marietta has minimal direct tariff exposure:

- **Domestic product:** Aggregates are locally sourced and consumed within relatively short trucking distances (typically under 50 miles). There is virtually no cross-border trade in aggregates.
- **No import dependence:** Martin Marietta's quarries, cement plants (now being divested), and distribution are almost entirely domestic.
- **Indirect infrastructure impact:** The primary tariff risk for Martin Marietta is macro: if tariffs slow economic growth or reduce federal infrastructure spending, demand for aggregates could soften. However, most aggregates demand is driven by public infrastructure (highways, bridges, water systems), which is funded by dedicated trust funds and state budgets less sensitive to tariff-driven economic cycles.
- **Input costs:** Diesel fuel, explosives, and equipment/parts may face modest tariff-related cost increases, but these are manageable within the company's margin structure.
