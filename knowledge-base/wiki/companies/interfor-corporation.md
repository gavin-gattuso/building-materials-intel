# Interfor Corporation (IFP.TO)

## Company Overview

Interfor Corporation is one of the world's largest lumber producers, headquartered in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada. The company operates sawmills across western Canada, the US South, and the US Northwest, producing a diversified range of lumber products including dimension lumber, studs, and specialty wood products. Interfor's geographic diversification strategy has been a key competitive advantage, providing exposure to multiple timber baskets and end markets.

Interfor produces approximately 3 billion board feet of lumber annually across its mill network. The company's US operations, particularly in the South, are a strategic advantage as they are not subject to Canadian softwood lumber duties. However, Interfor's Canadian operations face significant headwinds from combined antidumping and countervailing duties that rose from 14.4% to over 35%, plus additional Section 232 tariffs of 10% on Canadian lumber imports.

The company has paid cumulative duties of US$663.5 million ($10.09 per share on an after-tax basis) as of December 31, 2025, highlighting the enormous financial burden of the US-Canada softwood lumber dispute. Despite these challenges, Interfor maintains a strong balance sheet and continues to invest in its mill network to improve operational efficiency and product mix.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Interfor faces among the highest tariff burdens in the lumber industry:
- **Softwood Lumber Duties:** Combined AD/CVD duties rose from 14.4% to over 35% on Canadian lumber shipments
- **Section 232 Tariffs:** Additional 10% ad valorem duty effective October 14, 2025
- **Duty Payments:** Cumulative US$663.5 million in duties paid as of December 31, 2025
- **Exposure Split:** Approximately 25% of total lumber shipments subject to Canadian duties; remainder from US operations (duty-free)
- **Mitigation:** US South operations provide natural hedge as domestic production avoids import duties
- **Potential Upside:** Any resolution of the softwood lumber dispute or duty reduction would be materially positive
