# JELD-WEN Holding (JELD)

## Company Overview
JELD-WEN is one of the world's largest manufacturers of interior and exterior doors, windows, and related building products. Headquartered in Charlotte, North Carolina, the company operates manufacturing facilities across North America, Europe, and Australasia. JELD-WEN's products are sold through retail home centers, wholesale distributors, and directly to homebuilders and contractors.

The company is currently in the midst of a challenging turnaround, having experienced significant revenue and earnings declines driven by the court-ordered divestiture of its Towanda, Pennsylvania facility, soft end-market demand, and operational restructuring costs. Net loss widened sharply in 2025 as JELD-WEN undertook workforce reductions and strategic realignment actions.

JELD-WEN's core brands include JELD-WEN doors and windows, LaCantina luxury folding door systems, VPI commercial doors, and various regional brands across Europe and Australia. The company serves both new residential construction and repair/remodel markets.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Operational Restructuring:** Significant workforce reductions announced in Q3 2025, with additional cost reduction measures underway.
- **Transformation Journey:** JELD-WEN is executing a multi-year operational transformation to improve manufacturing efficiency, reduce complexity, and right-size the cost structure.
- **Market Focus:** Concentrating on core window and door categories with stronger margin profiles.
- **Cost Reduction:** Aggressive SG&A and manufacturing cost optimization programs.
- **Portfolio Simplification:** Evaluating product lines and geographic presence for potential rationalization.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

JELD-WEN has significant tariff exposure due to its global manufacturing footprint and cross-border material flows:
- Imported glass, hardware, and specialty components from Asia subject to tariffs
- Canadian lumber used in North American manufacturing operations
- European operations (significant revenue contribution) face separate trade policy dynamics
- Currency translation risk (EUR, GBP, AUD vs. USD)

The company's already-pressured margins make it particularly vulnerable to tariff-driven cost inflation that cannot be passed through in a soft demand environment.
