# Daikin Industries (6367.T)

## Company Overview
Daikin Industries, Ltd. is the world's largest manufacturer of HVAC (heating, ventilation, and air conditioning) systems, headquartered in Osaka, Japan. With a fiscal year ending March 31, the company operates across three core segments: HVAC & Refrigeration (air conditioners, heat pumps, applied systems), Chemicals (fluorochemicals, refrigerants), and Other (oil hydraulics, defense). Daikin is the global leader in both residential and commercial air conditioning, holding number one or two market positions across Japan, Asia, Europe, and North America.

Daikin's HVAC dominance spans the full spectrum from residential split systems to large-scale applied commercial systems, including the rapidly growing data center cooling segment. The company's Goodman brand (acquired in 2012) is a leading North American residential HVAC manufacturer, while Daikin Applied provides commercial and industrial HVAC solutions. The Chemicals segment produces fluoropolymers and refrigerants, positioning Daikin uniquely across both the equipment and consumable sides of the climate control industry.

The company has been rapidly expanding its data center cooling capabilities through targeted acquisitions and new manufacturing investments, recognizing the AI-driven explosion in cooling demand for hyperscale facilities.

## Recent Earnings

### Q3 FY2026 (Nine Months Ended December 31, 2025)
Revenue grew 2.0% to JPY 3.666 trillion but operating profit declined 3.4% to JPY 307.9B, reflecting cost pressures and margin compression in certain geographies. Net profit rose 4.6% to JPY 195.4B due to below-the-line items.

Daikin raised its full-year sales forecast to JPY 4.920T (up 1.7% from prior guidance) but lowered its operating profit forecast to JPY 413.0B (down 5.1%), reflecting the challenging operating environment including U.S. tariff uncertainty and input cost pressures.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Data Center Cooling Leadership:** Building comprehensive end-to-end cooling solutions for AI and hyperscale data centers through acquisitions (DDC Solutions, Chilldyne) and new manufacturing capacity.
- **Global HVAC Leadership:** Maintaining world number one position across residential and commercial HVAC categories.
- **Heat Pump Expansion:** Growing European heat pump presence aligned with EU decarbonization mandates and the transition from fossil heating.
- **Chemical Synergies:** Leveraging fluorochemical expertise in next-generation low-GWP refrigerants required by regulatory phase-downs.
- **Emerging Market Growth:** Expanding market share in Southeast Asia, India, and other high-growth markets.
- **Manufacturing Investment:** Significant capacity additions across multiple geographies to meet growing demand.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Daikin faces multiple tariff and trade headwinds:
- U.S. tariff policies creating uncertainty for Goodman's North American operations (some components imported from Asia)
- JPY weakness/volatility impacting reported results and cross-border manufacturing economics
- European regulatory requirements for refrigerant transitions adding compliance costs
- China manufacturing operations exposed to potential trade restrictions
- Global supply chain disruptions for electronic components and specialty metals

Management has highlighted U.S. tariff policies as a key factor weighing on the global economy during Q3 results.
