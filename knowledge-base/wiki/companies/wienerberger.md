# Wienerberger AG (WIE.VI)

## Company Overview

Wienerberger AG is the world's largest producer of bricks and one of the leading suppliers of building materials and infrastructure solutions, headquartered in Vienna, Austria. Founded in 1819, the company operates across over 200 production sites in 28 countries, primarily in Europe and North America. Wienerberger's product portfolio includes clay building materials (bricks, roof tiles, facade systems), concrete and calcium silicate products, plastic and ceramic pipes, and surface solutions.

Wienerberger serves three primary end markets: New Build Residential, Renovation, and Infrastructure (water and energy management). The company has been strategically transforming from a traditional brick manufacturer into a comprehensive provider of innovative building and infrastructure solutions. Key product categories include wall systems, roof systems, facade solutions, pavers, pipe systems, and surface solutions.

With group revenues of EUR 4.6 billion in 2025 and approximately 19,000 employees, Wienerberger is a major European building materials company. The company has announced the transformative acquisition of Italcer Group, a leading Italian ceramic solutions producer, which is expected to close in Q2 2026 and contribute more than EUR 100 million to group EBITDA in the medium term. Note: While classified under the Building Materials sector, Wienerberger's primary products (bricks, tiles, pipes) span ceramic/clay materials rather than steel.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

- **European Focus:** Primarily European production and sales footprint limits direct US tariff exposure
- **North American Operations:** US and Canadian operations face local market dynamics
- **Energy Costs:** European production heavily dependent on natural gas; energy price volatility is a key exposure
- **EU Trade Policy:** Subject to EU construction product regulations and trade agreements
- **Raw Materials:** Clay, sand, and ceramic raw materials primarily locally sourced, limiting import dependency
- **Competitive Environment:** European brick and tile markets face competition from alternative materials rather than imports
- **Italcer Integration:** Italian and Spanish ceramic manufacturing may face trade considerations for export markets
- **Net Assessment:** Relatively limited tariff exposure compared to steel/lumber peers; energy costs are the primary cost variable
