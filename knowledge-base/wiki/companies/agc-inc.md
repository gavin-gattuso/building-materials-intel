# AGC Inc. (5201.T)

## Company Overview

AGC Inc. (formerly Asahi Glass Co.) is a Japan-based multinational corporation and one of the world's largest glass manufacturers. Founded in 1907 and headquartered in Tokyo, AGC operates across four core business segments: Architectural Glass, Automotive Glass, Electronics, and Chemicals. The company produces flat glass, automotive glass, display glass, specialty chemicals, ceramics, and advanced electronic materials. AGC serves the construction, automotive, electronics, and chemical industries globally, with operations spanning Asia, Europe, and the Americas.

AGC holds leading global market positions in architectural glass, automotive glass, and EUV photomask blanks for semiconductor manufacturing. The company is a critical supplier in the semiconductor supply chain through its EUV mask blank technology. With annual revenue exceeding JPY 2 trillion (approximately $13-14 billion), AGC is the largest glass company in Asia and among the top three globally alongside Saint-Gobain and NSG/Pilkington.

The company has been undergoing a strategic portfolio transformation under its medium-term management plan "AGC plus-2026," emphasizing "ambidextrous management" to balance core business optimization with growth investments in high-value segments like electronics and life sciences.

- ROE of 4.7%
- Q4 2025 revenue of JPY 546.69 billion (0.14% above forecast)
- Q3 2025 was a standout quarter with operating profit surpassing JPY 40 billion for the first time in three years

**Segment Highlights:**
- Electronics segment benefited from strong EUV mask blank demand (achieved JPY 40B in sales in 2024, one year ahead of target)

AGC's M&A strategy under the plus-2026 plan focuses on portfolio optimization rather than aggressive acquisitions. The company is prioritizing structural reforms in low-ROCE businesses and selective investment in high-growth areas. Large-scale capacity expansion investments were completed in 2025, with new investments to be carefully selected going forward.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **AGC plus-2026 Medium-Term Plan:** "Ambidextrous management" strategy targeting a business portfolio resilient to market fluctuations with high asset efficiency, growth potential, and carbon efficiency
- **EUV Photomask Blanks:** Key growth driver in semiconductor supply chain; sales expected to increase again from 2026 onward after hitting JPY 40B milestone ahead of schedule
- **Thailand Facility Expansion:** New expanded facility expected to start operations in 2025 and contribute to profits from 2026
- **Structural Reforms:** Accelerating reforms in businesses with low ROCE while maintaining investment in innovation
- **Carbon Efficiency:** Targeting improved carbon efficiency across the portfolio as part of sustainability strategy
- **Portfolio Transformation:** Active rotation between core and strategic businesses to optimize returns

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

AGC's global manufacturing footprint mitigates some tariff risk, but the company faces exposure through:
- **Glass imports:** Potential impact from trade tensions between Japan, China, and the US on glass products
- **Chemicals segment:** Cross-border chemical trade subject to various tariff regimes
- **Electronics components:** Semiconductor supply chain subject to export controls and trade restrictions between US, Japan, and China
- **European operations:** AGC Glass Europe operations face energy cost pressures and EU carbon border adjustment mechanism (CBAM) considerations
- **Mitigation:** Localized manufacturing in key markets (Japan, Europe, Southeast Asia) provides natural hedging against trade barriers
