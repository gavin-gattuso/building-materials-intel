# Geberit AG (GEBN.SW)

## Company Overview
Geberit is the European market leader in sanitary products and piping systems, headquartered in Rapperswil-Jona, Switzerland. The company operates across three product areas: Installation & Flushing Systems (concealed cisterns, actuator plates, flushing systems), Piping Systems (supply and drainage piping, fittings), and Bathroom Systems (bathroom ceramics, furniture, shower products).

Founded in 1874, Geberit has built a dominant position in European sanitary technology through a combination of product innovation, plumber-focused go-to-market strategy, and premium brand positioning. The company sells primarily through professional plumbing trade channels and has developed deep relationships with installers, architects, and specifiers. Geberit's products are specified in new construction and renovation projects across residential and commercial buildings.

With CHF 3.16B in annual revenue and EBITDA margins near 30%, Geberit is one of the most profitable companies in the building products sector. The company generates approximately 95% of revenue in Europe, making it heavily dependent on European construction activity. Germany is the single largest market, followed by Switzerland, Benelux, Nordics, and Southern Europe.

## Recent Earnings

### Full Year 2025 Results

Net sales grew 4.8% in local currencies driven by volume recovery, though CHF-reported growth was dampened to 2.5% by currency headwinds. The volume growth marked a return to positive territory after two years of declining volumes in European construction markets.

**Regional Performance:**
- **Germany:** +5.6% (currency-adjusted) - strongest performance in the core market
- **Middle East & Africa:** +24.8% (currency-adjusted) - fastest growth region
- **Far East & Pacific:** -0.6% - Chinese market continued to decline

Net income was essentially flat at CHF 598M as EBITDA margin slipped 20 bps to 29.4%, reflecting higher input costs and investment in new marketing initiatives. EPS rose 0.5% to CHF 18.15, outpacing net income growth due to share buybacks.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

Geberit has pursued a highly disciplined approach to M&A, with no significant acquisitions announced in 2025-2026. The company prefers organic growth through innovation and market penetration over acquisitive expansion.

**Financial Capacity:** Net debt to EBITDA of 1.2x provides significant balance sheet flexibility for potential tuck-in acquisitions, but management has indicated strict return criteria must be met.

**Share Buyback Program (2024-2026):** A CHF 300M share repurchase program launched in September 2024 is ongoing, representing the primary capital return mechanism alongside dividends.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **New Marketing Initiative:** Launching a dedicated consumer/architect marketing program with incremental spending of CHF 20M in 2026 to increase brand pull-through with end consumers and designers.
- **Product Innovation:** Continuous new product introductions across all three product areas, including smart bathroom solutions and water-efficient technologies.
- **Digitalization & AI:** Ongoing investment in digital tools for plumber training, specification support, and AI-enhanced product development.
- **Sustainability Leadership:** Market-leading position in water-efficient products supporting European Green Deal and building renovation mandates.
- **Selective M&A:** Open to bolt-on acquisitions meeting strict criteria, with balance sheet capacity to support deals.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Geberit's manufacturing footprint is predominantly European (plants in Germany, Switzerland, Austria, Poland, Ukraine, and elsewhere in Europe), with products sold primarily within Europe. This local-for-local model limits direct tariff exposure. However:
- Raw material costs (brass, copper, ceramics, plastics) can be affected by global trade dynamics
- CHF strength against EUR remains a persistent headwind given the company reports in CHF but generates most revenue in EUR
- Minimal direct U.S./China tariff exposure given European market focus

## 2026 Outlook & Guidance

Geberit has indicated that "slight market growth is expected in 2026 overall, but no market recovery yet." The company expects:
- Slightly higher direct material prices in Q1 2026 vs. Q4 2025 (though down YoY)
- Incremental CHF 20M marketing investment
- Continued share buyback program execution

The next earnings report is scheduled for May 5, 2026. Key catalysts include European renovation activity (driven by energy efficiency mandates), German construction market stabilization, and continued Middle East growth.
