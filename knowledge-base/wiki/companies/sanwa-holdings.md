# Sanwa Holdings Corporation (5929.T)

## Company Overview
Sanwa Holdings Corporation is a Japan-based global manufacturer of doors, shutters, partitions, and access solutions. The company operates through three geographic segments: Japan (Sanwa Shutter Corporation), North America (Overhead Door Corporation), and Europe (Novoferm). With trailing twelve-month revenue of approximately $4.4B (JPY 660B+), Sanwa Holdings is one of the world's largest door and access systems manufacturers.

The company's key subsidiary, Overhead Door Corporation (acquired from Sanwa), is a leading North American provider of commercial and residential overhead garage doors, access control systems, and loading dock equipment. Overhead Door operates through more than 400 distributors across the United States and serves residential, commercial, institutional, and industrial end markets through three divisions focused on vehicular access, pedestrian access, and electronic access solutions.

In Europe, the Novoferm subsidiary manufactures industrial and residential doors, loading technology, and steel frames primarily for the German, Dutch, French, and broader European markets.

## Recent Earnings

### H1 FY2025 (Six Months Ended September 30, 2025)

Record-high profits were driven by strong Japan business performance, offsetting weaker overseas results. Operating profit rose 4.3% to JPY 33.7B and profit attributable to owners increased 11.3% to JPY 26.45B, exceeding initial forecasts.

**Segment Performance:**
- **Japan:** Strong results driven by robust domestic construction demand and pricing actions
- **North America (Overhead Door):** Results pressured by U.S. tariffs, trade friction, and slower commercial construction activity
- **Europe (Novoferm):** Challenged by high interest rates and slow recovery in European construction markets

Comprehensive income dropped to JPY 15.83B from JPY 47.22B primarily due to negative foreign currency translation adjustments (weak yen reversal impacting overseas asset valuations).

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

Sanwa Holdings has historically grown through acquisition, with the Overhead Door Corporation acquisition being the transformational deal that established its North American presence. Recent M&A activity has been limited:

- **Overhead Door subsidiary:** Completed acquisitions of Door Control and Door Concepts to strengthen its U.S. distribution and service network.
- No major platform acquisitions announced in 2025-2026.

The company's strategy focuses on bolt-on acquisitions to strengthen existing market positions rather than large transformational deals.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Japan Market Leadership:** Maintaining dominant position in Japanese door and shutter market through product innovation and service network expansion.
- **North American Growth:** Expanding Overhead Door's market reach through distribution network growth and new product introductions.
- **Enhanced Shareholder Returns:** New share repurchase program and increased dividends announced in 2025.
- **Operational Efficiency:** Cost reduction programs across all three regional segments.
- **Electronic Access Solutions:** Growing electronic and smart access product lines aligned with industry digitization trends.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Sanwa Holdings has material tariff and trade exposure:
- U.S. tariffs on imported steel and aluminum directly impact Overhead Door's manufacturing costs
- Trade friction between U.S. and key trading partners adds uncertainty for North American operations
- FX exposure (JPY vs. USD, EUR) significantly impacts reported results
- European operations face separate challenges from elevated interest rates and soft construction demand

Management has noted that the tariff environment is a key headwind for North American operations in the near term.

## 2026 Outlook & Guidance

Initial FY2025 full-year forecasts have been maintained:
- Japan expected to continue driving results with stable construction demand
- Overseas markets (North America and Europe) expected to remain sluggish
- North America and Europe face slow recovery due to tariffs and high interest rates
- Market recovery expected from FY2026 (April 2026 onward)

Key catalysts include eventual interest rate easing in the U.S. and Europe, resolution of trade tensions, and recovery in commercial construction activity.
