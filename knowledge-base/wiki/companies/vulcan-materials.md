# Vulcan Materials (VMC)

## Company Overview

Vulcan Materials Company is the largest producer of construction aggregates (crushed stone, sand, and gravel) in the United States and a major producer of aggregates-based construction materials including asphalt mix and ready-mixed concrete. Headquartered in Birmingham, Alabama, and listed on the NYSE as a component of the S&P 500, Vulcan is the clear industry leader with an unmatched reserve base and geographic footprint.

Vulcan operates approximately 400 active quarries and aggregate distribution facilities across the United States, with leading market positions in high-growth Sun Belt states including Texas, California, Florida, Georgia, Virginia, Tennessee, and the Carolinas. The company's approximately 17+ billion tons of proven and probable reserves represent decades of irreplaceable supply, creating substantial barriers to entry given the extreme difficulty and cost of permitting new quarries.

With FY 2025 total revenues of $7.9 billion and Adjusted EBITDA of $2.3 billion, Vulcan continues to execute a disciplined aggregates-focused strategy emphasizing pricing power, operational efficiency, and margin expansion. The company has been actively reshaping its portfolio to increase aggregates concentration, including the divestiture of its California ready-mix operations to CalPortland (Taiheiyo Cement subsidiary).

## Recent Earnings

**Q4 and Full-Year 2025 (reported February 17, 2026):**

Vulcan Materials reported record results with total revenues of $7,941 million, net earnings of $1,077 million (up 18% from $912 million), and Adjusted EBITDA of $2,324 million (up 13% year-over-year). Aggregates cash gross profit per ton reached a record $11.33, demonstrating the company's continued pricing power and operational discipline.

Operating cash flow reached $1.8 billion, enabling the company to return $698 million to shareholders through $438 million in share repurchases and $260 million in dividends while maintaining a strong balance sheet for future growth investments.

Management expressed encouragement about the demand backdrop in 2026, citing continued strength in public construction activity and improving private nonresidential opportunities.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

**California Ready-Mix Divestiture to CalPortland (~$712M, expected H1 2026):**

Vulcan entered into an agreement to sell its California ready-mixed concrete business to CalPortland Company (a subsidiary of Taiheiyo Cement) for approximately $712 million. The assets include 41 concrete plants and two cementitious material terminals in San Diego and the San Francisco Bay Area. The closing was originally expected by December 2025 but has been delayed to H1 2026 after the DOJ issued a Second Request under the Hart-Scott-Rodino Act.

This divestiture is consistent with Vulcan's aggregates-led strategy, shedding downstream assets to focus on the highest-margin, highest-return portion of the value chain and generating cash for redeployment.

**Recent Acquisitions (2024-2025):**
- **Superior Ready Mix (December 2024):** Ready-mix concrete and construction materials in Escondido, California.
- **Wake Stone:** Aggregates acquisition in North Carolina.
- **Syar Industries:** Aggregates and construction materials in Northern California.

Acquisitions had an approximately 100 basis point impact on 2025 results.

**M&A Strategy:**

Vulcan maintains a disciplined M&A outlook focused on aggregates-led growth. Management has indicated the company plans to continue leveraging its strong balance sheet and available credit facilities to pursue strategic acquisitions, particularly in aggregates.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Aggregates-led strategy:** Vulcan is systematically reshaping its portfolio to increase aggregates concentration, as evidenced by the California ready-mix divestiture. Aggregates offer the best unit economics (pricing power, margins, reserve longevity) in building materials.
- **Pricing discipline:** Vulcan guides for 4-6% aggregate pricing growth in 2026, reflecting the favorable supply-demand balance and the irreplaceable nature of permitted reserves.
- **Operational efficiency:** Continued focus on cost control, technology adoption (autonomous hauling, advanced drilling), and plant optimization to expand unit margins.
- **Capital allocation:** Balanced approach between growth investments (M&A, greenfield/brownfield expansion), maintenance capex, and shareholder returns (dividends + buybacks).
- **Reserve development:** Ongoing permitting and development of new reserves and expansion of existing quarries, particularly in high-growth markets.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Vulcan Materials has among the lowest tariff exposure of any major building materials company:

- **Purely domestic product:** Construction aggregates are heavy, low-value-per-ton commodities that are produced and consumed within short distances (typically under 30-50 miles by truck). There is essentially zero cross-border trade in aggregates.
- **No import dependence:** All of Vulcan's quarries and production facilities are domestic. The company does not import aggregates or any significant raw materials.
- **Indirect benefit from steel tariffs:** Tariffs on imported steel (50%) and aluminum (50%) increase costs for steel-based construction, potentially making concrete and aggregate-intensive construction methods relatively more cost-competitive.
- **Infrastructure spending sensitivity:** The primary macro risk is that tariff-driven economic disruption could slow construction activity. However, Vulcan's core demand drivers -- public infrastructure (highways, bridges, water systems) -- are funded by dedicated trust funds and less sensitive to tariff-driven cycles.
- **Input cost exposure:** Diesel fuel, explosives, equipment, and spare parts may face modest tariff-related cost increases, but these are a small share of total production costs and are manageable within the pricing environment.

- IIJA expiration in October 2026 without renewal
- Federal spending cuts reducing public construction activity
- Residential construction slowdown from elevated mortgage rates
- DOJ review delays on CalPortland/Vulcan transaction
