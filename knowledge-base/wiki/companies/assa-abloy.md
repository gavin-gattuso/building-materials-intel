# ASSA ABLOY (ASSA-B.ST)

## Company Overview
ASSA ABLOY is the global leader in access solutions, headquartered in Stockholm, Sweden. The company manufactures and sells locks, doors, access control systems, and related security products through four divisions: Americas, EMEIA, Asia Pacific, and Global Technologies. With annual revenue of approximately SEK 152B (~$14.5B USD), ASSA ABLOY holds the number one or two market position in virtually every market segment it serves.

Founded in 1994 through the merger of Swedish ASSA and Finnish Abloy, ASSA ABLOY has grown primarily through acquisition to become the dominant player in physical access control. The company has completed over 300 acquisitions, making it one of the most prolific serial acquirers in the building products industry. Its portfolio spans mechanical locks, electromechanical locks, digital access solutions, entrance automation, and security technology.

The company is well-positioned for the secular shift from mechanical to electronic and connected access solutions. The penetration of electronic locks remains low globally (estimated 5-10% of installed base), providing a long runway for growth as buildings upgrade to smart access control, mobile credentialing, and integrated security platforms.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Electrification of Access:** Accelerating the shift from mechanical to electronic access solutions, targeting significant growth in smart locks, mobile credentials, and cloud-based access management.
- **Acquisitive Growth Model:** Continuing the proven M&A engine with 20+ acquisitions annually, focusing on technology-led access solutions and geographic expansion.
- **Margin Expansion:** Record EBITDA and EBIT margins reflect ongoing operational efficiency improvements and mix enrichment.
- **Connected Solutions Platform:** Building an integrated digital ecosystem for physical security management.
- **Sustainability:** Increasing focus on sustainable manufacturing and product lifecycle environmental impact.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

As a Swedish-headquartered company with global manufacturing operations, ASSA ABLOY faces FX translation risk (SEK vs. USD, EUR, GBP). Direct tariff exposure is moderated by local-for-local manufacturing in key markets. However, cross-border component flows (electronics, specialty metals) could be impacted by escalating trade tensions. The company's diversified geographic footprint provides natural hedging.
