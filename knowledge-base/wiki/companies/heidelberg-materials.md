# Heidelberg Materials (HEI.DE)

## Company Overview

Heidelberg Materials AG (formerly HeidelbergCement) is one of the world's largest integrated manufacturers of building materials, headquartered in Heidelberg, Germany. The company operates across approximately 50 countries and is a global leader in cement, aggregates, ready-mixed concrete, and asphalt production. Heidelberg Materials is listed on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange and is a constituent of the DAX index.

The company operates through four geographic segments: Western and Southern Europe, Northern and Eastern Europe-Central Asia, North America, and Asia-Pacific. It holds leading market positions in cement in Germany, the UK, Australia, and several Asian markets, and has a growing aggregates platform in North America, Australia, and Europe.

Heidelberg Materials has distinguished itself through an aggressive sustainability strategy, particularly in carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS). The company operates the world's first industrial-scale CCS facility at Brevik, Norway, and is developing multiple additional CCUS projects across Europe and North America. With FY 2025 revenue of EUR 21.5 billion and a record result from current operations (RCO) of EUR 3.4 billion, the company is delivering on both financial performance and decarbonization commitments.

## Recent Earnings

**FY 2025 Results (reported February 25, 2026):**

Heidelberg Materials closed 2025 with record results across virtually all key metrics. Group revenue increased to EUR 21.5 billion, while the result from current operations (RCO) rose to a new record of EUR 3.4 billion, up 6% year-over-year. The RCOBD margin expanded to 21.8%, and return on invested capital (ROIC) reached 10.4%, significantly exceeding the 9.9% achieved in the prior year. Free cash flow remained at a very high level of EUR 2.1 billion.

**Q3 2025 (Nine Months ended September 2025):**

The company reported strong third-quarter results that prompted management to confirm the full-year outlook for 2025, with particular strength in North America and improving conditions in European markets.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

**Maas Group Construction Materials (AUD 1.7B, announced February 2026):**

Heidelberg Materials agreed to acquire the construction materials segment of Australia's Maas Group for an enterprise value of AUD 1.7 billion. The assets include 40 aggregate quarries producing 7-8 million tons, approximately 1 million cubic meters of concrete, plus asphalt and recycling operations. The implied EBITDA multiple is 8.4x after synergies. Completion is expected in H2 2026. This is a transformative deal for Heidelberg's Australian footprint.

**2025 Acquisitions:**
- **BURNCO Rock Products Ltd (Canada):** Expanded aggregates platform in western Canada.
- **Walan Specialty Construction Products (USA):** Expanded circular and low-carbon product portfolio in North America.
- **Midway Concrete (Australia):** Ready-mixed concrete business bolstering Australian operations.

**M&A Pipeline:**

CEO Dominik von Achten has signaled the company's M&A pipeline is "clearly full" and investors should expect more deal activity in 2026 than in 2025. Leverage is expected to increase modestly to around 1.5x EBITDA (from the Maas acquisition), well within the midterm target range of 1.5-2.0x.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **CCUS Leadership:** Heidelberg Materials is the undisputed leader in carbon capture for the cement industry:
  - **Brevik CCS (Norway):** Inaugurated mid-June 2025, the world's first industrial-scale CCS facility in cement. Captures approximately 400,000 tonnes of CO2 per year.
  - **Edmonton CCUS Hub (Canada):** Partnership with the Canadian and Alberta governments. Scheduled to be operational by late 2026, capturing over 1 million tonnes of CO2 annually. This will be the first full-scale CCUS solution for the global cement industry.
  - **Padeswood (UK):** Reached a funding agreement with the UK government in September 2025 for a carbon capture facility expected to capture around 800,000 tonnes of CO2 per year.
  - **Ennigerloh (Germany):** In collaboration with Calix and a European consortium, building a facility four times the size of Brevik.
- **10 million tonne target:** Pledged to cut cumulative CO2 emissions by 10 million tonnes through CCUS projects by 2030.
- **Circular economy:** Growing investments in recycled aggregates and alternative raw materials.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Heidelberg Materials has moderate tariff exposure, mitigated by its predominantly local production model:

- **North American operations:** U.S. and Canadian cement and aggregates operations are largely domestically sourced. The BURNCO acquisition strengthens Canadian aggregates self-sufficiency.
- **European exposure:** EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) may actually benefit Heidelberg by leveling the playing field against cheaper imports from regions without carbon pricing.
- **Australian operations:** The Maas Group acquisition will further localize the supply chain in Australia.
- **Cross-border trade:** Some cement clinker trade between European plants could face friction, but Heidelberg's scale and plant footprint provide flexibility.
- **Input costs:** Energy and fuel costs (coal, petcoke, electricity) remain the dominant cost driver, more impactful than tariffs.
