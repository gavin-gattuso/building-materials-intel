# Owens Corning (OC)

## Company Overview

Owens Corning is a global building and construction materials company headquartered in Toledo, Ohio. The company operates through three primary segments: Roofing, Insulation, and Doors (formerly part of the Composites segment, restructured following the Masonite acquisition). Owens Corning is the world's largest manufacturer of fiberglass insulation and a leading producer of residential roofing shingles in North America.

Following its transformative $3.9 billion acquisition of Masonite International in May 2024, Owens Corning expanded into interior and exterior doors, growing annual revenue to approximately $10.1 billion. The company divested its glass reinforcements business as part of its portfolio transformation, focusing on building envelope solutions. Owens Corning serves residential and commercial construction markets primarily in North America, with growing international operations in Europe, Asia Pacific, and Latin America.

The company has a strong track record of capital returns, with a commitment to return $2.0 billion to shareholders by end of 2026 through dividends and share repurchases. Owens Corning benefits from a repair and remodel-weighted demand profile, particularly in its Roofing segment, which provides relative earnings stability.

## Recent Earnings

**Q4 2025 Results:**
- Net sales of $2.14 billion, down 17% from prior-year period of $2.57 billion
- Adjusted EPS of $1.10, missing consensus estimate of $1.36 by approximately 19%
- Weakness driven by softer demand across all segments and weather impacts

**Full Year 2025 Results:**
- Net sales from continuing operations of $10.1 billion, up 3% year-over-year
- GAAP net earnings margin from continuing operations of negative 2%, driven by $1.2 billion non-cash impairment charge on Doors business
- Adjusted EBITDA margin from continuing operations of 22%
- Operating cash flow of $1.8 billion; free cash flow of $1.0 billion
- Returned $1.0 billion to shareholders through dividends and buybacks

**Doors Segment Impairment:**

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Masonite Integration:** Targeting approximately $125 million of run-rate cost synergies from the 2024 acquisition
- **Shareholder Returns:** Commitment to return $2.0 billion to shareholders by end of 2026; quarterly dividend increased 15% to $0.79/share in December 2025
- **Insulation Expansion:** New fiberglass insulation line in Kansas City, Kansas, providing flexible production for residential and non-residential customers
- **Portfolio Optimization:** Transformation from composites/glass reinforcements toward building envelope solutions (roofing + insulation + doors)
- **Operational Excellence:** Focus on manufacturing efficiency and cost management across all segments

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

- **Doors segment:** Primary tariff exposure; gross tariff impact of approximately $110 million in 2025, mitigated to $30 million net impact primarily in Doors
- **Q1 2026 tariff exposure:** Approximately $20 million gross, netting to roughly $10 million after mitigating actions, primarily in Doors
- **Roofing:** Largely domestically produced with limited import exposure
- **Insulation:** Primarily domestic manufacturing base limits direct tariff impact
- **Raw materials:** Some raw material inputs subject to tariff fluctuations
- **Supreme Court decision monitoring:** Company monitoring the dynamic tariff environment due to recent Supreme Court decision that could impact overall tariff exposure
