# CEMEX (CX)

## Company Overview

CEMEX is a global building materials company headquartered in Monterrey, Mexico, and one of the world's largest producers of cement, ready-mix concrete, and aggregates. The company operates in more than 50 countries across the Americas, Europe, Africa, the Middle East, and Asia, with a particularly strong presence in Mexico, the United States, and select European markets (UK, Germany, Poland, Czech Republic, Spain).

CEMEX has undergone a significant strategic transformation over the past several years, shifting from a heavily leveraged, commodity-focused cement producer to a more diversified building materials platform with an emphasis on higher-margin performance products, urbanization solutions, and sustainability. The company's "Sprint" strategic framework (2026-2027) targets accelerated EBITDA growth and margin expansion while maintaining disciplined capital allocation.

With trailing twelve-month revenue of approximately $15.8 billion and a growing presence in the U.S. market through bolt-on acquisitions, CEMEX competes with global peers like Holcim, Heidelberg Materials, and CRH while maintaining a differentiated position through its Mexican production base and cross-border logistics capabilities.

## Recent Earnings

**Q3 2025 Results:**

CEMEX reported revenue of $4.25 billion for the quarter ending September 30, 2025, representing 4.7% growth. The standout metric was EBITDA growth of 19% year-over-year, demonstrating the company's margin expansion strategy is gaining traction. Double-digit EBITDA growth and margin expansion was achieved across all geographic regions.

**H1 2025 Results:**

CEMEX accelerated its strategic transformation and reported record net income in the first half of 2025. Q2 2025 net sales were $4.1 billion with EBITDA of $823 million.

**Revenue Trend:**

Full-year 2024 revenue was $16.2 billion, down 2.1% year-over-year. The trailing twelve-month revenue through September 2025 showed a similar trend at approximately $15.8 billion, reflecting volume softness in some markets offset by pricing gains and mix improvement.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

**Omega Products International (announced February 2026):**

CEMEX reached an agreement to acquire Omega Products International, a leading stucco manufacturer in the western United States with approximately $23 million in annual EBITDA. Omega operates four production facilities in California, Nevada, and Colorado, with over 50 years of experience. The transaction is expected to close in Q1 2026. This deal aligns with CEMEX's strategy to expand into higher-margin performance materials in the U.S.

**Organizational M&A Build-out:**

In November 2025, CEMEX US appointed Will Price as Senior Vice President of Mergers & Acquisitions to lead strategic growth initiatives through targeted acquisitions, divestitures, and corporate development activities across the U.S. business. This signals an accelerating M&A pipeline.

**Strategic Focus:**

CEMEX's bolt-on M&A focus remains primarily in the U.S. in highly synergistic businesses such as aggregates and mortars, while selectively divesting non-core assets globally.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Sprint Framework (2026-2027):** CEMEX expects sales and EBITDA to grow annually by approximately 5% and 10%, respectively. Targets 45% free cash flow conversion in 2026 and 50% by 2027.
- **Performance Materials:** Expanding beyond commodity cement into stucco, mortars, and specialty products with higher margins and less cyclicality.
- **Mexico demand growth:** Expects 2.5-3% demand growth in Mexico in 2026, supported by nearshoring trends and infrastructure investment.
- **European pricing:** Plans potential price increases in Europe and the U.S. to offset inflation.
- **Sustainability:** CEMEX has been an early mover in alternative fuels and clinker substitution, with a growing portfolio of low-carbon products.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

CEMEX has significant and complex tariff exposure given its Mexico-based production and cross-border trade:

- **25% U.S. tariff on Mexican cement (effective April 5, 2025):** The U.S. imposed a 25% tariff on cement imports from Canada and Mexico. However, CEMEX products compliant with USMCA rules of origin may be exempt. CEMEX has stated it is in a "good position" to manage tariff impacts.
- **Surcharge pass-through:** CEMEX announced it would introduce an immediate surcharge to pass tariff costs to consumers.
- **Cross-border supply optimization:** Plans to supply more from Mexican plants via rail (Torreon plant in central Mexico) and sea (east Mexican coastal plants) to optimize logistics costs against tariff impacts.
- **Historic antidumping context:** The U.S. and Mexico agreed to resolve a longstanding antidumping order on Mexican cement, which had been in place for decades. The tariff environment has effectively replaced this with a broader trade barrier.
- **Net beneficiary potential:** If tariffs reduce cement import competition in the U.S. market, CEMEX's domestic U.S. production capacity could see improved utilization and pricing power.
