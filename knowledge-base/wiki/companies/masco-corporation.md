# Masco Corporation (MAS)

## Company Overview
Masco Corporation is a global leader in home improvement and building products, operating through two segments: Plumbing Products and Decorative Architectural Products. Headquartered in Livonia, Michigan, Masco's portfolio includes some of the most recognized brands in residential construction and renovation: Delta and Hansgrohe faucets, BrassCraft plumbing supply, Behr and KILZ paints and stains, and Liberty Hardware decorative hardware.

With approximately $7.6B in annual revenue, Masco holds leading market positions in North American faucets (Delta), European premium faucets (Hansgrohe), paint and coatings (Behr, sold exclusively through Home Depot), and decorative architectural hardware. The company generates roughly two-thirds of its revenue from repair and remodel (R&R) activity, providing more stability than new construction-dependent peers.

Masco's Plumbing Products segment (~$5.0B, 66% of revenue) has been the stronger performer, benefiting from pricing power and global brand strength. The Decorative Architectural Products segment (~$2.6B) has been pressured by the Kichler Lighting divestiture and softer coatings demand. The company's capital allocation strategy emphasizes share repurchases and dividends, with selective bolt-on M&A.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Plumbing Innovation:** Expanding Delta and Hansgrohe product lines with touchless, smart water, and premium design offerings.
- **Behr/Home Depot Partnership:** Deepening the exclusive Behr paint relationship with Home Depot through product innovation and in-store experience enhancement.
- **Capital Return:** Aggressive shareholder return program with $2.0B new repurchase authorization.
- **Long-Term EPS Growth Target:** Targeting ~10% average annual EPS growth through organic growth (3-5%), M&A (1-3%), margin expansion, and capital deployment.
- **Cost Productivity:** Ongoing manufacturing efficiency and SG&A optimization programs.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Masco faces moderate tariff exposure:
- Hansgrohe products manufactured in Germany for North American sale could be impacted by cross-Atlantic tariffs
- Some faucet and hardware components sourced from China
- Decorative hardware (Liberty) has China sourcing exposure
- U.S.-manufactured paint products (Behr) have minimal direct tariff risk
- The company has been diversifying its supply chain to reduce single-country concentration
