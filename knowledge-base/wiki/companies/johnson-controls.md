# Johnson Controls International (JCI)

## Company Overview
Johnson Controls International plc is a global leader in smart, healthy, and sustainable buildings, providing building automation systems, HVAC equipment, fire detection and suppression systems, and security solutions. Headquartered in Cork, Ireland (with major operational presence in Milwaukee, Wisconsin), the company serves commercial, institutional, and government buildings worldwide.

Following the transformational $8.1B divestiture of its residential and light commercial HVAC business to Bosch (completed August 2025), Johnson Controls has become a pure-play commercial building technology company. The company's revenue now comes from two primary segments: commercial HVAC (~60%) and fire & security (~40%). This strategic refocusing positions JCI to capitalize on the secular growth in building digitalization, data center infrastructure, and decarbonization.

The company holds a record backlog of $15B, driven by surging demand for data center cooling, building management systems, and fire & security solutions. JCI's integrated approach combining HVAC, controls, fire, and security into comprehensive building solutions provides a competitive differentiation versus point-solution competitors.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Pure-Play Commercial Buildings:** With the Bosch divestiture complete, JCI is fully focused on the higher-margin, faster-growing commercial building technology market.
- **Data Center Solutions:** Capitalizing on massive demand for data center HVAC, cooling, fire suppression, and security systems. Record backlog driven by data center wins.
- **Building Digitalization:** OpenBlue digital platform for building management, energy optimization, and predictive maintenance.
- **Decarbonization:** Providing solutions for commercial building electrification, heat recovery, and energy efficiency aligned with net-zero commitments.
- **Massive Capital Return:** $5.0B accelerated share repurchase using Bosch divestiture proceeds.
- **Service & Aftermarket Growth:** Expanding recurring service revenue streams through long-term contracts and connected building solutions.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

JCI has moderate tariff exposure through its global manufacturing and supply chain:
- HVAC equipment manufacturing in multiple countries (U.S., Mexico, China, Europe)
- Fire & security product components sourced globally
- Electronics and controls components with Asia-Pacific supply chain exposure
- The pure-play commercial focus may provide better pricing power to pass through tariff costs than residential-exposed peers

## 2026 Outlook & Guidance

Johnson Controls raised its FY2026 guidance following strong Q1 results. Key highlights:
- Continued acceleration in data center and decarbonization demand
- Record $15B backlog providing strong revenue visibility
- Organic growth of 6%+ demonstrated in most recent quarter
- Ongoing benefit from $5.0B share repurchase program

Key catalysts include data center construction acceleration, commercial building renovation for energy efficiency, and the full run-rate benefit of the residential HVAC divestiture/share buyback.
