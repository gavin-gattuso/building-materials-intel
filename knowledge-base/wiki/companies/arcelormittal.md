# ArcelorMittal (MT)

## Company Overview

ArcelorMittal S.A. is the world's largest steel and mining company, headquartered in Luxembourg City, Luxembourg. The company was formed in 2006 through the merger of Arcelor and Mittal Steel. ArcelorMittal operates in over 60 countries, producing a comprehensive range of steel products including flat carbon steel, long carbon steel, stainless steel, and tubular products. The company also has significant iron ore and coal mining operations, providing partial raw material self-sufficiency.

ArcelorMittal produced 55.6 million metric tonnes of crude steel and 48.8 million tonnes of iron ore in 2025. The company operates through multiple segments including NAFTA (North America), Europe, Brazil, ACIS (Africa and CIS), and Mining. ArcelorMittal is a key supplier to the automotive, construction, appliance, packaging, and energy industries globally.

The company has been executing a major strategic growth plan centered on high-return capital projects and targeted M&A. Key initiatives include the full acquisition of the Calvert, Alabama steel facility from Nippon Steel, a $1.2 billion NOES (non-grain-oriented electrical steel) facility investment at Calvert, and expansion projects in Brazil and Liberia. These growth projects are expected to increase future EBITDA potential by $2.1 billion.

- **Growth Projects:** Serra Azul (Brazil iron ore), Barra Mansa (Brazil steel), Liberia expansion

- **Capital Returns:** Active share buyback program alongside growth investments
- **NOES for EV/Renewables:** 150,000 Mt annual capacity targeting automotive, mobility, and renewable energy markets

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

- **Section 232 Beneficiary (US operations):** Calvert facility benefits from 50% steel tariffs protecting domestic production
- **European Exposure:** EU operations face competition from cheap imports; EU has safeguard measures in place
- **Canadian Retaliation:** Canada imposing 25% retaliatory tariffs on US steel and aluminum exports
- **Global Trade Complexity:** Operations in 60+ countries create complex multi-jurisdictional tariff exposure
- **Raw Materials:** Iron ore and coal trade subject to various export taxes and trade restrictions
- **Net Assessment:** US operations are net beneficiaries of Section 232; international operations face mixed tariff environment
