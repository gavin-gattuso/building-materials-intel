# Saint-Gobain (SGO.PA)

## Company Overview

Compagnie de Saint-Gobain S.A. is a French multinational corporation founded in 1665, making it one of the oldest companies in the world. Headquartered in Courbevoie, France, Saint-Gobain is the world's largest building materials company by revenue, operating across more than 75 countries with over 160,000 employees. The company designs, manufactures, and distributes materials and solutions for construction, mobility, and industrial applications.

Saint-Gobain's product portfolio spans flat glass, insulation, gypsum, mortars, construction chemicals, pipes, and high-performance materials. The company operates through multiple segments including Europe, Americas, and Asia-Pacific, with construction products (insulation, gypsum, mortars, construction chemicals) representing the largest share of revenue. Saint-Gobain has positioned itself as a solutions provider for sustainable construction, targeting energy efficiency, decarbonization, and light & sustainable construction.

Under its new strategic plan "Lead & Grow" (2026-2030), Saint-Gobain has announced approximately EUR 12 billion in growth investments and acquisitions, with strict return criteria. The company targets construction chemicals sales growth to EUR 9 billion by 2030 from EUR 6.5 billion in 2024, reflecting its strategic pivot toward higher-margin specialty products.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **"Lead & Grow" 2026-2030 Plan:** Approximately EUR 14 billion total in investments and acquisitions over five years
- **Construction Chemicals Focus:** Targeting EUR 9 billion in construction chemicals sales by 2030
- **Geographic Expansion:** Capitalizing on urbanization in Asia (India, Southeast Asia) and strong long-term demand in North America
- **Sustainability Leadership:** Positioning as key solutions provider for energy-efficient and decarbonized construction
- **Portfolio Rotation:** Active optimization with over 20% of sales rotating through acquisitions and divestments by 2030
- **Operational Excellence:** Continuous margin improvement through manufacturing efficiency and procurement optimization

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

- **North American operations:** Exposed to tariff impacts on construction materials; market weakness in first half of 2025
- **European operations:** Subject to EU regulations and CBAM; energy cost pressures from natural gas prices
- **Cross-border trade:** Global supply chains for specialty products subject to various tariff regimes
- **Mitigation:** Highly localized manufacturing footprint in over 75 countries provides natural hedge against trade barriers
- **Currency exposure:** Multi-currency revenue base (EUR, USD, GBP, INR, BRL) creates translation risk
- **Extreme weather:** Noted impact of extreme weather in Europe and North America since start of 2026 affecting operations
