# QXO Inc. (QXO)

## Company Overview
QXO is a building products distribution platform founded and led by serial acquirer Brad Jacobs, who previously built XPO Logistics into a $12B freight and logistics company through aggressive acquisition-led growth. QXO was established with a singular mission: to consolidate the highly fragmented $800B+ building products distribution industry into a technology-enabled, operationally efficient platform targeting $50B in annual revenue.

The company's acquisition strategy began in earnest with the $11B purchase of Beacon Roofing Supply in April 2025, instantly establishing QXO as one of the largest building products distributors in North America. This was followed by the $2.25B acquisition of Kodiak Building Partners, completed April 1, 2026, which broadened QXO's product offering beyond roofing into lumber, windows, doors, siding, and interior products.

QXO represents one of the most ambitious roll-up strategies in the building materials sector. With an estimated current revenue run-rate of $12-13B and over $3B in committed capital from institutional investors, the company has significant dry powder for additional acquisitions. The addressable market exceeds $200B, suggesting QXO is still in early innings of its consolidation thesis.

**Failed Bid:**
- In 2025, QXO made an unsolicited bid valued at approximately $5B for GMS Inc. (wallboard and ceiling products distributor). GMS rejected the offer and was subsequently acquired by SRS Distribution (a Home Depot subsidiary).

### Kodiak Deal Structure
- Purchase price: $2.0B cash + 13.16M shares of QXO stock
- Seller: Court Square Capital Partners
- QXO retains the right to repurchase shares at $40/share
- Broadens QXO's product portfolio beyond roofing into lumber, windows, doors, and construction supplies

### Financing
- Apollo Global Management-led investor group committed $1.2B in convertible preferred equity (later upsized)
- Total Series C convertible perpetual preferred stock commitment: $3.0B (including Temasek and other investors)
- This represents a $1.8B increase to the previously announced $1.2B financing

## Strategic Initiatives

- **Technology-Enabled Distribution:** Applying Jacobs' playbook from XPO by investing in digital ordering, route optimization, inventory management, and customer analytics platforms.
- **Procurement Leverage:** Consolidating purchasing across acquired platforms to negotiate better terms with manufacturers.
- **Operational Efficiency:** Streamlining overlapping branch networks, optimizing delivery logistics, and standardizing back-office functions.
- **Continued Acquisitions:** Pipeline remains active with $50B revenue target requiring significant additional M&A. Potential targets include regional distributors, specialty building products companies, and adjacent product categories.
- **Vertical Integration:** Potential expansion into value-added services (installation, fabrication) beyond pure distribution.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

QXO has significant exposure to trade policy given its reliance on distributed building materials, many of which are imported or use imported components:
- Canadian softwood lumber duties affect lumber pricing
- Tariffs on Chinese-manufactured building products (hardware, fasteners, tools)
- Steel and aluminum tariffs impact structural products
- Potential tariffs on European building products could affect certain specialty categories

As a distributor, QXO can partially pass through tariff-driven cost increases to customers, though margin compression may occur during transition periods.

## 2026 Outlook & Guidance

QXO's near-term outlook centers on integration execution and continued deal-making:
- Kodiak integration is the immediate priority (completed April 1, 2026)
- Additional acquisitions expected as the company pursues its $50B revenue target
- Organic growth tied to U.S. residential and commercial construction activity
- The $3.0B in committed preferred equity provides substantial M&A firepower

Key risks include integration execution, leverage levels, housing market weakness, and competition for acquisition targets from other consolidators and private equity.
