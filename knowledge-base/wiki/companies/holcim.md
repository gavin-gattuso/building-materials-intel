# Holcim (HOLN.SW)

## Company Overview

Holcim Ltd is a Swiss multinational building materials company headquartered in Zug, Switzerland, and listed on the SIX Swiss Exchange. Following the landmark June 2025 spin-off of its North American operations as Amrize (NYSE: AMRZ), Holcim has been reconstituted as a focused international building materials company with leading positions in Europe, Latin America, Australia, and North Africa.

Pre-spin-off, Holcim was the world's largest cement producer. The post-spin-off Holcim retains significant scale with FY 2025 net sales of CHF 15.7 billion and operates cement, ready-mixed concrete, and aggregates businesses across approximately 60 countries (excluding North America). The company maintains a strong presence in Switzerland, France, Germany, the UK, and has recently made a major push into Latin America.

Under its "NextGen Growth 2030" strategy unveiled at its March 2025 Investor Day, Holcim aims to leverage its sustainability leadership to deliver profitable growth in its core European and Australian markets while accelerating expansion in Latin America to benefit from strong market fundamentals and industrialization trends.

## Recent Earnings

**FY 2025 Results (reported February 2026):**

Holcim delivered excellent full-year 2025 results, with recurring EBIT growth of 10.3% in local currency, exceeding the top end of guidance. The recurring EBIT margin of 18.3% was described as industry-leading. Net sales grew 3.0% in local currency, and free cash flow rose to CHF 2,154 million. The results were achieved in the first full year following the Amrize spin-off, demonstrating the standalone business is performing strongly.

**Q3 2025 Results:**

Strong profitable growth continued in Q3, with the company on track to exceed guidance ranges across all key metrics.

## M&A Activity (2025-2026)

**Cementos Pacasmayo (Peru, ~$1.5B, completed March 31, 2026):**

Holcim acquired a majority stake in Cementos Pacasmayo, Peru's second-largest cement company, for approximately $1.5 billion on a 100% enterprise value basis. Pacasmayo has three cement plants with combined capacity of approximately 5 million tons per year, plus 28 ready-mix and precast concrete plants. The company reported net sales of $630 million in 2025. The acquisition implies an 8.8x EBITDA multiple, or 7.1x after expected run-rate synergies of approximately $40 million (realized by year three). This is the cornerstone of Holcim's Latin America acceleration strategy.

**Amrize Spin-off (completed June 23, 2025):**

Holcim completed the 100% spin-off of its North American operations as Amrize Ltd. through a dividend-in-kind distribution of one Amrize share for every outstanding Holcim share. Amrize shares trade on the NYSE and SIX under ticker "AMRZ." Amrize generated $11.7 billion in revenue in 2024 and operates more than 1,000 sites with 19,000 employees across every U.S. state and Canadian province.

**Bolt-on Acquisitions:**

Year-to-date through early 2026, Holcim completed 15 acquisitions across its international footprint, continuing its disciplined bolt-on strategy.

## Strategic Initiatives

- **NextGen Growth 2030 Strategy (announced March 28, 2025):** Leveraging sustainability leadership for profitable growth in Europe, Australia, and North Africa, while accelerating in Latin America. Targets organic net sales growth of 3-5% and recurring EBIT growth of 8-10% annually.
- **Latin America expansion:** Cementos Pacasmayo acquisition is the flagship deal. Latin America offers strong urbanization-driven demand, industrialization trends, and relatively lower competitive intensity.
- **Sustainability leadership:** Holcim has been at the forefront of low-carbon cement and concrete solutions, with a portfolio of ECOPact green concrete and other sustainable products.
- **Post-spin-off capital allocation:** With Amrize spun off, Holcim has a cleaner balance sheet and more focused capital allocation toward international growth and shareholder returns.

## Tariff & Trade Exposure

Post-spin-off, Holcim's tariff exposure is primarily related to its European and Latin American operations:

- **No direct U.S. tariff exposure:** Following the Amrize spin-off, Holcim has no significant U.S. operations and is therefore not directly impacted by U.S. tariffs on building materials imports.
- **EU CBAM impact:** The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (effective 2026) could benefit Holcim's European operations by raising the cost of cheaper cement imports from non-EU countries (Turkey, North Africa, Asia).
- **Latin America trade:** Cementos Pacasmayo operates primarily in the Peruvian domestic market, with limited cross-border trade exposure.
- **Currency risk:** As a Swiss-headquartered company reporting in CHF, Holcim faces significant currency translation risk from EUR, GBP, and Latin American currencies.
- **Energy costs:** European energy prices (natural gas, electricity) remain a more significant cost driver than tariffs.
