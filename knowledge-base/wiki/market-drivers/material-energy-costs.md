# Material & Energy Costs

## Current Levels (April 2026)
- Brent crude: $112.42/barrel (~$34 higher than 1yr ago)
- Natural gas: +10.9% MoM (February)
- Construction input prices: +12.6% annualized (Jan-Feb 2026)

## Material-Specific Pricing
- Steel bars/plates/structural: +12.1% YoY
- Steel pipe/tube: +9.4% YoY
- Aluminum: ~40% increase in US following tariffs
- Cement (US): $96/MT (March 2026)
- Ready-mix concrete: $125-200/cubic yard
- Lumber: $400-500/MBF (pre-pandemic range)

## Energy Forecast
- EIA: Brent to remain above $95/b next 2 months
- EIA: Brent to fall below $80/b in Q3 2026

## Impact on Building Materials
Energy costs directly affect manufacturing (kilns, smelters) and transport. Current oil spike adds $5-10/ton to delivered material costs. Combined with tariffs, creates dual cost pressure.
