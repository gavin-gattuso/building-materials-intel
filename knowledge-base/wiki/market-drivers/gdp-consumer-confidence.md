# GDP Growth & Consumer Confidence

## Consumer Confidence (March 2026)
- Overall CCI: 91.8 (up 0.8 from Feb)
- Present Situation: 123.3 (up 4.6)
- Expectations: 70.9 (down 1.7)
- Mixed signal: current conditions improving, future outlook weakening

## GDP Forecasts for 2026
- RSM: 2.2% growth
- Goldman Sachs: 2.8% growth (tariff fade + tax cuts via "One Big Beautiful Bill Act")

## Recession Probability
- Current: 30% (down from 40%)
- Trending favorably but still elevated

## Consumer Spending
- Focused on "cheap thrills" and necessities
- Moving away from expensive discretionary purchases
- Inflation expectations rising due to tariff passthrough and oil prices

## Impact on Building Materials
GDP growth supports overall construction activity. Consumer confidence affects residential remodeling spend directly. The mixed signal (better present, weaker expectations) suggests near-term stability but caution on discretionary renovation projects.
