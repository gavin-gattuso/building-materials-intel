# Labor Dynamics

## Current State (Q1 2026)
- New workers needed: 349,000-500,000
- Wage growth: +4% YoY (some firms +20% to compete)
- Recommended budget: 4.0% salary increases
- Workforce age: 1 in 5 workers is 55+

## Key Challenge: Retention Crisis
- 71.7% of contractors increased headcount in 2025
- 46% reported zero net growth due to turnover
- The shortage is a retention problem as much as a hiring problem

## Immigration Impact
- 28% of firms affected by immigration enforcement in past 6 months
- Tighter immigration policy adding upward wage pressure

## Economic Cost
- $10.8B/year cost to home building sector
- 19,000 homes/year not built due to labor shortages

## Impact on Building Materials
Labor shortages directly constrain construction activity, limiting material demand. However, they also accelerate adoption of labor-saving products (prefab, modular, 3D printing) and technology (AI scheduling, robotics).
