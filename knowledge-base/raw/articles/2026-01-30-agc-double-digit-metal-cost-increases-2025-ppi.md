# Double-Digit Increases in Aluminum, Steel and Copper Costs Drive Up Producer Price Indexes for Construction Materials in 2025

## January 2026 PPI Data (Released January 30, 2026)

- Nonresidential construction input prices rose at a **7.1% annualized rate** in January 2026 -- described as "blistering"
- PPI for materials and services used in nonresidential construction rose **2.9%** from January 2025 to January 2026

### Metal Price Increases (January 2025 to January 2026)

- **Aluminum mill shapes**: up **33.0%** YoY -- largest increase since supply-chain disruptions of early 2022
- **Steel mill products**: up **20.7%** YoY -- largest increase since supply-chain disruptions of early 2022
- **Copper wire and cable**: up approximately 80% YoY (per related Construction Dive reporting)
- **Switchgear**: up 67% YoY
- **Iron and steel**: up 58% YoY

### Other Material Categories

- Framing lumber composite: approximately $420/MBF (some sources report ~$872/MBF national average depending on grade)
- Softwood lumber: down 8.0% YoY as of November data
- Ready-mix concrete: down 0.7% YoY
- Steel rebar (#4): approximately $980/ton
- Concrete (3000 PSI): approximately $145/cubic yard

## Analysis

The January 2026 PPI data confirms that tariff-affected metals are driving the bulk of construction cost inflation. Non-tariffed materials like lumber and concrete remain relatively stable or declining, creating a bifurcated cost environment. The 7.1% annualized rate for nonresidential inputs signals intensifying cost pressure that could impact project feasibility and contractor margins throughout 2026.
