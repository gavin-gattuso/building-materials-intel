# PwC: Global M&A Trends in Industrials and Services -- 2026 Outlook

## Key Findings

- **2026 expected to be a growth year** for global engineering and construction M&A
- U.S.-targeted M&A surged **66% in H2 2025** as firms adjusted to the administration's reinforcement of Build America, Buy America provisions
- Construction and building materials subsectors **led the rebound** as companies sought to derisk supply chains, expand domestic capacity, and offset tariff impacts

## Strategic Drivers

### Tariff Management
- Firms pursuing **onshoring and nearshoring strategies** to strengthen supply-chain continuity and reduce tariff exposure
- Especially critical for critical materials and manufacturing inputs

### Labor and Cost Pressures
- Rising material costs and shortages of skilled labor continue to **compress margins** across the construction value chain
- M&A emerging as both a defensive and strategic tool, offering **scale advantages in procurement**, broader access to labor pools, and opportunities to adopt labor-saving approaches

### Technology-Enabled Solutions
- M&A momentum building around companies offering **labor-efficient, sustainable, and technology-enabled solutions**
- Companies offering **modular or prefabricated systems**, advanced materials, and AI-enabled design and project management tools attracting increased buyer interest

## End Market Themes

- End markets with steady demand -- **data centers, advanced manufacturing, and infrastructure modernization** -- remain particularly attractive
- Fragmentation across engineering, specialty contracting, and distribution creating opportunities to build multi-regional platforms
- Buyers targeting segments where **regional leaders can be integrated into larger platforms** with more predictable revenue and stronger operating leverage
