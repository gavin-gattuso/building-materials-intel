# The 2026 Construction, Cement and Concrete Outlook

## Key Points
- Primary 2026 risk to construction activity centers on inflation; without improvement in inflation and interest rates, no improvement expected in private construction sectors
- U.S. cement market has been in decline for three consecutive years, with more than a 10 million metric ton decline since 2022
- High interest rates expected to continue restraining private construction activity
- Inflation will continue to erode purchasing power of public funding
- Data center construction expected to post another year of strength but accounts for less than 2% of total cement consumption
- Data centers will not be a strong national driver of cement consumption growth
- Aggregates production estimated at 2.33 billion metric tons in 2025, down from 2.35 Gt in 2024
- Public infrastructure spending provides floor for demand but cannot offset private sector weakness alone
