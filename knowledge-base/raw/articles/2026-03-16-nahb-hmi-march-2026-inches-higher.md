# NAHB HMI March 2026: Builder Confidence Inches Up to 38

## Key Details
- NAHB/Wells Fargo Housing Market Index: **38** in March
- Up **2 points** from February (36)
- Above forecasts of **37**
- Still well below the **50** breakeven threshold
- Modest improvement ahead of spring selling season

## Context
- The March improvement came before the full impact of the Iran conflict on mortgage rates
- Mortgage rates were still near 6.0-6.1% during the March survey period
- The subsequent surge to 6.46% by early April may reverse this improvement in the April reading
- Builder use of price cuts and incentives remained elevated

## Analysis
- The 2-point increase is a modest positive but does not represent a meaningful shift in builder sentiment
- At 38, the HMI still indicates significantly more builders view conditions as poor than good
- The spring selling season typically brings seasonal improvement, but this year the seasonal boost is being offset by rate volatility and tariff uncertainty
- For building materials: the March HMI uptick is too small to signal a demand inflection; builders remain cautious
- The April HMI (released in mid-April) will be critical to assess whether the Iran-driven mortgage rate spike has reversed the tentative improvement
