# KPMG: January 2026 Construction Spending Weakens as Uncertainty Builds

## Key Findings

- January construction spending **fell 0.3%**, following an upward revision to December
- Year-over-year, construction spending rose a meager **1% before adjusting for inflation**
- Construction costs excluding labor rose **2.8% YoY**, putting real spending in the red on a year-over-year basis

## Residential Construction

- Private residential construction spending **slumped 0.8%** in January on lower single- and multifamily construction
- Record-breaking cold weather across many parts of the country weighed on construction activity
- Mortgage rates were trending around **6.1% in January** and fell to a 3.5-year low in February, which likely boosted residential construction

## Nonresidential Construction

- Private nonresidential construction **slipped 0.4%** for the month
- Only lodging, office, healthcare and communication infrastructure posted increases
- **Data center construction** (captured under office category) continued its record streak, adding **2.3%**

## Public Construction

- Public construction spending **added 0.6%** in January and **4.5% more than a year ago**

## Headwinds and Uncertainty

- Fuel costs, persistent tariffs and trade policy uncertainty are squeezing margins with little relief in sight
- Further tariff actions are likely; a **USMCA renegotiation later in 2026** adds another layer of uncertainty
- Construction materials prices surged **1.3% higher in February** and have risen at a **12.6% annualized rate** through the first two months of 2026
- Construction employment declined by **11,000 positions** in February
- The spring homebuilding season faces rising mortgage rates and elevated geopolitical uncertainty
