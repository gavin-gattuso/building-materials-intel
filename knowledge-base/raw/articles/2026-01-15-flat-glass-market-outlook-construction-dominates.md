# Flat Glass Market Outlook: Construction Holds 80% Share; $170B+ Market Growing 8.6% CAGR

## Key Details
- Global flat glass market: ~$169.1 billion in 2026, projected $356.3B by 2035 (CAGR 8.6%)
- Construction segment: 80.1% of market in 2025; growing at 8.7% CAGR (2026-2035)
- Alternative estimate: $340.98B in 2026 to $510.78B by 2035 (CAGR 4.6%)
- Key drivers: energy-efficient glazing (Low-E glass, insulated glass), urbanization, infrastructure development in emerging economies
- Modern architecture pushing adoption of glass facades and windows
- Residential, commercial, and high-rise construction activities fueling growth
- Global construction glass market entering "transformative decade" through 2035
- Regulatory imperatives for energy-efficient buildings strengthening demand
- AGC display glass revenue hit record JPY 270B in Q3 2025 (adjacent market data point)

## Analysis
- The flat glass market's 80%+ construction share makes it a direct proxy for global building activity, which benefits both AGC (Asia/global) and Saint-Gobain (Europe/Americas).
- The push for energy-efficient glazing aligns with both companies' product strategies: AGC's advanced architectural glass and Saint-Gobain's high-performance insulated glass units.
- The wide CAGR range (4.6% to 8.6%) across research firms reflects uncertainty about construction cycle timing, but the directional trend is clearly positive.
- For AGC, the construction glass market provides a more stable demand base than the consumer electronics glass market it is exiting, supporting the portfolio restructuring strategy.
