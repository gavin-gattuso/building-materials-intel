# State of U.S. Tariffs: January 19, 2026

## Headline Figures

- U.S. effective tariff rate: **16.9%** as of January 19, 2026 -- the highest since **1932**
- Would increase to **17.5%** if proposed tariffs on European countries take effect
- A new **10% global tariff** (150 days set to expire in July 2026) affects broad imports

## Construction-Relevant Tariff Rates

| Material | Tariff Rate | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Steel (all imports) | 50% | Section 232 |
| Aluminum (all imports) | 50% | Section 232 |
| Copper (semi-finished) | 50% | Various derivatives |
| Softwood lumber/timber | 10% | Section 232, effective Oct 14, 2025 |
| Kitchen cabinets & vanities | 50% | Increased from 25% on Jan 1, 2026 |
| Upholstered furniture | 30% | Increased from 25% on Jan 1, 2026 |
| Gypsum, drywall | Subject to 10% global tariff | |
| Lumber derivatives | 25% | Various |

## Impact Projections

- Aggregate construction costs estimated to rise roughly **8%** under current tariff conditions
- Longer-term tariff impacts expected to range from **5% to 25%** depending on material type
- Residential construction contracts by approximately **4.3%** in response to tariff-driven cost increases
- About **75%** of U.S. softwood timber and lumber imports came from Canada (~$7 billion total in 2024)

## NAHB Analysis

The Trump administration is imposing new or elevated tariffs on lumber, gypsum, steel, kitchen cabinets, bathroom vanities, and other products needed to construct new homes, raising costs across the board for new construction, renovations, and affordable housing development. NAHB estimated these tariffs add thousands of dollars to the cost of a new home.
