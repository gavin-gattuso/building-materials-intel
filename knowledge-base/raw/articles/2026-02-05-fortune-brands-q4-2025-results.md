# Fortune Brands Reports Q4 2025 Results: EPS Down 25%, Sets Conservative 2026 Guidance

## Key Details
- Q4 2025 EPS of $0.63, down 25% YoY; adjusted EPS of $0.86, down 12%
- Q4 2025 sales of $1.1 billion, down 2% (flat excluding China)
- Full-year 2025 sales of $4.5 billion, down 3% (down 1% excluding China)
- Full-year 2025 EPS of $2.47, down 34%; adjusted EPS of $3.61, down 12%
- 2026 guidance: net sales flat to +2%, operating margin 14.5-15.5%, EPS $3.35-$3.65
- Tariff-related impacts estimated at $151 million in 2026 (up $100M+ YoY)
- Free cash flow guidance: $400-$450 million
- Capex guidance: $110-$140 million
- Moen Flo Smart Water Monitor saw double-digit sales growth
- Yale Smart Lock with Matter standard saw strong reception

## Analysis
- Fortune Brands' 34% EPS decline in 2025 reflects the challenging plumbing and outdoor living environment. The $151M tariff headwind for 2026 is substantial relative to the company's profit base. The conservative guidance suggests management is not expecting a meaningful market recovery. However, the smart home products (Moen Flo, Yale locks) are bright spots that position the company for longer-term growth in connected building products.
