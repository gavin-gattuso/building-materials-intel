# Morgan Stanley Upgrades CEMEX to Overweight; Barclays Reiterates $15 Target

## Key Details
- **Morgan Stanley** (Mar 22, 2026): Upgraded CEMEX (CX) from Equal-Weight to Overweight, maintained $14 price target
- **Barclays** (Mar 12, 2026): Reiterated Overweight rating and $15.00 price target (implying 34% upside)
- **Itau BBA**: Upgraded to Outperform from Market Perform
- **Goldman Sachs**: Previously raised price target to $13
- **Scotiabank** (Oct 2025): Downgraded with $11.1 price target (the most recent bearish call)
- **Current stock price**: ~$12.58

## Analysis
- The Morgan Stanley upgrade is significant as it reflects growing institutional confidence in CEMEX's transformation story
- Three major banks now have Overweight/Outperform ratings, creating a strong bullish consensus
- The key investment thesis centers on Project Cutting Edge cost savings, U.S. aggregates expansion, and improving shareholder returns
- The gap between Barclays' $15 target and the current ~$12.58 price suggests meaningful upside if the Sprint strategy delivers
- CEMEX's stock has been re-rated from a distressed LatAm cement play to a U.S.-focused growth story, attracting a different investor base
