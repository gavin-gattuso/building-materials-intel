# January 2026 Housing Starts Surge 7.2% to 1.487M SAAR

## Key Details
- Housing starts: **1,487,000 SAAR** (seasonally adjusted annual rate) in January
- Month-over-month: **+7.2%** (from revised December 1,387,000)
- Year-over-year: **+9.5%** (from January 2025 rate of 1,358,000)
- Single-family authorizations: **873,000** rate
- Building permits: **1,376,000 SAAR** in January
- Permits month-over-month: **-5.4%** (from revised December 1,455,000)
- Permits year-over-year: **-5.8%** (from January 2025 rate of 1,460,000)

## Key Divergence: Starts Up, Permits Down
- Housing starts jumped 7.2% while building permits fell 5.4%
- This divergence suggests builders are working through existing permit backlogs rather than pulling new permits
- The permit decline signals potential future weakness in starts

## Data Availability Note
- **February and March 2026 data releases were delayed**
- Originally scheduled for March 17 and April 17 respectively
- Both rescheduled for release on **April 29, 2026**
- The delay limits real-time visibility into housing market trends

## Analysis
- The strong January starts number was a bright spot, but the simultaneous decline in permits is a cautionary signal
- The 7.2% monthly jump may reflect weather-related timing effects and builders front-running anticipated tariff cost increases
- The 5.8% year-over-year decline in permits is concerning for building materials demand outlook in mid-to-late 2026
- For building materials companies: the starts-permits divergence means near-term material demand may hold up but the pipeline is weakening
- The Census data release delays add uncertainty at a critical time when the industry is navigating tariffs, rate volatility, and geopolitical shocks
