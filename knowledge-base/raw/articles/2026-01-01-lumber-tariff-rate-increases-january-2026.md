# Lumber and Wood Product Tariff Rate Increases Take Effect January 1, 2026

## January 1, 2026 Rate Changes

| Product Category | Previous Rate | New Rate (Jan 1, 2026) |
|------------------|---------------|----------------------|
| Upholstered furniture | 25% | **30%** |
| Kitchen cabinets & vanities | 25% | **50%** |
| Softwood timber & lumber | 10% | 10% (unchanged) |
| Other lumber derivatives | 25% | 25% (unchanged) |

## Background

- President Trump imposed **Section 232** tariffs on timber, lumber, and derivative products
- Original tariffs effective **October 14, 2025**
- Based on Secretary of Commerce Section 232 investigation finding wood imports threatened national security
- About **75% of U.S. softwood timber and lumber imports** came from Canada (~$7 billion total in 2024)

## Industry Impact

- NAHB warned these tariffs add **thousands of dollars** to the cost of a new home
- Tariffs affecting: lumber, gypsum, steel, kitchen cabinets, bathroom vanities, and related products
- Impacts new construction, renovations, and affordable housing development
- Softwood lumber prices remained down 8% YoY despite tariffs (demand weakness offsetting tariff pressure)

## NAHB Response

NAHB has been vocal in opposing the tariffs, arguing they undermine housing affordability at a time when the U.S. already faces a 3.7-million-unit housing shortage. The association urged Congress and the administration to consider exemptions for construction materials essential to residential building.
