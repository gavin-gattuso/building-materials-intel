# FMI Building Products M&A and Sector Update: February 2026

## Deal Volume Trends
- Total 2025 deal activity declined approximately 30% year-over-year
- Q4 2025 transaction volume increased vs. earlier quarters (signs of stabilization)
- Strategic buyers represented 63% of total transactions in 2025
- 89% including sponsor-backed add-ons
- Private equity remained disciplined, focused on targeted opportunities

## 2026 Outlook
- Building products M&A enters 2026 with renewed momentum
- Improved activity in late 2025 setting stage for recovery
- Center of gravity shifting from scale toward scope and capability plays
- Deal flow upheld by:
  - Booming data center construction
  - Pent-up demand for affordable housing
  - Aging housing stock driving repair/remodel activity

## Market Conditions by Segment
- Single-family construction: steadying amid affordability pressures and rising inventory
- Repair and remodel: continuing to benefit from aging housing stock
- Nonresidential: data center construction notable growth area
- Residential: mixed signals with weak builder sentiment but stable completions

## Notable February 2026 Transactions
- QXO acquires Kodiak Building Partners for $2.25 billion (announced Feb 11)
- SRS Distribution acquired LS Building Products and Sider Lumber & Supply
- IBP completed three acquisitions with combined annual sales over $22 million
- Multiple bolt-on acquisitions across insulation, roofing, and distribution

## Valuation Environment
- Multiples compressing slightly from peak 2021-2022 levels
- Strategic buyers paying premium for capability-enhancing acquisitions
- Private equity dry powder remains elevated but deployment remains selective
