# Taiheiyo Cement's Luzon Terminal Begins Operations Early 2026; 700,000 TPA Capacity

## Key Details
- **Location**: Luzon, Philippines
- **Operational start**: Early 2026
- **Capacity**: Up to 700,000 tonnes per annum of cement
- **Purpose**: Supply cement to the Luzon market, the most economically active region in the Philippines
- **Part of broader strategy**: Expanding cement supply capabilities across Asia-Pacific markets
- **Saiki Ash Center**: JPY 7.6 billion investment in Japan to supply multiple countries

## Analysis
- The Philippines represents one of the fastest-growing cement markets in Southeast Asia, driven by infrastructure spending and urbanization
- Luzon (including Metro Manila) accounts for the majority of Philippine cement consumption
- The 700,000 tpa capacity adds meaningful volume to Taiheiyo's international portfolio
- This aligns with Taiheiyo's medium-term plan to offset declining Japanese domestic demand with Asian growth markets
- The terminal model (importing clinker/cement rather than operating a kiln) is capital-light compared to building a new cement plant
- Competition in the Philippine market includes CEMEX (Philippines operations) and local producers
