# Mortgage Rates Rise to 6.46%: Fifth Consecutive Weekly Increase

## Key Details
- 30-year fixed-rate mortgage: **6.46%** as of April 2, 2026
- Previous week: **6.38%**
- **Fifth consecutive week** of increases
- Rates now **half a percentage point higher** than one month ago
- Year-over-year comparison: still below **6.64%** from April 2025

## 2026 Mortgage Rate Timeline
- Dec 31, 2025: **6.15%**
- Jan 8, 2026: **6.16%**
- Jan 15, 2026: **6.06%** (2026 low)
- Feb: rates dipped briefly below **6.00%** (~5.98%)
- Mar 5: **6.00%**
- Mar 19: **6.22%** (up from 6.11%)
- Apr 2: **6.46%** (2026 high)

## Drivers of Recent Increase
- **Iran conflict** driving oil prices higher, creating inflationary expectations
- Brent crude above **$94/barrel**, up ~50% from start of year
- **10-year Treasury yield** rising on inflation fears
- Fed maintaining rates at 3.50-3.75% with no near-term cut expected
- Tariff-driven inflation keeping long-term rate expectations elevated

## Analysis
- The 40+ basis point increase from the February low to April represents a significant affordability shock during the critical spring selling season
- At 6.46%, the monthly payment on a median-priced home ($420,000 with 20% down) is approximately **$2,100** -- up ~$100/month from February lows
- NAHB data shows **29% of builders cutting prices** and **61% offering incentives** to compensate
- The rate increase comes at the worst possible time for builders entering the spring selling season
- For building materials: rising mortgage rates directly suppress new home sales, housing starts, and demand for residential building products
