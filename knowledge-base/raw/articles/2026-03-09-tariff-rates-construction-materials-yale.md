# State of U.S. Tariffs as of March 9, 2026: Comprehensive Impact on Construction Materials

## Overall Tariff Environment

- Estimated average tariff rate on all imports: **12%** (Urban-Brookings Tax Policy Center)
- After Section 122 tariffs: rate rose to **10.5%**, the highest since 1943
- A 10% time-limited tariff applies globally for 150 days, set to expire in July 2026

## Construction-Specific Tariff Rates

| Material | Tariff Rate |
|----------|-------------|
| Steel | Up to **50%** (Section 232) |
| Aluminum | Up to **50%** (Section 232) |
| Copper products | **50%** |
| Softwood lumber & timber | **10%** |
| Lumber derivatives | **25%** |
| Mexican & Canadian cement | **25%** |

## Economic Impact on Construction

- Construction output contracts by **1.7%** under current tariff regime
- US manufacturing output expands by **0.7%** (intended domestic substitution effect)
- Aggregate construction costs estimated to rise roughly **8%** under current policy conditions
- Longer-term tariff impacts expected to range from **5% to 25%** depending on material type
- Overall project expenses increased **4-6%** for U.S. construction firms

## AGC Industry Response

- AGC urging contract updates to include tariff escalation clauses
- Many imported construction materials serve as raw inputs for domestically manufactured products, amplifying tariff impacts through the supply chain
- Industry calling for tariff exclusion processes for materials without adequate domestic supply

## NAHB Position

- NAHB estimates tariffs add approximately **$10,900** to the cost of a typical new home
- Building materials trade policy remains a top NAHB advocacy priority
