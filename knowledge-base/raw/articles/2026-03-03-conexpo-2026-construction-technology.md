# CONEXPO-CON/AGG 2026: Construction Industry's Largest Trade Show Draws 140,000+ Attendees

## Event Overview

- **Dates**: March 3-7, 2026
- **Location**: Las Vegas Convention Center
- **Attendance**: More than **140,000 people** from **128 countries**
- **Exhibit Space**: Over **3 million square feet**
- **Exhibitors**: More than **2,000 exhibitors**

## Top Technology Themes

### Electrification & Alternative Power
- Electric and hydrogen-powered machinery prominently featured
- Major OEMs showcasing battery-electric excavators, loaders, and compact equipment
- Hydrogen fuel cell prototypes from multiple manufacturers

### AI & Smart Equipment
- AI-powered construction equipment with smart controls and predictive maintenance
- Digital Project Delivery (DPD) progressing from innovation strategy to contractual standard
- Common Data Environment (CDE) requirements becoming standard

### Robotics & Automation
- Construction robotics market valued at **$1.30 billion** in 2026
- Projected to reach **$11.14 billion by 2040**
- Automates bricklaying, demolition, material handling, excavation, and concrete placement
- Gravis Robotics' Gravis Rack won Contractors' Choice for best technology

### Prefabrication
- More projects shifting labor into factory settings
- Components manufactured in parallel with site preparation to shorten timelines
- Addresses labor shortage by moving work to controlled environments

## Contractors' Choice Awards

- **Best Equipment**: Husco's GenSteer
- **Best Technology**: Gravis Robotics' Gravis Rack

## 3D Printing in Construction

- 3D-printed concrete projects built up to **50% faster** vs traditional methods
- Technology reduces labor requirements by **50-70%** on suitable scopes
- Material waste reduced by up to **40%**

## Major Exhibitors

Caterpillar, CASE Construction Equipment, John Deere, Hitachi, Hyundai, SANY, Kobelco, Komatsu, Liebherr, Volvo CE

## Sustainable Materials Innovation

- Biochar emerging as bio-based material that actively sequesters carbon
- Produced by transforming organic waste into charcoal-like material through pyrolysis
- WPI researchers developed new building material using enzyme that turns CO2 into solid minerals
