# ABI January 2026: Architecture Billings Decline to 43.8, Remain Soft

## Key Details
- AIA/Deltek Architecture Billings Index: **43.8** in January (down from 47.1 in December 2025)
- Any reading below 50 indicates declining demand for architects' services
- Inquiries into new projects declined for the **first time since April 2025**
- Value of newly signed design contracts also softened
- This marks **contraction for 37 of the last 40 months**

## Sector Performance
- All specializations remained in contraction territory
- Multifamily residential: slower rate of decline but no billings growth since **mid-2022**
- Commercial/industrial: continued weakness
- Institutional: mixed signals

## Regional Breakdown
- All regions showed declining billings
- The breadth of contraction across both sectors and regions is concerning

## Analysis
- The ABI is a leading indicator for nonresidential construction with a **9-12 month lead time**
- January's sharp drop to 43.8 (from 47.1 in December) signals potential further weakness in commercial construction spending through late 2026 and into 2027
- The decline in new project inquiries is a particularly concerning development -- it had been one of the few bright spots in recent months
- For building materials companies with commercial/institutional exposure, the ABI suggests demand headwinds ahead
- Data center construction is a notable exception to the broad nonresidential weakness, but it is not large enough to offset the overall trend in billings
