# Atlanta Fed GDPNow: Q1 2026 GDP Estimate Falls to 1.6%

## Key Data Points
- Latest GDPNow estimate (April 2, 2026): **1.6%** annualized real GDP growth for Q1 2026
- Previous estimate: **1.9%**
- The estimate has declined steadily from an initial **3.1%** reading on February 20

## Tracking History (Q1 2026)
- February 20: **3.1%** (initial estimate)
- Early March: **3.2%**, then revised to **2.1%**
- Mid-March: continued decline to **2.0%**
- Late March: **1.9%**
- April 2: **1.6%** (latest)

## Context
- Q4 2025 GDP growth was revised down to **0.7%** (second estimate), signaling significant deceleration
- The GDPNow decline from 3.1% to 1.6% reflects weakening consumer spending data, softer business investment, and trade uncertainty
- The model is purely data-driven with no subjective adjustments
- Q1 2026 advance estimate from BEA expected late April

## Construction Industry Implications
- Decelerating GDP growth signals weakening demand environment for construction activity
- A sub-2% GDP growth rate historically correlates with slower construction starts
- Consumer spending weakness (driving the GDPNow decline) threatens residential renovation and new home demand
- Building materials companies with infrastructure exposure (IIJA-funded) are relatively insulated from GDP softness
- If Q1 comes in near 1.6%, it would be the second consecutive quarter of sub-2% growth, raising recession probability estimates
