# IIJA Infrastructure Spending Reaches Peak Execution Phase in 2026

## Funding Status

- U.S. DOT released IIJA Funding Status report as of **January 31, 2026**
- 2026 is the year of "Shovels in the Ground" -- peak disbursement phase
- **40% YoY increase** in "Heavy Civil" solicitations in 2026 compared to 2024

## Spending Projections

- U.S. transportation construction market expected to grow nearly **3%** to a record **$209.1 billion** in 2026
- Overall U.S. construction market expected to grow **5.6%** annually to reach **$1.27 trillion** in 2026
- Investment in structures expected to pivot from 2025 decline to modest growth of **~1.8%** in 2026

## IIJA Allocation Breakdown

- **Road/Bridge repair**: $110 billion (largest allocation)
- **Water Infrastructure**: $55 billion
- Total new investment: **$550 billion**
- 90% of funding distributed via **state formula grants** (not direct federal contracts)

## Key Challenges

- High construction cost inflation has reduced the **real purchasing power** of IIJA funds
- $550 billion buys less physical infrastructure in 2025-2026 than it would have in 2021
- Labor shortages constrain execution capacity, particularly for specialized trades
- Tariff-driven material cost increases further erode purchasing power

## Complementary Federal Programs

- **CHIPS Act** megaprojects driving data center and semiconductor facility construction
- Bipartisan support for federal infrastructure investment remains strong
- Energy infrastructure and grid modernization adding to construction demand pipeline

## State-Level Activity

- State DOT lettings are the primary channel for IIJA-funded projects
- Contractors should focus on state-level procurement for maximum opportunity
