# Wienerberger FY 2025: Strong Profit Increase; Announces Italcer Acquisition

## Key Details
- **FY 2025 Revenue**: EUR 4.6 billion
- **FY 2025 Operating EBITDA**: EUR 754 million
- **FY 2025 Net Profit**: EUR 168 million (significant increase YoY)
- **FY 2025 Free Cash Flow**: EUR 474 million (strengthened significantly)
- **Net Debt**: Reduced further during 2025
- **Q4 2025 Performance**: Missed analyst expectations (Q4 seasonally weaker)
- **Italcer Acquisition**: Definitive agreement to acquire 50% + 1 share of Italcer Group
- **Italcer Seller**: Mindful Capital Partners, Miura Partners, and co-shareholders
- **Italcer Option**: Option to acquire remaining shares in H1 2027
- **Italcer Profile**: Leading multi-brand specialist in high-end design ceramic surfaces; ~1,200 employees
- **Italcer EBITDA Contribution**: Expected to contribute >EUR 100 million to Group EBITDA midterm; ~EUR 50 million in 2026 (8 months of contribution)
- **Italcer Financing**: Cash-on-hand and bank loans
- **Expected Close**: Q2 2026, subject to competition authority approvals

## Analysis
- Wienerberger's Italcer acquisition significantly expands its renovation solutions business, adding high-end ceramic surface products to its building materials portfolio
- The staged acquisition structure (50%+1 now, option for remainder in 2027) is conservative and limits initial cash outflow
- The >EUR 100 million midterm EBITDA contribution represents meaningful growth for a company generating EUR 754 million in operating EBITDA
- This acquisition pivots Wienerberger further from traditional brick manufacturing toward a diversified building solutions provider
- CEO Heimo Scheuch's mandate was extended through 2029, providing leadership continuity for the transformation strategy
