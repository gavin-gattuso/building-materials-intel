# CRE Lending Recovery Underway But $1.8T Maturity Wall Looms

## Key Details
- **$1.8 trillion** in commercial real estate loans maturing in 2026 across ~7,000 properties
- CRE lending up **35% year-over-year** through late 2025
- Institutional sales activity increased **17%** through October 2025
- Sales volume up more than **40% year-over-year** in Q3 2025
- Colliers forecasts **15-20% increase** in CRE sales volume in 2026
- Bond market spreads: corporate-government bond spread narrowed to approximately **1 percentage point** (below historical average)
- 10-year Treasury expected to remain below **4%** for much of 2026

## Construction Lending Conditions
- Construction lending constrained **not by debt capital availability** but by equity availability and project fundamentals/profitability
- As construction costs come in slightly and asset values increase, more projects are penciling out
- Construction activity expected to increase incrementally in 2026
- Banks "easing back into commercial real estate lending"

## Sector Outlook
- **Data centers:** Strongest sector, massive capital flowing into AI infrastructure
- **Industrial:** Stabilizing after overbuilding cycle
- **Multifamily:** Regional variation; Sunbelt markets cooling while supply-constrained markets improving
- **Office:** Still challenging but pricing reset creating opportunistic buying

## Analysis
- The $1.8 trillion maturity wall is the single biggest CRE risk factor for 2026 -- forced refinancing at higher rates could trigger distressed sales or construction project delays
- The improvement in lending conditions and narrowing bond spreads are positive for new construction project financing
- For building materials: the CRE lending recovery supports nonresidential construction demand, but the maturity wall could create abrupt project cancellations if refinancing becomes difficult
- Data center construction continues to be the standout growth driver, attracting institutional capital at an unprecedented scale
