# KPMG Global Construction Survey 2025/2026: Resilient by Design

## Key Findings

- Survey draws on perspectives of **375 engineering, construction, and real estate leaders** collected through online surveys and in-depth interviews with KPMG experts and industry executives
- **71% of respondents are optimistic** about the industry's direction, up from 66% in 2023
- Robust government stimulus and demand for sustainability-linked and AI-driven projects are fueling optimism, yet the environment remains volatile

## The "Risk Delta" Paradox

- Critical insight: marked decline in executives' risk appetite despite growing opportunities
- An expanding divergence between mounting industry risks and a shrinking appetite for taking them on
- Amidst growing uncertainty, reduced willingness to take on risk is creating strategic tension

## Strategic Priorities (Ranked by Respondents)

1. **Operational efficiency and profitability** -- 75%
2. **Market expansion and client focus** -- 72%
3. **Technology and innovation** -- 61%
4. **Risk and resilience management** -- 53%

## Sector Growth Outlook (Next 12-24 Months)

- **Water and utilities** -- 91% expecting expansion (top growth area)
- **Green power generation** -- 89%
- **Infrastructure projects** -- 89%
- **Transport, warehousing and logistics** -- strong outlook
- **Residential** -- rounding out top six

## Resilience as Strategic Capability

- More than half of organizations expect **collaborative contracting, supply chain digitization, and offsite manufacturing** to become standard within five years
- Resilience is evolving from a reactive safeguard to a strategic capability enabling agility, innovation, and long-term value creation

## Workforce Readiness as Top Barrier

- The sector's biggest barrier is not technology -- it is workforce readiness
- Companies are doubling down on talent, culture, and leadership
- Digital fluency and mechanization skills are now essential for productivity and innovation
