# AGC Inc Announces Major Governance Overhaul and Leadership Changes for 2026

## Key Details
- Shinji Miyaji resigned as Director and Senior Executive Vice President effective March 27, 2026
- Yoshio Takegawa promoted to Executive Vice President CFO (effective Jan 1, 2026), later to become Representative Director
- Takegawa previously served as President of AGC's Automotive Company
- AGC plans transition to a "Company with Audit and Supervisory Committee" structure, subject to shareholder approval in March 2026
- Multiple board and audit & supervisory board departures effective March 27, 2026
- Governance changes announced December 9, 2025, with implementation beginning January 1, 2026
- Company maintains target of ~3% dividend on equity ratio (DOE) and 50%+ total return ratio

## Analysis
- The governance overhaul signals AGC's effort to modernize its corporate structure in line with Japanese corporate governance reforms and shareholder expectations.
- Bringing in a new CFO from the operating side (Automotive Company) suggests a more commercially-focused financial leadership approach.
- The transition to an Audit and Supervisory Committee structure is a significant governance reform that enhances independent oversight and aligns with Tokyo Stock Exchange best practices.
- These changes come at a critical juncture as AGC executes its "AGC plus-2026 Strategy" to drive recovery and growth.
