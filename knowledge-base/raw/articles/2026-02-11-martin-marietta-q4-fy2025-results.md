# Martin Marietta Reports FY2025 Record Results; Sets Strong 2026 Guidance

## Key Details
- **Q4 2025 revenue**: $1.53 billion (up from $1.41 billion)
- **Q4 2025 gross profit**: $468 million (up from $424 million)
- **Q4 2025 net earnings**: $233 million (down 6% YoY)
- **Q4 2025 adjusted EBITDA**: $515 million
- **FY2025 revenue**: $6.15 billion (up from $5.66 billion)
- **FY2025 gross profit**: $1.89 billion (up from $1.64 billion)
- **FY2025 net earnings**: $990 million
- **FY2025 adjusted EBITDA**: $2.07 billion
- **Aggregates shipments**: 198.5 million tons
- **Average selling price**: $23.30 per ton
- **Gross profit per ton**: Up 12% YoY
- **2026 Initial Guidance**: Revenue $6.42-$6.78B; Adjusted EBITDA $2.16-$2.31B
  - Aggregates volume growth: 1-3%
  - Average selling price growth: 4-6%
  - Capital expenditures: $550-$600 million
- Results announced February 11, 2026

## Analysis
- Martin Marietta's FY2025 results confirm a multi-year infrastructure supercycle is underway, with record revenue and EBITDA
- The 12% increase in gross profit per ton demonstrates exceptional pricing power in tight aggregates markets
- The Q4 net earnings decline of 6% was primarily due to higher costs and one-time items, not a demand issue
- The 2026 guidance implies continued mid-single-digit growth, with pricing as the primary driver (4-6%) over volume (1-3%)
- Capital expenditures of $550-$600M reflect investments in capacity to meet growing infrastructure demand
- Martin Marietta's aggregates-focused strategy continues to deliver superior margins vs. vertically integrated cement peers
