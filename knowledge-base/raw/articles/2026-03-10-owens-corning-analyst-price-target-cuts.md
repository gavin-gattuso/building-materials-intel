# Multiple Analysts Cut Owens Corning Price Targets Amid Macro Headwinds

## Key Details
- Evercore ISI lowered price target to $117 from $121, maintaining "In Line" rating; cited macroeconomic concerns
- Earlier Evercore cut was from $151 to $121, citing roofing margin pressure
- RBC Capital reduced price target to $143 from $145
- Citi reduced price target to $135 from $137
- Deutsche Bank cut price target to $134 from $166 (largest absolute cut)
- Overall consensus remains Buy from 12 analysts as of March 2026
- Average one-year price target range: $111-$174 depending on source
- Stock fell ~5.2% after Q4 results and Doors impairment disclosure

## Analysis
- The wave of price target cuts reflects concern about two key issues: (1) the Doors segment underperformance post-Masonite acquisition, and (2) broader macro headwinds affecting residential construction demand.
- Deutsche Bank's steep cut from $166 to $134 (a 19% reduction) is the most aggressive and suggests significant re-rating of the company's near-term earnings power.
- Despite the cuts, the consensus Buy rating signals that most analysts view the current weakness as cyclical rather than structural, with long-term value in OC's dominant market positions.
