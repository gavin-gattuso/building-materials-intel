# New Residential Construction: January 2026

## Housing Starts (Seasonally Adjusted Annual Rate)

- **Total starts**: 1,487,000 (January 2026)
  - **+7.2%** above revised December estimate of 1,387,000
  - **+9.5%** above January 2025 rate of 1,358,000
- **Single-family starts**: 935,000
  - **-2.8%** below revised December figure of 962,000
- **Multi-family starts** (5+ units): 524,000
  - Headline gain driven entirely by multi-family segment

## Building Permits (Seasonally Adjusted Annual Rate)

- **Total permits**: 1,376,000 (January 2026)
  - **-5.4%** below revised December rate of 1,455,000
  - **-5.8%** below January 2025 rate of 1,460,000
- **Single-family permits**: 873,000
  - **-0.9%** below revised December figure of 881,000

## New Home Sales (January 2026 Data)

- Sales at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of **587,000**
  - **-17.6%** below December 2025 rate of 712,000
  - **-11.3%** below January 2025 rate of 662,000
- Median sales price: **$400,500** (-4.5% from December's $419,200)
- Average sales price: **$499,500** (-5.9% from December's $530,900)
- Supply: **9.7 months** at current sales rate (+21.3% above December's 8.0 months)

## December 2025 New Home Sales

- SAAR: **745,000**
- Median sales price: **$414,000**
- Average sales price: **$532,600**
- Inventory: 472,000 homes for sale

## Key Takeaway

The headline gain in housing starts was driven entirely by the multifamily segment, while single-family starts declined. Permitting activity -- an important near-term leading indicator -- pulled back across both segments, signaling potential softening ahead. New home sales fell sharply in January with rising months-of-supply suggesting buyer hesitancy amid economic uncertainty.
