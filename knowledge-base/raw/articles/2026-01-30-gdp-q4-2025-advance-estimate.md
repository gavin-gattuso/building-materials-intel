# Q4 2025 GDP Advance Estimate: Real GDP Growth Slows Sharply to 1.4%

## Key Details
- Real GDP increased at an annual rate of **1.4%** in Q4 2025 (advance estimate), well below expectations
- This represents a dramatic slowdown from **4.4% growth in Q3 2025**
- Contributors to growth: increases in consumer spending and investment
- Drags on growth: decreases in government spending and exports
- Full-year 2025 GDP growth estimated at approximately **2.5%**
- The second estimate (released March 13, 2026) revised Q4 GDP down further to **0.7%**

## Full Year 2025 GDP Composition
- Consumer spending remained the primary growth engine
- Business investment showed mixed signals
- Government spending contracted in Q4 after strong earlier quarters
- Net exports were a drag on Q4 growth

## 2026 Outlook
- Forecasters project **2.4% real GDP growth** for full-year 2026
- Goldman Sachs projects **2.5% Q4/Q4 growth**, above consensus of 2.1%
- Atlanta Fed GDPNow tracker providing real-time Q1 2026 estimates
- Recession probability estimates range from **20% (Goldman) to 42% (Moody's)**

## Analysis
- The sharp Q4 deceleration raised immediate recession concerns heading into 2026
- Construction sector contribution to GDP held at approximately **4.4-4.5%** of total GDP
- Total construction spending at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of **$2.19 trillion** in January 2026
- Weak GDP print initially supported the case for further Fed rate cuts, but persistent inflation complicated that narrative
- For building materials companies, slowing GDP growth signals potential demand softening, particularly in commercial and industrial construction segments
