# Taiheiyo Cement's CalPortland Delays Vulcan Ready-Mix Acquisition to H1 2026 After DOJ Scrutiny

## Key Details
- **Buyer**: CalPortland Company (wholly owned U.S. subsidiary of Taiheiyo Cement)
- **Seller**: Vulcan Materials Company
- **Target**: Vulcan's California ready-mixed concrete business assets
- **Deal value**: $712 million
- **Original expected close**: December 2025
- **Revised close**: First half of 2026
- **Reason for delay**: DOJ issued a "Second Request" for additional information under the Hart-Scott-Rodino Antitrust Improvements Act
- **Strategic rationale**: CalPortland expanding its ready-mix concrete presence in California

## Analysis
- The DOJ Second Request signals significant antitrust scrutiny of the deal, given the concentrated California ready-mix market
- CalPortland and Vulcan are both major players in California construction materials, and their combination could raise market share concerns
- The $712 million price tag reflects the strategic value of Vulcan's California ready-mix operations and their proximity to major infrastructure projects
- For Vulcan, this divestiture is consistent with its aggregates-first strategy and recycling capital into higher-return aggregates assets
- For Taiheiyo Cement, the CalPortland expansion represents the primary growth vehicle outside of Japan's declining domestic market
- If approved, this would make CalPortland one of the dominant ready-mix suppliers in California, a market benefiting from major infrastructure spending
