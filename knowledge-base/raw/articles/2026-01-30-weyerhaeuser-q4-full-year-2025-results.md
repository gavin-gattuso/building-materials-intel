# Weyerhaeuser Reports Q4 2025 Results: EPS of -$0.09 Beats Estimates; Mixed Revenue

## Key Details
- Q4 2025 EPS of -$0.09, beating consensus estimate of -$0.13 by 30.8%
- Revenue of $1.5 billion, missing Wall Street estimates
- Adjusted FFO of $0.09 per share topped expectations
- Returned $766 million in total cash to shareholders in full-year 2025, including $160 million in share repurchases
- Q1 2026 guidance: earnings before special items and Adjusted EBITDA comparable to Q4 overall
- Climate Solutions Adjusted EBITDA expected ~$90 million higher than Q4
- Real Estate, Energy & Natural Resources segment expects ~$75M higher earnings and ~$90M higher Adjusted EBITDA due to timing of real estate sales and a large conservation easement transaction
- Q1 2026 results due April 30, 2026 after market close
- Quarterly base dividend of $0.21/share ($0.84 annualized); yield of ~3.14%
- Full-year 2026 fee harvest volume target: ~35.5 million tons

## Analysis
- Weyerhaeuser's Q4 loss was narrower than expected, but the revenue miss reflects the persistent weakness in lumber pricing that has plagued the entire wood products industry.
- The Climate Solutions segment is emerging as a meaningful earnings contributor and differentiator for Weyerhaeuser vs. pure-play lumber peers.
- The $766M returned to shareholders demonstrates the REIT's commitment to its 75-80% Adjusted FAD return target even in a weak lumber pricing environment.
- The conservation easement transaction in Q1 2026 is a notable catalyst for near-term earnings improvement.
