# What War Risk Could Mean for Builders, Rates and Spring Demand in 2026

## Key Points
- Geopolitical uncertainty from Iran conflict creating headwinds for housing recovery
- Builders started 2026 with margin pressure before additional geopolitical risk emerged
- Housing market poised for growth in 2026 if Iran conflict doesn't raise rates
- War risk could push mortgage rates higher, undermining spring demand recovery
- Early 2026 results showed slightly higher demand but lower margins
- Builder incentives doing much of the work to sustain sales volumes
- Conflict-driven rate increases would be particularly damaging given already fragile affordability
