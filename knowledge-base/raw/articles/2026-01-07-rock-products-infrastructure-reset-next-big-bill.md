# The Coming Infrastructure Reset: Politics, Economics and the Next 'Big Bill'

## Key Points
- IIJA is now in its fifth year of funding, providing $56.8 billion in highway funding for FY 2026
- Current legislation expires September 30, 2026, requiring Congress to pass new multi-year surface transportation reauthorization
- Historically, trillion-dollar infrastructure bills only pass during recession, crisis, or broad national-security urgency
- Absent a major trigger, Congress unlikely to support another program at IIJA's original scale in inflation-adjusted terms
- Industry groups lobbying for continuation of robust federal infrastructure investment
- Reauthorization debate will be central policy issue for aggregates and construction sectors through 2026
- Outcome will significantly impact demand projections for aggregates, cement, and concrete through the late 2020s
