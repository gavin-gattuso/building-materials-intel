# Conference Board Leading Economic Index Falls 0.1% in January 2026 to 97.5

## Key Data Points
- LEI: **97.5** (2016=100) in January 2026
- Monthly change: **-0.1%** (following -0.2% decline in December 2025)
- Consumer expectations retreated again
- Building permits softened

## Component Analysis
- On a six-month basis, 7 out of 10 components were advancing (November 2025 through January 2026 -- three straight months of widespread strength)
- Despite the topline decline, the breadth of advancing components signals resilience in the underlying economy
- Key drags: consumer expectations, building permits, and stock prices
- Key strengths: labor market indicators, manufacturing orders, credit conditions

## Historical Context
- The LEI has been signaling headwinds to economic activity
- The index remains well below its 2021 peak
- The six-month annualized rate of change provides a better signal than single-month readings
- Persistent weakness in building permits is a direct concern for construction and building materials demand

## Construction Industry Implications
- Declining building permits within the LEI confirms softening residential construction pipeline
- The consumer expectations component aligns with the CCI Expectations Index below 80 (recession warning)
- However, widespread component strength on a 6-month basis suggests the economy is not yet in freefall
- For building materials: the permit decline translates to fewer housing starts in 3-6 months, pressuring wood, insulation, and roofing demand
