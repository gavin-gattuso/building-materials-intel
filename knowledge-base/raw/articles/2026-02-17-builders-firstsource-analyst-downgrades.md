# Builders FirstSource Faces Wave of Analyst Downgrades and Price Target Cuts

## Key Details
- **Jefferies Downgrade** (Dec 15, 2025): Buy to Hold, citing soft 2026 outlook
- **Stephens & Co Downgrade** (Jan 20, 2026): Overweight to Equal-Weight
- **Goldman Sachs PT Cut** (Feb 18, 2026): Price target cut to $142.00
- **Zacks Downgrade** (Feb 21, 2026): Hold to Strong Sell
- **RBC Capital Upgrade** (Mar 5, 2026): Sector Perform to Outperform, $119 PT
- **William Blair Initiation** (Feb 26, 2026): Outperform
- **Stifel PT Cut** (Mar 16, 2026): PT lowered from $115 to $93, maintained Hold
- **DA Davidson PT Cut**: PT lowered to $111
- **Overall Consensus**: Buy from 19 analysts (as of Jan 16, 2026)
- **Average PT**: $134.03 (range $95.95 to $157.50)
- **Share Buyback**: Since August 2021, repurchased 99.3 million shares (48.1% of total outstanding) at avg $80.90/share for $8.0 billion total

## Analysis
- The wave of downgrades reflects deep concern about the U.S. housing cycle, with persistently high mortgage rates suppressing new construction starts
- The divergence between bearish (Stifel $93) and bullish (Goldman $142) price targets reflects uncertainty about the housing recovery timeline
- RBC's upgrade to Outperform at $119 and William Blair's Outperform initiation suggest contrarian bulls see value emerging after the stock's pullback
- BLDR's massive buyback program ($8.0 billion over 3.5 years, reducing share count by 48%) has been an extraordinary capital return, offsetting earnings declines
- The stock represents a leveraged bet on the U.S. housing recovery; the timing of a mortgage rate decline is the key swing factor
