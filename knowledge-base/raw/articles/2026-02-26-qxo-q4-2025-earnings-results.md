# QXO Reports Q4 2025 Results: $2.19B Revenue; Beacon Integration Exceeding Expectations

## Key Details
- **Q4 2025 Net Sales**: $2.19 billion
- **Q4 2025 Adjusted EBITDA**: $150.3 million (6.9% margin)
- **Q4 2025 Adjusted Net Income**: $52.1 million
- **Q4 2025 Adjusted Diluted EPS**: $0.02
- **Full-Year 2025 Net Sales**: $6.84 billion
- **Full-Year 2025 Adjusted EBITDA**: $647.8 million (9.5% margin)
- **EBITDA Run Rate**: >$1 billion (including Kodiak) achieved in under 10 months
- **Beacon Integration**: Integration progressing; CEO Brad Jacobs says opportunities exceed initial expectations
- **Organic Growth Target**: "Confident we will at least double legacy Beacon EBITDA organically" -- Brad Jacobs
- **Revenue Target**: $50 billion in annual revenues within the next decade
- **Analyst Consensus**: Strong Buy from 11 analysts; average PT $33.45 (98.99% upside)
- **PT Range**: $26 to $50

## Analysis
- QXO's first full reporting period post-Beacon demonstrates the scale of the platform: $6.84 billion in FY 2025 revenue from just 8 months of Beacon contribution
- Brad Jacobs' target to "at least double legacy Beacon EBITDA organically" is an ambitious claim that, if achieved, would justify the premium paid for Beacon
- The >$1 billion EBITDA run rate including Kodiak positions QXO as one of the largest building products distributors in North America
- The wide analyst PT range ($26-$50) reflects the binary nature of the investment: success depends on Jacobs' ability to execute the roll-up playbook that worked at XPO Logistics
- QXO's strategy of tech-enabled distribution (AI-powered inventory, digital tools) differentiates it from traditional building products distributors
