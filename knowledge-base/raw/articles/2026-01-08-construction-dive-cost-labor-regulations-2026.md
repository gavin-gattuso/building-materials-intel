# Construction Dive: How Contractors Can Navigate Cost Pressures, Labor Shortages and Regulatory Hurdles in 2026

## Three-Front Challenge

### 1. Cost Pressures
- Construction input prices rising at 7.1% annualized rate to start 2026
- Tariff-affected metals (aluminum +33%, steel +21%) driving bulk of increases
- Contractors unable to fully pass costs through in bid prices (bid price increases only ~2.1% vs 3.6% input cost increases)
- Margin compression across the industry

### 2. Labor Shortages
- 349,000 net new workers needed in 2026 (ABC)
- Over half needed just to replace retirees
- Wages rising 6-8% annually
- Immigration enforcement adding further upward pressure on wages
- Electrician demand surging due to data center boom

### 3. Regulatory Hurdles
- EPA kiln regulations driving 4-6% cement/concrete price increases
- Build America, Buy America provisions affecting material sourcing
- New Section 232 tariffs on lumber and wood products
- State-level building code changes for energy efficiency

## Contractor Adaptation Strategies

- Integrating data analytics and cloud-based estimating platforms
- Adopting ML forecasting models for material price prediction
- Shifting toward modular/offsite construction to reduce on-site labor needs
- Stockpiling materials ahead of anticipated tariff increases
- Diversifying supplier networks to reduce single-source risk
- Investing in training and upskilling existing workforce
- Using technology (drones, wearables, AI) to boost productivity

## Industry Outlook

Despite the three-front challenge, contractors report cautious optimism for 2026. Strong public infrastructure spending, data center demand, and the structural housing shortage provide demand visibility. The firms best positioned are those investing in technology, diversifying their material sourcing, and building workforce development programs.
