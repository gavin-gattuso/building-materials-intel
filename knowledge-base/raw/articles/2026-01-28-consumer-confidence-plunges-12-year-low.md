# Consumer Confidence Plunges to 12-Year Low in January 2026

## Key Data Points

- Conference Board Consumer Confidence Index fell **9.7 points** in January to **84.5** (1985=100), from a revised 94.2 in December
- **Present Situation Index**: dropped 9.9 points to **113.7**
- **Expectations Index**: fell 9.5 points to **65.1** -- well below the **80 threshold** that usually signals a recession ahead
- This is the lowest level since **May 2014 (82.2)** -- surpassing COVID-19 pandemic depths
- All five components of the Index deteriorated

## Consumer Concerns

- References to **prices and inflation** remained elevated
- **Oil and gas prices** and **food and grocery prices** were top concerns
- Mentions of **tariffs and trade**, **politics**, and the **labor market** also rose in January
- Americans deeply pessimistic about the outlook despite robust GDP growth and relatively low overall unemployment

## Contrasting Data

- Ipsos global consumer confidence index showed a slight increase in January 2026
- YouGov headline consumer confidence rose 0.8 points from 107.1 to 107.9
- The divergence suggests U.S.-specific concerns (tariffs, political uncertainty) are driving the domestic pessimism

## Implications for Construction

- Consumer confidence at this level has historically correlated with reduced housing demand and deferred renovation spending
- The sub-80 Expectations reading is a recession warning that could dampen construction investment plans
- However, construction activity often lags confidence shifts by 6-12 months
