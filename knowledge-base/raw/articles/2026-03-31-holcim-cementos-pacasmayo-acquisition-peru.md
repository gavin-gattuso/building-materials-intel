# Holcim Completes $1.5 Billion Acquisition of Majority Stake in Peru's Cementos Pacasmayo

## Key Details
- **Transaction value**: ~US$1.5 billion (on 100% basis)
- **Stake acquired**: 50.01% (via purchase of Inversiones Aspi S.A. from Hochschild Group)
- **Target**: Cementos Pacasmayo S.A.A., leading Peruvian building materials producer
- **Projected 2025 net sales**: USD 630 million
- **EBITDA margin**: 28%
- **Valuation**: 8.8x 2025 consensus EBITDA; 7.1x after run-rate synergies (~$40M in year 3)
- **Assets**: 3 cement plants with ~5 million tonnes/year combined capacity; 28 ready-mix and precast concrete plants
- **Agreement signed**: December 16, 2025
- **Completion**: March 30, 2026
- **Strategic fit**: Aligns with NextGen Growth 2030 strategy for Latin America expansion

## Analysis
- This acquisition marks Holcim's re-entry into Peru and significantly expands its Latin American footprint
- The 28% EBITDA margin reflects Pacasmayo's dominant position in northern Peru with limited competition
- The 7.1x post-synergy multiple is attractive for a high-margin, market-leading cement business
- The timing is notable: as CEMEX divests Latin American assets (Colombia, Panama), Holcim is expanding aggressively in the region
- Peru's infrastructure deficit and growing population support long-term cement demand
- This deal, combined with the CEMEX Colombia acquisition ($485M), positions Holcim as the dominant cement player in the Andean region
