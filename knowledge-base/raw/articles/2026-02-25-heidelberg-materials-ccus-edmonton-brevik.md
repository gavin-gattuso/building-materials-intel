# Heidelberg Materials Leads Cement Industry CCUS: Edmonton Plant Expected Online Late 2026

## Key Details
- **Edmonton CCUS facility** (Canada): Expected operational by late 2026; will capture >1 million tonnes CO2 annually
  - Partnership with Government of Canada, Government of Alberta, and Mitsubishi Heavy Industries
  - Uses MHI's proprietary Advanced KM CDR Process with KS-21 solvent
  - CO2 transported via pipeline and permanently sequestered
  - World's first full-scale CCUS solution for the cement industry
- **Brevik CCS facility** (Norway): Inaugurated mid-June 2025; world's first industrial-scale CCS in cement
- **Lengfurt CCU facility** (Germany): Partnership with Linde; world's first industrial-scale CCU in cement; CO2 reused as raw material
- **Target**: Carbon-captured near-zero concrete across entire product portfolio by 2050

## Analysis
- Heidelberg Materials is the clear global leader in cement industry decarbonization, with operational facilities on three continents
- The Edmonton facility's 1 Mtpa capture capacity will be a landmark achievement for the entire cement sector
- CCUS technology is critical for the cement industry because process emissions from calcination cannot be eliminated through fuel switching alone
- The Brevik and Lengfurt projects provide proof-of-concept that is now being scaled at Edmonton
- Regulatory frameworks like the EU ETS and Canada's carbon pricing create direct economic incentives for CCUS investment
- This positions Heidelberg Materials favorably for future carbon border adjustments and green building mandates
