# Vulcan Materials Q4 2025 Earnings Miss; FY2025 Delivers $2.3B EBITDA Record

## Key Details
- **Q4 2025 EPS**: $1.70 (missed estimate of $2.13)
- **Q4 2025 revenue**: $1.91 billion (missed estimate of $1.95 billion)
- **Q4 2025 adjusted EPS**: Declined from $2.17 in prior-year quarter
- **Stock reaction**: Down 8.24% in pre-market trading
- **FY2025 adjusted EBITDA**: $2.3 billion (up 13% YoY)
- **FY2025 EBITDA margin**: 29.3% (up 160 bps)
- **FY2025 revenue**: $7.94 billion (up 7.06%)
- **Aggregates cash gross profit per ton**: $11.33 (met $11-$12 target range)
- **2026 Guidance**: Adjusted EBITDA $2.4B-$2.6B (growth of 3-12% vs. 2025)
  - Aggregates volume growth: 1-3%
- **Seven analysts** revised earnings expectations downward for upcoming periods

## Analysis
- The Q4 miss triggered a significant sell-off, but the full-year results tell a more positive story with record EBITDA and margin expansion
- The $11.33 per-ton cash gross profit represents consistent execution toward the company's long-term profitability targets
- The wide 2026 guidance range ($2.4B-$2.6B) reflects uncertainty around residential construction recovery and IIJA timing
- Management flagged that 2026 will be "a very active year on the strategy side and the M&A front"
- The downward analyst revisions suggest the Street is cautious on near-term execution despite the strong structural story
- Vulcan's 29.3% EBITDA margin is among the highest in the aggregates sector, demonstrating pricing discipline
