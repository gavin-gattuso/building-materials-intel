# Mortgage Rates Fall Below 6% for First Time in Years

## Key Rate Data (February 2026)
- 30-year fixed-rate mortgage fell below 6% for the first time since 2022
- Freddie Mac PMMS early February: 30-year FRM averaged 6.11% (Feb 5)
- Mid-February: rates dropped to approximately 5.98%
- Late February rates (Feb 27):
  - Zillow: 5.87% (30-year fixed)
  - Bankrate: 6.04% (30-year fixed)
  - 15-year fixed: 5.37% average
- Year-over-year comparison: rates were 6.76% for 30-year mortgages in February 2025

## Driving Factors
- Treasury yields declining amid economic uncertainty
- Tariff-related market volatility pushing investors to safe-haven bonds
- Federal Reserve rate cuts working through the system
- Geopolitical uncertainty (late February Iran conflict) driving flight to safety

## Market Impact
- Sub-6% rates represent a significant psychological threshold for homebuyers
- However, many existing homeowners remain "locked in" at rates below 4% from 2020-2021
- Rate spread between existing and new mortgages still limits housing turnover

## Forward-Looking Forecasts
- Fannie Mae February 2026 Housing Forecast: rates expected to sit at ~6% for most of 2026 and 2027
- MBA forecast: gradual decline through 2026 but staying above 5.5%
- Freddie Mac PMMS weekly surveys showed rates hovering in the 5.87%-6.11% range throughout February

## Housing Market Context
- New home sales in January 2026: 587,000 SAAR (down 17.6% from December, down 11.3% YoY)
- Median new home sales price (January 2026): $400,500
- Inventory of new homes for sale: 476,000 (January 2026)
- Sub-6% rates may begin to unlock some pent-up demand in spring 2026
