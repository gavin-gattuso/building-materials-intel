# Fed Leaves Rates Unchanged at January 2026 FOMC Meeting

## Decision

- The FOMC maintained the target range for the federal funds rate at **3-1/2 to 3-3/4 percent** on January 28, 2026
- This halted three consecutive quarter-point reductions delivered in late 2025 (September, November, December)
- Total 2025 rate cuts: **75 basis points**

## Dissents

- Governors **Stephen Miran** and **Christopher Waller** voted against the hold, both advocating another quarter-point cut

## Economic Assessment

- Economic activity has been expanding at a "solid pace"
- Job gains have remained low
- Unemployment rate has shown some signs of stabilization
- Inflation remains "somewhat elevated"

## Forward Guidance

- Officials maintained a median forecast for **one rate cut in 2026**, consistent with December projection
- Next FOMC meeting: **March 17-18, 2026**, with policy announcement on March 18

## Implications for Construction

- Holding rates steady signals the Fed is cautious about inflation resurgence, partly driven by tariff-related cost pressures
- Construction lending rates remain elevated relative to 2021-2022 levels
- The pause creates uncertainty for project financing and housing demand
- Mortgage rates hovered near 6.1-6.2% in January, reflecting the Fed's wait-and-see posture
