# Johnson Controls Launches 1-Gigawatt AI Data Center Thermal Management Reference Design Guides

## Key Details
- Reference Design Guide Series for 1-GW AI data centers launched February 2, 2026
- Maps full thermal chains for water-cooled, air-cooled, and absorption chiller architectures
- First guide: water-cooled chiller plant blueprint
- Provides sizing for 220MW compute quadrants
- Integration with CRAHs, CDUs, and YORK centrifugal chillers
- Aligns thermal designs with NVIDIA DSX reference architecture
- Supports high-density AI factories with scalable, repeatable deployment
- Options for zero water consumption, high-temperature TCS readiness
- NVIDIA DSX alignment for compatibility with NVIDIA-focused system designs
- Improved PUE/WUE across diverse climates and elevations

## Analysis
- This is a landmark move in HVAC: Johnson Controls is essentially creating the "blueprints" for cooling the next generation of AI data centers at gigawatt scale. The NVIDIA DSX alignment is critical -- it means Johnson Controls' designs are compatible with the dominant AI compute architecture. This positions JCI as a design partner, not just a component supplier, which deepens customer relationships and creates switching costs. The gigawatt-scale focus targets the largest, most capital-intensive data center projects.
