# January 2026 PPI: Nonresidential Construction Inputs Surge at 7.1% Annualized Rate

## Key Details
- Nonresidential construction input prices rose at a **7.1% annualized rate** in January 2026
- Driven by tariff-affected materials: steel, aluminum, copper wire, industrial controls equipment
- Metals and metal products prices up **16.6% year-over-year**
- Aluminum mill shapes PPI rocketed up **33.0%** year-over-year -- largest since early 2022 supply chain disruptions
- Metal molding and trim up **61.7%** year-over-year

## Residential Construction Inputs
- Price index for inputs to new residential construction: data updated in February release
- Building material prices up **3.5% year-over-year** as of latest data
- Goods used in new residential construction up **3.0% year-over-year**
- Services component up **4.2% year-over-year**

## Material-Specific PPI Movements
- **Steel:** Up approximately 13% year-over-year driven by 50% Section 232 tariffs
- **Aluminum:** Up 33% year-over-year on 50% tariffs and constrained supply
- **Copper wire:** Significant increases following 50% copper tariff implementation
- **Lumber and plywood:** Relatively flat year-over-year, first sustained stabilization since pandemic

## Analysis
- The 7.1% annualized rate for nonresidential inputs is a "blistering pace" per AGC
- Tariff-driven metal price increases are the primary inflation driver, not broad-based demand-pull inflation
- This creates an asymmetric cost environment: metal-intensive projects face severe cost escalation while wood-frame residential is relatively sheltered
- AGC-NCCER survey found **43% of contractors** reported at least one project canceled, postponed, or scaled back due to tariff-driven cost increases
- For building materials companies: metal products producers benefit from pricing power, while downstream contractors and developers face margin compression
