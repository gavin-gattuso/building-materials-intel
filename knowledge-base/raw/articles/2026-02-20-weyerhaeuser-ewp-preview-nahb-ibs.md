# Weyerhaeuser Previews New Engineered Wood Products at NAHB International Builders' Show

## Key Details
- Weyerhaeuser showcased new engineered wood products at the NAHB International Builders' Show (IBS) in February 2026
- Product previews tied to $500M TimberStrand facility in Arkansas (operations target: 2027)
- New Arkansas facility to produce ~10 million cubic feet annually; doubles North American TimberStrand capacity
- Expected $100M+ annual Adjusted EBITDA at full capacity
- Facility uses southern yellow pine feedstock with biomass-fueled cogeneration
- EWP portfolio expansion part of $1.5B incremental EBITDA target by 2030
- Nearly 200 new jobs planned for Monticello, AR area

## Analysis
- The NAHB IBS showcase positions Weyerhaeuser as an innovation leader in engineered wood products, which are gaining share from traditional lumber and steel in mid-rise construction.
- The TimberStrand product line addresses a growing market for engineered structural lumber that offers superior dimensional stability and load-bearing capacity.
- At $100M+ annual EBITDA potential, the Arkansas facility alone would represent a meaningful step toward the $1.5B incremental EBITDA target.
- The use of biomass cogeneration aligns with sustainability trends and reduces operating costs, potentially giving Weyerhaeuser a competitive edge in the EWP segment.
