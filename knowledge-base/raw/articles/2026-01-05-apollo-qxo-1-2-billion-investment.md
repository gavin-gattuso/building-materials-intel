# Apollo Leads $1.2 Billion Investment in Brad Jacobs' QXO

## Deal Details

- **Investment**: Apollo Global Management led a **$1.2 billion** investment in QXO Inc. on January 5, 2026
- **Additional funding**: An additional **$1.8 billion** from Apollo, Temasek, and other investors announced January 12, 2026
- **Total war chest**: Approximately **$3 billion** in acquisition capital
- **Deadline**: QXO needed to announce a deal by **July 15, 2026**
- **Pipeline**: QXO was reported to be in late-stage talks with **7 potential acquisition targets**

## Context

- Brad Jacobs' QXO is pursuing a roll-up strategy in the **$250+ billion** building products distribution market
- QXO's addressable market exceeds **$200 billion**
- This followed the pattern set by Jacobs' previous ventures (XPO Logistics)

## Subsequent Activity

- On **February 11, 2026**, QXO announced the acquisition of **Kodiak Building Partners** for **$2.25 billion** from Court Square Capital Partners
- Kodiak generated approximately **$2.4 billion** in revenues in 2025
- Kodiak distributes lumber, trusses, windows and doors, construction supplies, waterproofing, roofing, and complementary exterior products
- The Kodiak deal closed on **April 1, 2026**

## Strategic Significance

This investment and subsequent deal confirmed QXO as the most aggressive new consolidator in building products distribution, joining the wave of mega-deals reshaping the sector (alongside Home Depot/SRS, Lowe's/FBM). The building products distribution sector is rapidly consolidating as scale advantages in technology, procurement, and logistics become more critical.
