# Fitch Upgrades Vulcan Materials to BBB+ with Stable Outlook

## Key Data Points
- **Rating action:** Upgraded Long-term Issuer Default Rating from BBB to **BBB+**
- **Short-term IDR:** Upgraded from F2 to **F1**
- **Outlook:** Stable
- **Date:** August 28, 2025

## Rationale
- Strong EBITDA and free cash flow margins underpin the upgrade
- Leading market position with diverse quarry network spanning **23 states**
- Substantial financial flexibility
- EBITDA margins expected to stabilize around **30%** in coming years
- Free cash flow margin forecast between **9-10%** in 2025 and 2026

## Sector Context
- Fitch expects continued softness in U.S. construction environment in 2025
- Residential and non-residential construction expected to decrease slightly before improving marginally in 2026
- Infrastructure spending (IIJA) provides demand visibility for aggregates

## Construction Industry Implications
- The upgrade reflects Vulcan's dominant position in U.S. aggregates -- a critical building material
- BBB+ rating reduces Vulcan's borrowing costs, supporting continued M&A and capital investment
- Aggregates companies benefit from local market monopolies and high barriers to entry
- Vulcan's 23-state quarry network positions it to capture IIJA infrastructure spending across regions
- The upgrade contrasts with the broader sector trend of increasing negative outlooks, highlighting Vulcan's relative strength
