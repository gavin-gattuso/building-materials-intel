# Heidelberg Materials Expands Australian Footprint with AUD 1.7 Billion Maas Group Acquisition

## Key Details
- **Target**: Construction materials business of Maas Group Holdings (Australia)
- **Enterprise value**: AUD 1.7 billion (cash and debt free basis)
- **EBITDA multiple**: 8.4x after synergies
- **Assets acquired**: 40 quarries with combined reserves of over 350 million tonnes, 22 concrete plants, two asphalt operations, and a recycling site
- **Expected completion**: Second half of 2026
- **Additional context**: Since 2021, Heidelberg Materials has made 31 acquisitions and 12 disposals globally
- **Other recent acquisitions**: BURNCO Rock Products Ltd (Canada), Walan Specialty Construction Products (USA), Midway Concrete (Australia)

## Analysis
- This is a transformative deal for Heidelberg Materials' Australian operations, adding significant scale in aggregates and concrete
- The 350 million tonnes of quarry reserves provides decades of production runway in a growing market
- The 8.4x post-synergy EBITDA multiple is reasonable for high-quality aggregates assets, in line with recent North American transactions
- Australia's infrastructure pipeline (particularly in New South Wales and Queensland) supports long-term demand for construction materials
- This deal aligns with CEO Dr. Dominik von Achten's pure-play heavy building materials strategy, concentrating on aggregates, cement, and concrete
- The acquisition complements earlier moves in North America (BURNCO) and positions Heidelberg Materials as a global aggregates leader
