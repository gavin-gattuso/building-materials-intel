# Construction Hiring Falls to Slowest Rate on Record in February 2026

## February 2026 Labor Market Data
- Job openings: 202,000 (last day of February, per BLS)
  - Down 28,000 from January
  - Down 53,000 from year-ago levels
- Hiring rate: 3.3% (slowest on record)
  - Down from 4.4% in January
  - Down from 4.2% year-prior
- Indicates contractors holding onto workforce; workers less inclined to leave positions

## Key Labor Market Dynamics
- Stagnation in both hiring and layoffs
- "Lock-in" effect: workers staying put, employers not expanding
- Immigration policy tightening adding upward wage pressure
- Construction wages rising faster than broader economy

## Wage Trends
- Annual wage hikes of 6-8% expected in 2026
- Base pay for skilled laborers jumping 8-10% in some metros over last 12 months
- Labor shortages making bidding riskier for contractors

## 2026 Workforce Demand
- Industry needs estimated 349,000 net new workers in 2026 (ABC estimate)
- Reduced from previous years when demand was stronger
- Significant but manageable compared to prior peak estimates
- Demographic pressures as large portions of workforce near retirement

## Industry Context
- Construction labor market shifting from acute shortage to structural constraint
- Wage growth outpacing inflation creates margin pressure for contractors
- Tighter immigration policy limits traditional labor supply pipeline
- Firms increasingly investing in prefabrication and automation to offset labor challenges
