# PwC Engineering & Construction: US Deals 2026 Outlook

## M&A Activity Overview
- M&A activity in construction and building products remained mostly stable in 2025
- Brief rebound in 2024 was followed by fragile demand conditions
- Uncertain outlook constraining deal volumes
- 2025 deal activity declined ~30% year-over-year across building products

## 2026 Deal Environment
- Strategic buyers continuing to lead transactions
- Private equity disciplined, focused on targeted opportunities
- Center of gravity shifting from scale plays toward scope and capability acquisitions
- Data center construction boom driving deal interest in electrical, mechanical segments

## Key M&A Themes for 2026
- Capability-driven acquisitions (technology, specialized installation)
- Geographic expansion into high-growth markets (Texas, Florida, Southeast)
- Vertical integration plays (manufacturing + installation)
- Infrastructure-focused services companies attracting premium valuations
- Sustainability/energy efficiency capabilities commanding interest

## Deal Flow Drivers
- Booming data center construction
- IIJA infrastructure spending in peak execution year
- Pent-up demand for affordable housing (eventual recovery)
- Aging housing stock supporting repair/remodel segment
- Technology adoption driving demand for specialized capabilities
