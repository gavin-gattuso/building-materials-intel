# NAHB/Wells Fargo HMI Falls to 36 in February 2026

## Headline Data
- NAHB/Wells Fargo Housing Market Index (HMI): 36 in February (down 1 point from January)
- 22nd consecutive month below the breakeven level of 50
- Second straight monthly decline to start 2026

## HMI Component Scores
- Current sales conditions: 41 (unchanged from January)
- Future sales expectations (next 6 months): 46 (down 3 points from January)
- Traffic of prospective buyers: 22 (down 2 points from January)

## Regional HMI Scores (3-Month Moving Averages)
- Northeast: 43 (down 1 point)
- Midwest: 43 (unchanged)
- South: 35 (down 1 point)
- West: 33 (down 2 points)

## Price Cutting Activity
- 36% of builders cut prices in February (down 4 percentage points from January)
- Average price reduction: 6% (unchanged from January)

## Key Drivers of Weakness
- Persistent affordability challenges
- High housing price-to-income ratios
- Elevated land and construction costs
- Mortgage rates remaining above 6%
- Tariff uncertainty adding to material cost concerns

## Context
- Builder sentiment has remained subdued throughout late 2025 and early 2026
- January 2026 HMI was 37 (had already lost ground from late 2025)
- Readings below 50 indicate more builders view conditions as poor than good
- The traffic of prospective buyers component at 22 signals exceptionally weak buyer foot traffic
