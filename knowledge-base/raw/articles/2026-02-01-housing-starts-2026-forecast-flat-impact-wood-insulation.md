# Housing Starts 2026 Forecast: Essentially Flat vs. 2025; Muted Outlook for Wood and Insulation Demand

## Key Details
- Zonda/John Burns national outlook: single-family and multi-family starts essentially flat compared with 2025
- 2026 characterized as "another muted year in housing" with potential to be softer than baseline
- Key headwinds: affordability constraints, elevated interest rates, cautious consumer sentiment
- Wood fiber insulation market: $0.99B in 2026, reaching $1.34B by 2031 (CAGR 6.25%)
- Residential projects hold 53.22% of insulation demand; commercial construction growing at 6.86% CAGR
- Despite flat starts, insulation demand growing due to building code mandates and retrofit subsidies
- North America: fastest-growing region for wood fiber insulation
- Carbon-intensity limits in building codes and mandatory whole-life carbon accounting in public procurement support insulation growth
- State and provincial retrofit incentives accelerating demand independent of new construction

## Analysis
- Flat housing starts in 2026 are a headwind for all wood and insulation companies, but the impact varies by business model: pure lumber producers (Canfor, Interfor, West Fraser) are most exposed, while diversified players (Owens Corning, Saint-Gobain, UFP Industries) benefit from renovation and code-driven demand.
- The disconnect between flat housing starts and growing insulation demand highlights the importance of building code mandates as a demand driver independent of new construction volumes.
- For Weyerhaeuser, flat starts pressure the Wood Products segment but are partially offset by Climate Solutions and Real Estate segments that have different demand drivers.
- The housing affordability crisis may be reaching a structural plateau where starts cannot materially increase until either rates drop significantly or land/labor costs decline.
