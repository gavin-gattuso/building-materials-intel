# Daikin Applied Acquires Chilldyne and DDC Solutions to Build Data Center Cooling Ecosystem

## Key Details
- Daikin Applied acquired Chilldyne in November 2025, a developer of direct-to-chip liquid cooling solutions for data centers
- Previously acquired DDC Solutions (San Diego) in August 2025, maker of high-density cooling cabinets and management software
- Chilldyne and DDC Solutions have proven integration enabling efficient cooling for high-density rack environments
- Daikin opening two new manufacturing facilities in 2026: one for modular systems for hyperscale data centers, one to double output for custom air handlers
- Part of broader FUSION30 strategic plan focusing on solutions-based business structure
- Target: #1 sales position in North America
- Strategy includes expanding in the "Global South" (emerging markets)

## Analysis
- Daikin's back-to-back data center cooling acquisitions mirror the strategies of Carrier and Trane Technologies, confirming that AI-driven data center cooling is the most important growth vector in HVAC. The Chilldyne + DDC Solutions combination gives Daikin an integrated liquid cooling platform. The two new manufacturing facilities demonstrate Daikin's willingness to invest aggressively in capacity to capture this market. The race among the top three global HVAC companies (Daikin, Carrier, Trane) for data center cooling dominance is intensifying.
