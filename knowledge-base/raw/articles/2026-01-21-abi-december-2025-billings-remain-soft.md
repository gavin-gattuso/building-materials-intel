# ABI December 2025: Architecture Billings Close Year Soft at 48.5

## Key Data Points
- AIA/Deltek Architecture Billings Index: **48.5** in December 2025 (up from 45.3 in November)
- Below the 50 breakeven threshold, indicating continued contraction in billings
- Architecture firm billings declined throughout 2025 and most months since October 2022, with only three exceptions
- Project backlogs remain solid, averaging **6.3 months** across all firms
- Large firms ($5M+ annual billings) averaged backlogs of **8.6 months**
- Institutional-focused firms averaged backlogs of **8.2 months**

## Regional Performance
- Midwest: **51.7** (above 50 -- growth for fourth consecutive month)
- South: **47.7**
- West: **45.3**
- Northeast: **44.2** (weakest region)

## Sector Performance
- Institutional: **48.7**
- Commercial/Industrial: **47.8**
- Multifamily Residential: **45.5**
- Mixed Practice: **44.0**

## Analysis
- The ABI is a leading indicator for nonresidential construction with a 9-12 month lead time
- December's reading suggests continued softness in nonresidential construction activity through Q3-Q4 2026
- The Midwest's above-50 reading is a bright spot, potentially driven by manufacturing investment (CHIPS Act)
- Solid backlogs provide some buffer against the prolonged billing decline, suggesting existing projects continue but new commitments remain weak
- 2025 ended with contraction in 37 of the last 40 months -- the longest downturn since the post-GFC period
