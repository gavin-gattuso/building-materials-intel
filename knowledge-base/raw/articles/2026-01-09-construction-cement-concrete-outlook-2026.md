# The 2026 Construction, Cement and Concrete Outlook

## Key Details
- **IIJA expiration**: September 30, 2026 (5-year program; $1.2 trillion total, $550 billion new spending)
- **Peak IIJA execution**: 2026 represents the peak spending year for IIJA-funded projects
- **Post-2026 uncertainty**: Replacement program is critical for volume outlook in concrete-related industries
- **2026 midterm elections**: Increase likelihood of patchwork reauthorization rather than comprehensive surface transportation bill
- **Residential sector**: High mortgage rates and low affordability constrain new housing starts
- **Nonresidential**: Data centers, warehouses, and reshoring-driven manufacturing provide growth
- **Aggregates trend**: Six key trends identified for 2026 including sustainability, technology adoption, and labor challenges
- **Price trajectory**: Continued positive pricing momentum for aggregates; cement prices up mid-single digits

## Analysis
- The IIJA expiration risk is the elephant in the room for the entire cement/aggregates sector
- A patchwork reauthorization (short-term extensions) would reduce spending predictability and could disrupt project pipelines
- The 2026 midterm election cycle makes comprehensive legislation unlikely before 2027
- State DOTs have built backlogs of IIJA-funded projects that will sustain activity through 2027 even if federal reauthorization is delayed
- Data center construction is emerging as a meaningful new demand driver, particularly for aggregates producers near major metro areas
- The combination of tariff-driven import costs, domestic supply constraints, and infrastructure demand supports continued pricing power
- Companies with the strongest exposure to public infrastructure (CRH, Martin Marietta, Vulcan) are best positioned for the transition period
