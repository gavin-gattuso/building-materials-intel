# Martin Marietta Materials Q4 2025: Record Revenue of $6.15B, Up 9%

## Full-Year 2025 Results
- Record full-year revenue: $6.15 billion (up 9% over 2024)
- Consolidated adjusted EBITDA margins: approximately 35.5%
- Aggregate pricing increased 12% in 2025
- Record gross profit per ton: $8.59

## Key Performance Drivers
- Pricing power more than offset inflationary pressures on diesel, labor, and explosives
- Infrastructure spending from IIJA supporting public-sector volumes
- Continued strength in U.S. public works construction
- Efficiency improvements driving margin expansion

## Strategic Positioning
- MLM benefits from significant exposure to infrastructure and heavy construction
- IIJA entering final year of authorization (expires September 30, 2026)
- Public works demand expected to remain robust through 2026
- Company well-positioned for reauthorization cycle

## Industry Context
- Aggregates sector showing remarkable pricing discipline
- MLM and Vulcan Materials both reported record profitability metrics
- Infrastructure demand offsetting softness in residential construction
- Both companies able to push through double-digit price increases despite mixed volume trends
- Construction materials sector overall underperformed in Q4 (revenue estimates missed by 1.2%)
