# KPMG Building Products and Solutions Market Perspective 2026

## Market Conditions

- Rising interest rates, tariffs, and general uncertainty impacted building products M&A market
- Transaction volume reduced since post-COVID peak of 2021
- However, a flurry of **large-scale, billion-dollar transactions** defied the overall volume trend

## Structural Demand Drivers

- Freddie Mac estimates housing shortage of **3.7 million units** -- providing a structural demand floor
- R&R (repair & remodel) spending is **uniquely durable**, with both residential and non-residential demand expected to persist
- Bipartisan support for federal infrastructure investment driving commercial project activity

## Industry Transformation

- Home Depot's acquisition of **SRS Distribution** and emergence of **QXO** as a consolidator are transforming the distribution sector
- Leading to consolidation across different home services sectors (roofing, drywall, insulation)
- Professional contractor channels becoming the primary battleground for large retailers

## Market Clarity

- While U.S. tariff strategy and foreign policy remains in flux, increasing clarity in:
  - Supplier channel configurations
  - "Normalized" pricing levels
  - How firms are adjusting procurement strategies

## Outlook

- Long-term tailwinds continue to exist for the industry
- Near-term headwinds from tariffs and interest rates
- But structural growth drivers (housing shortage, aging housing stock, infrastructure spending) support sustained M&A activity
- The sector continues to attract both strategic and financial buyers
