# Martin Marietta's Results Signal Multi-Year Infrastructure Supercycle

## Key Details
- Martin Marietta's FY2025 results confirm a strong multi-year demand cycle for aggregates
- Public construction remains a powerful tailwind, underpinned by multi-year IIJA infrastructure funding
- Stronger contract awards across core markets support volume growth
- Pricing power remains solid due to limited quarry supply, high barriers to entry, and tight local markets
- Aggregates are primarily domestically sourced, providing natural tariff protection
- The IIJA provides federal funding through September 2026, with peak execution phase underway
- Both Martin Marietta and Vulcan Materials benefit from the same demand dynamics
- **Morgan Stanley**: Reduced MLM price target from $706 to $702, maintained Overweight
- **Citigroup**: Raised MLM price target from $780 to $804 in mid-February
- **MLM consensus**: Moderate Buy; average price target $689.40 (4.2% upside)

## Analysis
- The infrastructure supercycle thesis is well-supported by IIJA spending data and state DOT contract awards
- Aggregates producers like Martin Marietta and Vulcan Materials are among the primary beneficiaries of this cycle
- The domestic nature of aggregates production provides insulation from tariff disruption, unlike cement which is ~20% imported
- The key risk is IIJA expiration in September 2026 and potential for a patchwork reauthorization rather than a comprehensive new surface transportation bill
- State and local government budgets remain healthy, providing a secondary demand floor even if federal spending decelerates
- The Citigroup price target increase to $804 reflects long-term confidence in MLM's cash flow generation and competitive positioning
