# January 2026 SLOOS: Construction Lending Standards Expected to Tighten Modestly

## Key Details - Q4 2025 Activity
- Standards for **multifamily property loans:** modest net share of banks eased
- Standards for **construction and land development loans:** remained basically unchanged on net
- Standards for **nonfarm nonresidential property loans:** basically unchanged on net
- **Large banks** reported easing CRE loan standards on balance
- **Other banks** reported tightening standards on balance -- a notable divergence

## Demand Trends
- **Moderate** net share of banks reported stronger demand for nonfarm nonresidential loans
- **Modest** net share reported stronger demand for construction and land development loans
- Overall CRE loan demand improving relative to prior quarters

## 2026 Outlook from Banks
- Banks expect lending standards to remain **basically unchanged** for most loan categories in 2026
- **Exception:** Modest net shares of banks expect to **tighten standards for construction and land development loans**
- Credit quality expectations: significant to moderate net shares of banks expect **improvements** for all CRE loan types in 2026

## Reasons Banks Cited for Expected Tightening
- Less favorable or more uncertain economic outlook
- Expected deterioration in collateral values
- Expected deterioration in credit quality of loan portfolio
- Expected reduction in risk tolerance

## Analysis
- The expectation of tighter construction lending standards in 2026 is a headwind for new project starts
- The large-bank vs. small-bank divergence is important: large banks easing while small banks tighten means access to construction credit may depend on borrower size and banking relationships
- The improvement in CRE loan demand is positive, but the anticipated tightening of standards could cap the recovery
- For building materials: tighter construction lending directly reduces new project starts, the primary driver of material demand
- The $1.8 trillion CRE maturity wall in 2026 adds refinancing risk that could further constrain lending
