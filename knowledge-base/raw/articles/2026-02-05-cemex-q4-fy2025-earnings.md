# CEMEX Q4 2025 Earnings: Revenue Beat but EPS Misses Estimates

## Key Details
- **Q4 2025 EPS**: $0.13, missing analyst estimate of $0.20
- **Q4 2025 revenue**: $4.18 billion, exceeding estimate of $4.01 billion
- **Stock price at report**: $12.75, near 52-week high of $13.35
- **Stock surged more than 100% in 2025**
- **Project Cutting Edge** delivered $200 million in recurring EBITDA savings in 2025 (ahead of target)
- Q3 2025 alone generated ~$90 million in EBITDA savings from the program
- **US operations** delivered record fourth-quarter EBITDA and higher margins
- Goldman Sachs estimates 2026 EBITDA of $3.4 billion (+11% YoY), 1% above consensus
- **Q1 2026 earnings** scheduled for April 27, 2026 (est. EPS $0.10, revenue $3.81B)

## Analysis
- The EPS miss was offset by strong revenue performance, indicating top-line momentum but margin compression in certain segments
- Project Cutting Edge is transforming CEMEX's cost structure; the program is running ahead of schedule
- The 100%+ stock appreciation in 2025 reflects market confidence in CEMEX's self-help transformation story
- U.S. operations continue to outperform, benefiting from IIJA-funded infrastructure projects and resilient nonresidential construction
- CEMEX's pivot to a higher-margin, U.S.-focused aggregates business through divestitures and bolt-on acquisitions is reshaping the investment thesis
