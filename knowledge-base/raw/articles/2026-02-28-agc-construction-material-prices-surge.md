# Construction Material Prices Surge at 12.6% Annualized Rate in Early 2026

## Headline Data
- Input prices for nonresidential construction surged at a 12.6% annualized rate during the first two months of 2026 (ABC data)
- Inputs to nonresidential construction grew 1.3% month-over-month in February
- Prices 3.7% higher in February 2026 than February 2025

## Key Material Price Changes (Year-over-Year, Feb 2025 to Feb 2026)
- Aluminum mill shapes: +39.1% (largest YoY increase since early 2022 supply-chain disruptions)
- Steel mill products: +20.9% (largest YoY increase since early 2022)
- Fabricated structural metal bar joists and rebar: +20.0%
- Copper and brass mill shapes: +15.1%
- Steel bars, plates, and structural shapes (PPI): +12.1%
- Steel pipe and tube (PPI): +9.4%

## Lumber Prices
- National average for framing lumber: ~$872 per thousand board feet (MBF)
- Year-over-year lumber costs up nearly 13%

## Cement and Concrete
- Concrete and cement seeing 4-6% price increases
- New EPA kiln regulations contributing to cost pressure
- 25% tariffs on Mexican and Canadian cement adding upward pressure
- Canada supplies ~5 million metric tons of cement; Mexico ~2 million metric tons
- Together account for over 25% of all U.S. cement imports

## Energy Cost Drivers (February 2026)
- Natural gas prices: +10.9% month-over-month
- Unprocessed energy materials: +6.0% month-over-month
- Crude petroleum: +4.7% month-over-month

## Geopolitical Impact (Late February)
- Iran conflict began February 28, 2026 (US-Israeli airstrikes)
- Oil prices nearing $100/barrel
- Diesel prices jumped 20%+ in early weeks of conflict
- Strait of Hormuz disruption forcing tanker reroutes around Africa
- IEA characterized as "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market"
- AGC noted the February PPI data doesn't yet capture the full Iran war impact

## Industry Context
- Characterized as a "cascading failure across multiple supply chains"
- Spearheaded by spikes in oil, copper, aluminum, and steel
- Most volatile materials: steel, asphalt, and diesel
- Construction output projected to contract 2.4% despite manufacturing expansion
