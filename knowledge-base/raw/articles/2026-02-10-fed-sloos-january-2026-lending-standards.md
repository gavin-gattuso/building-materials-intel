# Fed January 2026 SLOOS: Banks Expect to Tighten Construction Lending Standards

## Survey Overview
- January 2026 Senior Loan Officer Opinion Survey (SLOOS)
- Covers changes in Q4 2025 lending standards, terms, and demand
- 60 domestic banks + 18 U.S. branches of foreign banks responded
- Survey received December 10, 2025; responses due January 2, 2026

## Construction Lending Key Findings
- Banks expect to tighten standards for construction and land development loans in 2026
- Modest net shares reported expecting tighter standards
- Construction and land development was an exception to the broader trend of unchanged standards
- Reflects cautious approach to construction lending market

## Broader Commercial Real Estate
- Banks reported generally unchanged standards for CRE loans in recent period
- Stronger demand for CRE loans reported
- 93% of large banks expect CRE standards to hold steady or ease further in 2026
- Demand expected to strengthen across all loan categories

## Structural Shifts in Construction Lending
- Traditional bank lending undergoing structural shift
- More construction/development capital flowing through nonbank financial intermediaries
- Nonbanks now play larger role in originating and servicing construction and CRE loans
- Underwriting standards tightening for 2026 refinancings across industry
- Lenders recalibrating debt service tests for higher rates and stricter assumptions

## Implications for Building Materials Sector
- Tighter construction lending could constrain new project starts
- However, IIJA-funded public works projects less dependent on commercial lending
- Data center and industrial construction backed by corporate capital less affected
- Residential construction most exposed to lending standard changes
- "Sorting year" for commercial real estate lending (banks, CMBS, private credit)
