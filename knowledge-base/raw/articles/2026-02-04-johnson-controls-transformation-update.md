# Johnson Controls Transformation: Post-Divestiture Pure-Play Commercial Building Focus

## Key Details
- Completed divestiture of residential and light commercial HVAC businesses to Bosch (valued at $8.1 billion total, JCI portion ~$6.7 billion)
- Also divested Air Distribution Technologies business
- Divestitures represented approximately 20% of pre-transaction sales
- Post-divestiture portfolio: ~60% commercial HVAC, ~40% fire and security
- Now a pure-play provider of comprehensive solutions for commercial buildings
- 1,000+ employees engaged in business system transformation (kaizens, GembaWalks)
- 80+ kaizens completed, 350 senior leaders trained
- Focus areas: AI data centers, building automation, service/aftermarket growth

## Analysis
- Johnson Controls has arguably undergone the most dramatic transformation in building materials over the past two years. By shedding residential HVAC (to Bosch for $8.1B) and focusing on commercial buildings, JCI has repositioned itself as a higher-growth, higher-margin pure-play. The internal transformation (kaizen culture, GembaWalks) represents the operational side of this strategic shift. The result is visible in Q1 FY2026's 40% EPS growth and 190 bps margin expansion. This is a company that is fundamentally different from what it was two years ago.
