# Global Cement Demand to Remain Stable in 2026 as Surplus Emerges

## Key Details
- **Global cement demand**: Broadly stable in 2026; capacity anticipated to be in surplus
- **U.S. cement prices (Q1 2026)**: 96 USD/MT in March
- **Germany cement prices**: 250 USD/MT in March 2026
- **UK cement prices**: 140 USD/MT in March 2026
- **U.S. cement price forecast**: 3.9% rise to $169/MT (one source); only 0.6% rise to $163.9/MT (IBISWorld)
- **European price expectations**: 3-4% increases in most markets
- **U.S. expectations**: Mid-single-digit increases for 2026
- **U.S. cement market value**: Expected to reach $15.22 billion, growing 4.5% annually through 2029
- **Key drivers**: IIJA-funded highway, bridge, port, and broadband projects
- **Headwinds**: High mortgage rates constraining residential construction; China property sector decline

## Analysis
- The divergence between stable global demand and capacity surplus suggests pricing pressure in international markets
- The U.S. market is relatively insulated due to tariffs and strong infrastructure spending
- Mid-single-digit U.S. price increases benefit domestic producers like CRH, Martin Marietta, and Vulcan Materials
- European price increases of 3-4% support Holcim and Heidelberg Materials but are modest compared to cost inflation
- The surplus globally could create export pressure from Asian producers, though tariffs limit U.S. market impact
- The expiration of the IIJA at the end of September 2026 is the single biggest risk to the positive demand outlook
