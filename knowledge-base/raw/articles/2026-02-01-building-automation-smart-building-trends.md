# Building Automation Market 2026: $122.5 Billion Growing to $191 Billion by 2030

## Key Details
- Intelligent Building Automation Technologies Market: $122.49 billion (2026) to $191.14 billion (2030), 11.8% CAGR
- Five global trends reshaping building automation (per Johnson Controls):
  1. AI-powered optimization shifting from insights to automated operational decisions
  2. Agentic AI synthesizing millions of data points for dynamic controls
  3. Occupant-centric design with mobile access, responsive environments, personalized settings
  4. Integrated platforms connecting HVAC, lighting, security, and fire systems
  5. Energy management via smart HVAC adjusting ventilation based on occupancy and CO2 levels
- Key drivers: rising energy costs, stricter regulations, aging infrastructure, workforce shortages
- Next-generation BAS (Building Automation Systems) reduce complexity and improve performance
- Predictive maintenance and fault detection reducing downtime and operating costs

## Analysis
- The building automation market at $122.5B is already massive and growing at nearly 12% annually. Johnson Controls is uniquely positioned across all five trends given its portfolio of HVAC, fire, security, and controls. The shift toward agentic AI in buildings represents a paradigm change -- buildings that actively optimize themselves rather than waiting for manual adjustments. For building materials companies, the automation trend increases the value of connected products (smart locks from ASSA ABLOY/Fortune Brands, smart water monitors from Masco/Fortune Brands) that integrate into building management systems.
