# CEMEX Completes Panama Divestiture for ~$200 Million; Continues U.S. Aggregates Pivot

## Key Details
- **Operations sold**: CEMEX's Panama operations
- **Buyer**: Grupo Estrella
- **Enterprise value**: ~US$200 million
- **Valuation multiple**: ~12x adjusted EBITDA (trailing 12 months ended August 31, 2025)
- **Part of broader strategy**: Systematic divestiture of non-core Latin American assets to fund U.S. aggregates expansion
- **Total divestitures**: ~US$2.2 billion across Panama, Colombia, and other markets
- **Acquisition strategy**: Targeting 100 family-owned U.S. aggregates bolt-on acquisitions

## Analysis
- The 12x EBITDA multiple achieved for Panama assets is a premium valuation, reflecting competitive bidding for cement assets in Central America
- CEMEX is executing a clear portfolio transformation: selling LatAm cement assets at premium multiples and redeploying into U.S. aggregates
- The $2.2 billion divestiture total provides significant firepower for the planned 100 bolt-on U.S. acquisitions
- This strategy mirrors what Martin Marietta and Vulcan Materials have done: concentrating on aggregates in the most attractive U.S. growth markets
- The risk is that CEMEX may be selling growing LatAm assets to buy into a more competitive U.S. market at higher valuations
- However, the U.S. aggregates market's structural advantages (barriers to entry, pricing power, IIJA tailwind) justify the strategic pivot
