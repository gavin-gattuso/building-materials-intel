# Weyerhaeuser Unveils 2030 Growth Strategy Targeting $1.5 Billion in Incremental Adjusted EBITDA

## Key Details
- Target: $1.5 billion incremental Adjusted EBITDA by 2030 vs. 2024 baseline
- $1 billion of identified, largely controllable growth initiatives
- Key growth levers: increased harvest, export and product pricing uplift, expanded distribution and engineered wood capacity, real estate development, climate solutions, AI-driven operational improvements
- $500 million investment in new TimberStrand facility near Monticello/Warren, Arkansas
- Arkansas facility: ~10 million cubic feet annual capacity, doubles North American TimberStrand capacity
- Facility expected to generate $100M+ annual Adjusted EBITDA at full capacity
- Construction begins 2025; operations targeted for 2027
- ~200 new jobs in the Monticello area
- Southern yellow pine feedstock with biomass-fueled cogeneration system
- Full-year 2026 Strategic Land Solutions Adjusted EBITDA target: ~$425 million
- Shareholder return target: 75-80% of annual Adjusted FAD

## Analysis
- The $1.5B EBITDA growth target is ambitious but achievable given the diversified nature of growth drivers across timberlands, wood products, real estate, and climate solutions.
- The $500M Arkansas TimberStrand investment is the centerpiece capital project, representing a bet on engineered wood products gaining market share from traditional lumber and steel in construction.
- Climate solutions (carbon credits, renewable energy) and AI-driven operational improvements are emerging themes that differentiate Weyerhaeuser from pure commodity lumber producers.
- The 75-80% FAD return target provides significant shareholder returns while still funding substantial growth capex.
