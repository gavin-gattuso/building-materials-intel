# Daikin Reports Q3 FY2026: Revenue Up 2%, Operating Profit Down 3.4%, Raises Sales Forecast

## Key Details
- Nine months (April-December 2025) revenue of JPY 3.666 trillion, up 2.0% YoY
- Operating profit of JPY 307.9 billion, down 3.4%
- Net profit attributable to owners of JPY 195.4 billion, up 4.6%
- Q1 EPS rose to JPY 278.43 from JPY 215.53 YoY
- Full-year FY2026 revised forecast: net sales JPY 4.920 trillion (raised 1.7% from prior guidance)
- Operating profit forecast lowered to JPY 413.0 billion (down 5.1% from prior)
- Full-year EPS forecast: JPY 928.95
- Dividend forecast unchanged at JPY 330 per share
- US tariff direct impact on operating profit: JPY 18 billion (absorbed through price increases and cost reductions)
- Initially estimated JPY 47 billion ($320M) impact, now recalculated slightly lower
- Tariff structure: 54% on Chinese goods, 30-35% on Mexico/Canada parts
- Daikin implementing up to 7% price increases on select products
- Ductless, VRV, and light commercial products specifically impacted
- Opening two new manufacturing facilities in 2026

## Analysis
- Daikin's results show the strain of tariffs on a Japan-headquartered manufacturer with significant US exposure through Daikin North America. The operating profit decline despite revenue growth reflects tariff absorption costs. However, the raised sales forecast suggests confidence in demand. The new manufacturing facilities signal commitment to localizing production, which should reduce tariff exposure over time. Daikin's position as the world's largest HVAC company gives it pricing power to pass through cost increases.
