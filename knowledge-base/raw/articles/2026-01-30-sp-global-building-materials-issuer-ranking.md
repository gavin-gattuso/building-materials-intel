# S&P Global Issuer Ranking: Global Building Materials -- Strongest to Weakest (January 2026)

## Key Data Points
- S&P Global ranks its universe of rated building materials companies by rating, outlook, stand-alone credit profile (SACP), business risk, financial risk, and liquidity
- **14% of rating outlooks are negative** (vs. 7% a year ago -- doubled)
- Approximately **70% of negative outlooks originate from U.S.-based companies**
- Negative outlooks reflect high debt service costs on cash flows and margin pressure from subdued demand

## Sector-Wide Assessment
- Building materials sector entered 2026 with mixed credit fundamentals
- Tariffs and trade policy uncertainty creating margin pressure
- Iran conflict energy shock adding to cost headwinds
- Infrastructure spending (IIJA) provides demand floor for aggregates and cement
- Data center construction remains a bright spot

## Key Rating Factors
- **Leverage:** Companies that reduced debt during the 2021-2023 recovery cycle are better positioned
- **Margins:** EBITDA margins under pressure from rising input costs (energy, transportation, raw materials)
- **Liquidity:** Most investment-grade issuers maintain adequate liquidity buffers
- **M&A:** Active acquisition strategies (CRH, Holcim) consuming cash but building scale

## Construction Industry Implications
- The doubling of negative outlooks (7% to 14%) signals growing credit stress in the sector
- U.S. companies most affected, reflecting tariff exposure and interest rate sensitivity
- Investment-grade companies with infrastructure exposure remain well-positioned
- Speculative-grade issuers face refinancing risk as debt matures in a higher-rate environment
