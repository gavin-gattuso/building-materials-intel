# S&P Global Industry Credit Outlook 2026: Building Materials

## Overall Assessment
- Modest recovery in building materials expected, skewed toward second half of 2026
- Most regions assuming steady rebound in residential markets
- Some pickup in renovation expected alongside lagging new build activity
- Global credit conditions expected to remain resilient in 2026

## Regional Dynamics
- U.S.: High home prices and higher-than-expected mortgage rates pricing many buyers out
- China: Primary property sales expected to decline further amid soft demand
- Europe: Gradual residential recovery expected
- Overall recovery pattern uneven across geographies

## Key Credit Themes
- Economic growth holding up, supported by tech investments
- Active refinancing in 2025 pushed out maturities for many borrowers
- Policy interest rates decreasing
- Building materials issuers generally well-positioned from a liquidity standpoint

## Risks to Outlook
- Tariff escalation increasing cost pressures
- Geopolitical uncertainty (Iran conflict)
- Persistent affordability challenges in residential markets
- Rising input costs squeezing margins for some producers
- Potential for further mortgage rate volatility

## Sector-Specific Observations
- Aggregates producers showing strong pricing discipline and margin expansion
- Building products distributors facing mixed demand signals
- Insulation and roofing segments benefiting from building code upgrades
- Data center and infrastructure construction providing nonresidential demand support
