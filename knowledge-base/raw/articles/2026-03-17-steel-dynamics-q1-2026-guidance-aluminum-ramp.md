# Steel Dynamics Guides Q1 2026 EPS to $2.73-$2.77; Aluminum Mill Ramp Accelerates

## Key Details
- **Q1 2026 EPS Guidance**: $2.73 to $2.77 per diluted share
- **Q4 2025 Comparison**: $1.82/share (50% sequential increase)
- **Q1 2025 Comparison**: $1.44/share (90% year-over-year increase)
- **Steel Operations**: Meaningfully higher profitability driven by increased shipments and metal margin expansion
- **Selling Prices**: Average realized selling values increased more than scrap raw material costs
- **Columbus Aluminum Mill**: Ramp-up accelerating; company now expects to exit 2026 at 90% capacity utilization (previously 75%)
- **Aluminum Mill Capacity**: 650,000 metric tonnes annual capacity
- **Aluminum Investment**: $2.7 billion total investment (including satellite slab centers)
- **Product Qualifications**: Secured in industrial, beverage can, and automotive markets
- **Through-Cycle EBITDA**: Investments in aluminum, Sinton, and flat-rolled coating lines associated with ~$1.4 billion of through-cycle EBITDA capability
- **Working Capital Note**: Temporary buyback slowdown due to ~$126 million profit-sharing payment and elevated working capital from aluminum ramp

## Analysis
- The 90% exit-rate target for the aluminum mill (up from 75%) is a significant positive development, suggesting commissioning is proceeding more smoothly than steel mill start-ups
- Q1 2026 guidance represents one of the strongest sequential improvements in Steel Dynamics' history, driven by the domestic steel price recovery above $1,000/ton
- The aluminum platform diversifies STLD beyond steel and positions it in the growing aluminum flat-rolled market for beverages, automotive, and industrial applications
- With $1.4 billion in through-cycle EBITDA capability from recent investments, Steel Dynamics is building a structurally higher earnings base
