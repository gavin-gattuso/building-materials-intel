# Consumer Confidence March 2026: Edges Up 0.8 Points to 91.8

## Key Details
- Consumer Confidence Index: **91.8** in March (up from 91.0 in February)
- Month-over-month change: **+0.8 points**
- Present Situation Index: **123.3** (up 4.6 points from February)
- Expectations Index: **70.9** (down 1.7 points from February)
- Expectations Index remains below the **80-point recession warning threshold** for the third consecutive month

## Q1 2026 Consumer Confidence Trajectory
| Month | CCI | Present Situation | Expectations |
|-------|-----|-------------------|--------------|
| January | 89.0 (revised) | 113.7 | 65.1 |
| February | 91.2 | 120.0 | 72.0 |
| March | 91.8 | 123.3 | 70.9 |

## Key Divergence
- Present Situation Index improving (+9.6 points from January to March): consumers feel current conditions are okay
- Expectations Index declining from February peak: consumers are worried about the future
- The divergence widened in March, possibly reflecting growing concerns about the Iran conflict, tariffs, and potential recession

## Analysis
- Three consecutive months with the Expectations Index below 80 is a persistent recession warning signal
- The improving Present Situation Index provides a partial offset -- consumers are spending now even as they worry about the future
- For building materials: the "spend now, worry later" pattern may support near-term renovation demand but signals potential pullback ahead
- The Iran conflict (which escalated in March) and rising gas prices are likely factors in the declining expectations
- The dual headwinds of consumer pessimism about the future and rising mortgage rates create a challenging environment for housing demand
