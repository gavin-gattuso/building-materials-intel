# Construction Dive March 2026 Economic Roundup: What Early 2026 Data Shows for the Construction Industry

## Construction Spending (January 2026 data)

- Nonresidential construction: seasonally adjusted annual rate of **$728.2 billion**, **0.4% below** revised December estimate
- Commercial construction spending slipped
- Public sector lifting nonresidential construction spending as private projects lag

## Nonresidential Outlook

- Nonresidential construction faces slowing growth, widening sector imbalances, and continued economic uncertainty
- Nonresidential building spending expected to remain sluggish through 2026
- Data center construction is the standout bright spot, driving manufacturing construction numbers

## Key Economic Indicators for Construction

- Input prices surged at **12.6% annualized rate** in first two months of 2026
- Contractor backlog declining, signaling potential slowdown ahead
- Public sector construction buoyed by IIJA spending reaching peak disbursement

## Sector Breakdown

### Strong Sectors
- Data centers and technology-related construction
- Infrastructure (roads, bridges, water) via IIJA funding
- Public sector construction

### Weak Sectors
- Office construction (continued post-pandemic adjustment)
- Certain commercial segments
- Residential starts face headwinds from affordability constraints

## Employment Data

- Construction added jobs but faces structural challenges
- 349,000 net new workers needed in 2026 (down from prior years)
- More than half needed to replace retirees rather than support growth
- Wage growth of 8-12% projected for construction sector
