# Construction Material Prices Jump 12.6% in Early 2026 Amid Energy Spike

## Key Statistics
- Annualized rate (Jan-Feb 2026): 12.6% increase in construction input prices
- Monthly increase (February): 1.3% for nonresidential construction input prices
- Year-over-year: 3.7% increase vs. February 2025
- Year-to-date: 3.1% since February 2025

## Energy Cost Increases
- Natural gas: +10.9% month-over-month (February)
- Unprocessed energy materials: +6%
- Crude petroleum: +4.7%
- Oil prices: Near $100/barrel (attributed to Iran conflict)

## Other Material Cost Increases
- Copper: Notable gains
- Lumber: Notable gains
- Steel: Notable gains

## Key Quotes
- Anirban Basu: "That will put upward pressure on construction materials prices directly by raising diesel prices and, indirectly, by raising shipping costs."
- Ken Simonson (AGC): "Disruption of oil, natural gas, and aluminum supplies from the Middle East is pushing up construction costs further."
- Jeffrey Shoaf: "Reducing uncertainty around tariffs and stabilizing supply chains would help contractors keep projects moving forward."

## Industry Sentiment
- Fewer than 1 in 4 contractors expect profit margin shrinkage over next six months
- Owners beginning to delay projects due to rising costs
