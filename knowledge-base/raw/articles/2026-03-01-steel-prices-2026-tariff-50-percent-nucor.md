# Steel Prices Break $1,000/Ton as 50% Tariffs Reshape Market

## Key Details
- Hot-rolled coil (HRC) domestic price: **$1,005 per short ton** (SMU average, March 2026)
- Above the critical **$1,000/ton threshold** for the first time in two years
- Nucor raised consumer spot price (CSP) by **$10/ton** effective March 23, 2026
- Prior to the increase, Nucor had held at **$950/ton** through Q1
- Section 232 tariffs on steel: **50%** for all trading partners except UK (25%)
- Tariffs doubled from 25% to 50% in **June 2025**

## Tariff Structure
- **50% tariff** on all steel imports (Section 232, doubled June 2025)
- UK exception: maintained at **25%**
- Spread between US HR prices and imports remains wide and widening
- Import competition compressed to historically low levels

## Price Drivers
- 50% tariff wall creating sheltered domestic pricing environment
- Surging **data center construction demand** supporting steel consumption
- Scheduled mill maintenance outages constraining near-term supply
- Compressed import competition removing traditional price ceiling

## Construction Impact
- Steel-intensive commercial and industrial projects facing 13%+ year-over-year cost increases
- Contractors report **10-15% budget overruns** on projects with significant steel components
- Structural steel, rebar, metal decking all reflecting the elevated pricing environment
- Data center construction is the single largest new demand driver

## Analysis
- The 50% tariff has fundamentally restructured the US steel market, creating sustained domestic pricing power
- For building materials companies: steel producers (Nucor, SDI, USS) benefit from pricing power; downstream fabricators and contractors face margin pressure
- The $1,000+/ton HRC price adds approximately **$3,000-5,000** to the cost of a typical residential home and significantly more for commercial structures
- Data center buildout is expected to accelerate in 2026, providing continued steel demand support independent of traditional construction cycles
