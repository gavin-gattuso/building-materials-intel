# January FOMC: Fed Holds Rates at 3.50-3.75% with Two Dissents

## Key Details
- Federal funds rate maintained at **3.50% - 3.75%**
- **Two dissents:** Governors Stephen Miran and Christopher Waller voted for a quarter-point cut
- This was Miran's **fourth consecutive dissent** (had previously advocated for a deeper half-point cut)
- The hold followed three consecutive cuts in late 2025 (September, November, December) totaling **75 basis points**
- No Summary of Economic Projections released at this meeting

## Statement Changes
- Statement noted "economic activity has been expanding at a solid pace"
- "Job gains have remained low, and the unemployment rate has shown some signs of stabilization"
- "Inflation remains somewhat elevated"
- Removed clause indicating higher risk from weakening labor market vs. heightened inflation -- signaling a more balanced risk assessment

## Forward Guidance
- Markets expected the Fed to wait until at least **June 2026** before the next rate adjustment
- The two dissents highlighted a growing divide within the committee
- CME FedWatch priced in ~60% probability of a hold through the March meeting

## Analysis
- The two dissents are notable -- a growing minority sees the economy weakening enough to warrant further cuts
- For construction: the rate hold keeps the fed funds rate well above the neutral rate estimated at ~3%, maintaining restrictive monetary policy
- Mortgage rates at ~6.1% at the time of the January meeting continue to constrain housing demand
- The pause signals the Fed is data-dependent and cautious about declaring victory over inflation
- Building materials companies should plan for sustained higher rates through at least mid-2026
