# Building Materials Tariff Impact 2026: Steel at 50%, Construction Input Prices Surge

## Key Details
- Steel and aluminum face 50% tariffs on all imports; copper faces 50% tariff on semi-finished products
- Nonresidential construction input prices rose at 7.1% annualized rate in January 2026
- Doors, windows, and frames: costs rising from Canadian and Chinese sources
- 20% of door locks/viewers from China -- 10% price jump expected
- HVAC systems: heating/cooling units from Mexico facing 6% increase; thermostats up 8%
- Plumbing: Chinese-dominated supply chain seeing 4-13% increases (toilets/sinks +13%, water heaters +9%)
- Windows/exterior doors from Canada: relatively modest 1% cost increase
- ASSA ABLOY implementing 6.25% tariff surcharge on US products
- Masco estimates $200M total tariff exposure; Fortune Brands at $151M
- Daikin facing JPY 18 billion operating profit impact
- Company-specific mitigations: sourcing diversification, selective pricing, cost reduction

## Analysis
- Tariffs are the single largest cost headwind facing building materials companies in 2026. The 50% tariffs on steel, aluminum, and copper affect virtually every building product category. Companies with diversified manufacturing footprints (ASSA ABLOY, Masco) are better positioned than import-dependent competitors. The divergent impact across categories -- modest for Canadian windows, severe for Chinese plumbing fixtures -- creates winners and losers within the sector. Companies that started supply chain de-risking early (Masco since 2018) have a structural advantage.
