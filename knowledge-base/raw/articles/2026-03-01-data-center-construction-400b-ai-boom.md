# Data Center Construction: The $400B+ AI Infrastructure Boom Reshaping Building Materials Demand

## Key Details
- Global AI-infrastructure spending forecast: **$400-450 billion** by 2026 (65% jump from 2024)
- Hyperscalers planning nearly **$700 billion** on data center projects in 2026
- **Amazon:** projecting **$200 billion** in 2026 capex (up from $131B in 2025)
- **Google:** estimating **$175-185 billion** (up from $91B in 2025)
- **Meta:** estimating **$115-135 billion** (up from $71B in 2025)
- Over **150 new hyperscale data centers** to be completed worldwide by end of 2026
- Over **23 GW** of data center capacity under construction globally (75% in the US)
- Moody's projects **$3 trillion** in global spending over the next five years

## Building Materials Impact
- Data centers are **extremely copper-intensive** for power distribution systems
- **Steel:** structural steel, metal decking, and rebar for massive floor loads
- **Concrete:** thick reinforced foundations to support server equipment weight
- **Electrical equipment:** transformers, switchgear, cabling -- all tariff-affected
- **Cooling systems:** HVAC, piping, specialized thermal management systems
- AI data centers consume **10-15x more energy** than conventional ones

## Construction Workforce Impact
- Demand for electricians with precision wiring skills has surged
- Nearly **one-fifth of electricians** are over age 55 -- retirement risk
- Data center construction is absorbing skilled labor from other construction sectors
- Specialty contractors commanding premium rates

## Key Challenge
- US AI data center expansion relies on **Chinese electrical equipment imports** (Bloomberg, April 2026)
- Tariffs on electrical components could constrain build-out pace

## Analysis
- Data center construction is the single brightest spot in the US construction landscape in 2026
- It is driving demand for copper, steel, concrete, and electrical equipment at levels that offset weakness in other nonresidential sectors
- For building materials: companies exposed to electrical distribution, heavy concrete, and structural steel are best positioned to capture data center demand
- The labor absorption effect means data center construction is competing with traditional construction for the same limited pool of skilled workers, further tightening the labor market
- The reliance on Chinese electrical equipment is a vulnerability that tariffs could exploit, potentially slowing the buildout
