# ArcelorMittal Navigates Tariff Landscape with Calvert EAF and European Trade Protections

## Key Details
- **US Tariff Impact**: 50% Section 232 tariffs expected to cost ArcelorMittal approximately $100 million per quarter ($150 million total projected losses)
- **Calvert Acquisition**: Completed purchase of Nippon Steel's 50% stake in AM/NS Calvert (Alabama)
- **Calvert EAF Status**: New 1.5 million ton/year electric arc furnace produced first slabs in June 2025; expected to reach full capacity by mid-2026
- **"Melted and Poured" Compliance**: Approximately half of Calvert production meets U.S. "melted and poured" requirements, avoiding additional tariffs
- **Slab Supply Agreement**: Seven-year slab agreement signed with Nippon Steel and U.S. Steel
- **NOES Investment**: $1.2 billion investment announced in February 2025 to build non-grain-oriented electrical steel (NOES) facility at Calvert with capacity of 150,000 metric tonnes/year
- **Canadian Operations**: ArcelorMittal Dofasco reduced workforce and curtailed operations to ~50% production capacity
- **European Outlook**: Management expects EU tariffs and CBAM to restore European production levels and profitability in 2026
- **North America EBITDA Share**: Approximately 40% of group EBITDA

## Analysis
- ArcelorMittal's dual strategy of expanding U.S. production capacity (Calvert EAF) while navigating tariff costs on imports demonstrates sophisticated trade policy management
- The Calvert investment positions ArcelorMittal to capture domestic U.S. margins while the NOES facility targets the high-growth EV and renewable energy markets
- Canadian operations are bearing the brunt of tariff impacts, with significant production curtailments
- The company's view that both U.S. and European trade protections will benefit it over time reflects confidence that tariff regimes are becoming structural rather than temporary
