# ABI February 2026: Billings Rise to 49.4, Signs of Stabilization

## Key Details
- AIA/Deltek Architecture Billings Index: **49.4** in February (up from 43.8 in January)
- A **5.6-point increase** -- the largest monthly gain in over a year
- Still below the 50 breakeven threshold, but approaching stabilization
- Project inquiries index: **52.3** (above 50, indicating growth in inquiries)
- Design contracts index: **48.0** (still contracting but rate of decline slowing)

## Regional Performance
- South: **50.0** (breakeven -- strongest region)
- West: **47.2**
- Midwest: **46.3**
- Northeast: **41.9** (weakest)

## Sector Performance
- Multifamily residential: **48.2**
- Institutional: **49.2**
- Commercial/industrial: **45.7**
- Mixed practice: **41.8**

## Forward Outlook (Q2 2026 Expectations)
- **48%** of firm leaders expect billings to remain about the same in Q2 vs. Q1
- **31%** expect billings to increase by 5% or more
- **21%** expect billings to decrease by 5% or more

## Analysis
- The 5.6-point jump from January is encouraging and suggests the prolonged contraction may be bottoming out
- The inquiries index above 50 (52.3) is a positive forward-looking signal -- more firms are seeing new project interest
- The South reaching 50.0 reflects infrastructure spending and data center activity in that region
- AIA Chief Economist Richard Branch noted the "broader economy continues to struggle, with unemployment increasing in February"
- For building materials: if the ABI stabilization holds, it signals nonresidential construction demand may stop deteriorating by late 2026/early 2027
- However, the Iran conflict and tariff uncertainty that emerged in March could reverse this tentative improvement
