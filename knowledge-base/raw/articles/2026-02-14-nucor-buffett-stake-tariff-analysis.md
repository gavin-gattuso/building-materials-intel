# Warren Buffett Takes Substantial Stake in Nucor Amid Tariff Uncertainty

## Key Details
- **Berkshire Hathaway**: Warren Buffett has taken a substantial stake in Nucor (NUE)
- **Investment Thesis**: Buffett signals confidence in Nucor's fundamentals despite potential tariff policy shifts
- **Tariff Context**: Since mid-2025, a 50% tariff on global steel imports has protected domestic prices
- **Policy Risk**: Reports on February 16, 2026 suggest the administration may exempt "derivative" steel products to combat consumer inflation
- **Import Share Decline**: Foreign import share of U.S. finished steel market dropped from ~25% to 16% in October 2025, estimated 14% in November
- **Apple Grove Mill**: $4 billion sheet mill in West Virginia expected operational by late 2026, targeting automotive and sustainable energy markets
- **Helion Energy**: $35 million investment in fusion power startup for 500 MW carbon-free electricity by 2030
- **Capital Plan**: $6.5 billion through 2027 targeting high-margin areas including green steel

## Analysis
- Buffett's entry into Nucor is a significant signal of value recognition in the U.S. steel sector
- The potential exemption of derivative steel products introduces downside risk to the tariff protection narrative, but Nucor's domestic EAF model insulates it more than integrated/imported-slab producers
- Nucor's Apple Grove facility represents a generational capacity addition that will expand the company's presence in high-margin flat-rolled steel for automotive and clean energy
- The combination of tariff protection, new capacity, and Buffett endorsement creates a compelling narrative for long-term investors
