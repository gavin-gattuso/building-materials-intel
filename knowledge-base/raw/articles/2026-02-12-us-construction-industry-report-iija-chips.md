# U.S. Construction Industry Report 2026: CHIPS Act, Data Centers & IIJA Reshape Investment

## IIJA Status (as of February 2026)
- Authorization expires September 30, 2026
- FY2026 spending bills rescinded over $2.3 billion in IIJA allocations
  - $879 million from National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) program
  - Core programs (highways, bridges, water infrastructure) survived
- Congressional committees beginning preliminary reauthorization work
- No reauthorization bill introduced yet
- Agencies pushing to obligate remaining allocations before expiration

## Key IIJA Funding Areas
- Road/bridge repair: $110 billion
- Water infrastructure: $55 billion
- Total Federal highway programs: ~$350 billion over FY2022-2026
- 40% year-over-year increase in "Heavy Civil" solicitations compared to 2024

## Transportation Construction
- U.S. transportation construction market expected to grow nearly 3% to record $209.1 billion in 2026

## Data Center Construction
- Projected to grow another 20% in 2026, driven by AI demand
- Booming data center construction supporting deal flow in building products M&A
- AI-driven investment continuing to provide momentum

## CHIPS Act Impact
- Megaproject spending from CHIPS Act redefining construction investment patterns
- Semiconductor fabrication facilities creating massive construction demand
- Combined with IIJA, creating sustained nonresidential construction pipeline

## Forward Outlook
- Reauthorization fight ahead with seven months to IIJA expiration
- Peak execution phase creating urgency for state and local agencies
- Infrastructure investment expected to remain elevated through at least 2028
