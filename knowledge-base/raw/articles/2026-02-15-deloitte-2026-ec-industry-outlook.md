# Deloitte 2026 Engineering and Construction Industry Outlook

## Overall Outlook
- Cautiously optimistic for commercial construction in 2026
- Data center and energy infrastructure expansion providing continued momentum
- Investment in structures projected to pivot from 2025 decline to modest growth (+1.8%) in 2026

## Data Center Construction
- Projected to grow another 20% in 2026
- Driven by artificial intelligence demand
- AI-related outlays continuing to support E&C work
- Segment growth led by AI-driven data center construction and associated energy infrastructure

## Labor Challenges
- Industry needs roughly 499,000 new professionals by 2026
- Large portions of workforce nearing retirement
- Rising material costs and persistent labor shortages are primary headwinds
- Industry enters 2026 confronting shifting project demand patterns

## Technology Transformation
- Construction robotics market: $1.30 billion in 2026, projected to reach $11.14 billion by 2040
- 3D concrete printing moving from novelty to scalable solution (up to 50% faster builds)
- Digital Project Delivery becoming contractually expected standard
- Firms must transition to digitally-enabled ecosystems
- Agentic AI, BIM, and robotics are key enabling technologies

## Key Growth Areas
- Data centers: 20% growth
- Energy infrastructure: significant expansion
- Infrastructure (IIJA): peak execution year
- Industrial/manufacturing (CHIPS Act): megaproject pipeline

## Key Risks
- Rising material costs (12.6% annualized in early 2026)
- Persistent labor shortages
- Tariff uncertainty
- Geopolitical risks (Iran conflict)
- Residential market weakness
