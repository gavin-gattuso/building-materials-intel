# NAHB HMI April 2026: Builder Confidence Edges Up to 40, Still Deeply Negative

## Key Details
- NAHB/Wells Fargo Housing Market Index: **40** in April (up from 38 in March)
- Above economists' expectation of **38**
- Still well below the **50** breakeven threshold
- **29% of builders** cut home prices in April (unchanged from March)
- Average price reduction: **5%**
- **61% of builders** used sales incentives (up from 59% in March)

## Tariff Impact on Builders
- **60% of builders** said suppliers have already hiked prices or plan to due to tariffs
- Average supplier price increase: **6.3%** in response to tariffs
- Estimated additional cost per new home: **$10,900**
- Tariffs making it "difficult for builders to accurately price homes and make critical business decisions"

## Analysis
- The HMI has been below 50 for every month since mid-2024, reflecting a prolonged period of poor builder sentiment
- The April reading of 40 shows modest improvement but is still historically weak -- comparable to recession-era readings
- The combination of 6.46% mortgage rates, tariff-driven cost increases ($10,900/home), and geopolitical uncertainty is keeping builders cautious
- The heavy use of incentives (61%) and price cuts (29%) shows builders are sacrificing margin to maintain volume
- For building materials: the incentive-heavy environment means builders are squeezing costs aggressively, putting pressure on material suppliers' pricing power
- Any sustained improvement in the HMI would require either meaningfully lower mortgage rates or a resolution of tariff uncertainty -- neither appears imminent
