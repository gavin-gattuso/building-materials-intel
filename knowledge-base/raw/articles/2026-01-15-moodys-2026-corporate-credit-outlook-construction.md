# Moody's 2026 Corporate Credit Outlook: Construction Sector Faces Headwinds

## Key Data Points
- Global GDP projected at **2.5-2.6%** in 2026-2027
- Advanced economies growth: approximately **1.5%**
- Emerging markets growth: approximately **4%** (led by India)
- Volatile U.S. trade policies (tariffs) identified as primary risk to construction sector credit

## Six Key Credit Risks for 2026
1. **Tariff uncertainty:** Companies struggling to pass on higher costs to cautious consumers
2. **Geopolitical disruption:** Iran conflict driving energy cost inflation
3. **Interest rate persistence:** Higher-for-longer rates pressuring leveraged issuers
4. **Labor market softening:** Potential demand weakness if unemployment rises
5. **Refinancing risk:** $1T+ in corporate debt maturing in 2026-2027
6. **Policy volatility:** Federal spending cutbacks and regulatory uncertainty

## Construction Sector Specifics
- Infrastructure spending (IIJA/IRA/CHIPS) provides a floor for demand
- Data center construction remains a structural growth driver
- Residential construction constrained by affordability and high mortgage rates
- Commercial real estate faces ongoing stress from office market weakness
- Building materials companies with investment-grade ratings have manageable refinancing profiles

## Construction Industry Implications
- Moody's 1.5% advanced economy growth forecast suggests limited upside for construction demand
- Tariff pass-through ability separates winners from losers -- companies with pricing power (aggregates, specialty products) fare better
- Refinancing risk concentrated in speculative-grade building products companies
- The combination of tariffs, energy costs, and interest rates creates a challenging margin environment for 2026
