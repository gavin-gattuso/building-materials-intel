# 50% Copper Tariff Drives Up MEP Costs Across Construction

## Key Details
- Copper products subject to **50% tariff** since August 2025
- Updated rules effective **April 6, 2026**: goods made almost entirely of copper face 50% tariff on full item value
- StoneX forecast: copper prices averaging **$11,490/mt** for 2026
- US copper prices elevated **above $5,200/metric ton** for domestic consumers
- Construction material costs up **4-6%** overall due to tariffs on steel, aluminum, and copper combined

## Construction Applications Affected
- Copper tubing and plumbing fixtures
- Electrical wiring (residential and commercial)
- HVAC components
- Switchgear and electrical distribution panels
- Building automation and controls

## Industry Impact
- Copper drives MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) costs across virtually every project
- Contractors report switchgear and electrical panel quotes exceeding budgets by **10-15%**
- Data center projects -- which are extremely copper-intensive for power distribution -- face acute cost pressure
- Nonresidential construction input prices surged at **7.1% annualized rate** in January, with copper wire as a key driver

## Analysis
- Unlike steel and aluminum, the US has limited domestic copper production capacity, making tariff-driven price increases harder to mitigate through domestic sourcing
- The April 6, 2026 rule change expands the tariff to finished copper products, not just raw copper, broadening the cost impact
- For building materials companies: copper product distributors and electrical component manufacturers face pricing pass-through challenges
- The electrical intensity of data center construction means copper demand will remain elevated even if traditional construction slows
- Copper tariffs compound the challenge for builders already facing steel (+50%) and aluminum (+50%) tariffs
