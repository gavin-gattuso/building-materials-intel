# Fortune Brands CEO Shakeup: Banati Steps Aside After Activist Ed Garden Joins Board

## Key Details
- March 16, 2026: Amit Banati decided to step aside and will not assume CEO role in May
- Banati also stepped down from the Board
- Banati to receive $18.4 million despite never officially working a day as CEO
- Ed Garden (Garden Investment Management) appointed as Class I director effective March 16, 2026
- Garden is son-in-law of activist investor Nelson Peltz
- David Barry (President of Security and Connected Products) named Interim CEO, effective immediately
- CFO Jonathan Baksht also stepped down; Ashley George appointed Interim CFO
- Comprehensive CEO search launched
- Fortune Brands will seek shareholder approval to declassify its board (move to annual elections)
- Garden agreed to cap stake below 9.9% and withdraw board nominees
- Garden will join Nominating/Governance and Compensation committees

## Analysis
- This is one of the most dramatic corporate governance stories in building materials in 2026. The activist-driven leadership overhaul at FBIN -- resulting in both the CEO-designate and CFO departing within weeks -- creates significant uncertainty. However, Ed Garden's track record at Trian Partners (Nelson Peltz's firm) suggests a focus on margin improvement, capital allocation discipline, and potential portfolio changes. The board declassification is a positive governance development. The interim leadership period will be closely watched by investors.
