# NAHB: Building Material Price Growth Remains Elevated Despite a Sluggish Market

## Residential Construction Input Prices (November 2025 Data)

- Price index for inputs to new residential construction rose **0.1%** in November
- Up **4.2%** from a year ago
- Price growth for residential construction inputs remained above **3%** since June 2025

## Material-Specific Trends

### Rising Prices
- **Metal molding and trim**: up **48.2% YoY** in November -- largest single-category increase
- Metal products broadly continue to experience significant price increases
- Final demand energy: jumped **4.6%** for the month of November

### Declining Prices
- **Softwood lumber**: down **8.0% YoY** in November
- **Ready-mix concrete**: down **0.7% YoY**
- These declines reflect stagnation in construction spending rather than tariff relief

## Final Demand PPI

- PPI for final demand rose **0.2%** in November, following 0.1% increase in October
- Energy component was primary driver of the monthly increase

## Market Context

- Residential building material price growth continued climbing toward end of 2025 even as new home construction market showed signs of slowing
- The bifurcation between tariff-affected metals (rising sharply) and non-tariffed materials (stable/declining) is creating complex cost forecasting challenges for builders
- Freddie Mac estimates a housing shortage of **3.7 million units**, providing a structural demand floor despite near-term softness
