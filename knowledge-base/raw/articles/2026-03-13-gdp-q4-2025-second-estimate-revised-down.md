# Q4 2025 GDP Second Estimate Revised Down Sharply to 0.7%

## Key Details
- Real GDP revised down to **0.7% annualized rate** in Q4 2025 (second estimate)
- Original advance estimate was **1.4%** -- a significant downward revision of 0.7 percentage points
- This is the weakest quarterly growth since early 2022
- Q3 2025 had been **4.4%**, making the Q4 slowdown even more pronounced
- Consumer spending revised slightly lower
- Business investment data mixed; equipment spending softened

## Revision Details
- The downward revision reflected updated data on consumer spending, trade, and inventories
- Government spending remained a drag on Q4 growth
- Third and final estimate expected April 30, 2026

## Market Reaction
- Treasury yields fell on the weaker growth data, briefly supporting lower mortgage rates
- Stock markets showed mixed reaction as weak growth could support rate cuts but also signals demand weakness
- Fed futures market increased probability of a June 2026 rate cut

## Analysis
- Sub-1% GDP growth in Q4 2025 is concerning for the construction sector, which depends on broad economic expansion
- However, the Q4 weakness was partly driven by volatile components (trade, inventories) that may not persist
- Construction as a percentage of GDP remained at approximately **4.4%**, showing the sector's resilience
- For building materials, the key question is whether Q1 2026 rebounds or the slowdown deepens
- The Iran conflict beginning in March 2026 added a new headwind not captured in this Q4 data
