# Capstone Partners: Construction Services M&A Update -- February 2026

## Key Findings

- Construction M&A activity positioned to **remain robust** as sector participants pursue inorganic growth to bolster scale, market share, profit margins, and skilled workforce capacity
- Deal volume in construction services totaled **562 transactions** announced or closed in 2025, an **18.2% increase from 2024**
- Construction M&A activity expanded for the **third consecutive year** in 2025

## Growth Drivers for 2026

- Growing **data center and power infrastructure demand** expected to continue uplifting construction M&A activity and valuation strength throughout 2026
- Accelerating **AI power demand** anticipated to propel energy infrastructure construction
- While macroeconomic volatility, rising prices, and elevated mortgage rates are anticipated to keep both residential and nonresidential construction spending subdued through H1 2026, booming data center construction will likely continue uplifting sector backlogs

## Labor Market Challenges

- **53% of contractors** expect hiring to remain difficult or worsen in 2026
- **57%** cite insufficient supply of workers as top concern
- **56%** cite rising labor costs as top concern

## Consolidation Rationale

- Sector participants looked to offset rising input costs and skilled labor shortages through added scale
- Durable backlogs and increased consolidation activity supported 2025 deal expansion
