# Oil Prices Reach $111/Barrel as Strait of Hormuz Crisis Continues to Hammer Construction Costs

## Oil Price Timeline -- March 2026

| Date | Brent Crude Price | Event |
|------|------------------|-------|
| Jan 1, 2026 | ~$74/bbl | Pre-crisis baseline |
| Feb 28 | ~$78/bbl | Operation Epic Fury begins |
| Mar 4 | ~$89/bbl | Iran declares Strait closed |
| Mar 8 | $100/bbl | First time above $100 since 2022 |
| Mar ~15 | $126/bbl (peak) | Peak crisis pricing |
| Mar 30 | $111.10/bbl | End-of-month settlement |

- Full-month increase: approximately **50%** from start of 2026
- Peak surge: **47%** in less than a month

## IEA Oil Market Report -- March 2026

- The closure has been described as the largest disruption to energy supply since the 1970s
- Strait normally carries approximately **20%** of world's crude oil and LNG
- Also disrupted petrochemicals, fertilizer, and plastics raw materials

## Construction Cost Transmission

- Rising oil prices directly elevate:
  - Equipment fuel costs (diesel-powered machinery)
  - Asphalt and bituminous products prices
  - Material transportation costs
  - Petrochemical-derived products (PVC, insulation, adhesives)
- Construction input prices surging at **12.6% annualized rate**
- Oil shock creating cascading failures across multiple material supply chains
