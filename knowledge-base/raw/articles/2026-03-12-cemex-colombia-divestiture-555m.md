# CEMEX Divests Colombian Operations for $555 Million; Holcim Primary Buyer

## Key Details
- **Announcement date**: March 12, 2026
- **Total divestiture value**: ~US$555 million
- **Valuation multiple**: ~10x 2025 EBITDA
- **Primary buyer**: Holcim Group, acquiring cement plant (Caracolito), grinding mill (Santa Rosa), and portfolio of ready-mix concrete, aggregates, mortar, and admixture plants for US$485 million
- **Additional sales**: ~US$70 million in proceeds expected from remaining asset sales to other third parties
- **Retained assets**: Two cement plants (Maceo and Cucuta) with 1.6 million tons/year combined capacity, plus a grinding mill (Clemencia), ready-mix concrete plants, and aggregates quarries
- **Expected closing**: End of 2026, subject to regulatory approvals
- **Panama sale completed earlier**: Operations sold to Grupo Estrella for ~US$200M enterprise value (~12x adj. EBITDA)

## Analysis
- This is part of CEMEX's systematic portfolio rotation: divesting non-core Latin American assets to fund U.S. aggregates expansion
- The $2.2 billion in total divestitures (Colombia + Panama + other) is being recycled into a focused U.S. bolt-on acquisition strategy targeting 100 family-owned aggregates businesses
- Holcim is the primary beneficiary, expanding its Latin American footprint as CEMEX retreats, creating a competitive shift in the Colombian market
- The 10x EBITDA multiple for the Colombia assets is a premium valuation, reflecting the strategic value of these operations
- CEMEX retains a meaningful Colombia presence with 1.6 Mtpa of cement capacity, suggesting a selective rather than full exit
