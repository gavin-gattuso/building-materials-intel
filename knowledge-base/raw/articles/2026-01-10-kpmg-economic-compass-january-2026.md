# KPMG January 2026 Economic Compass: The Two Faces of the Economy

## GDP Outlook

- The Conference Board revised GDP growth **down by 0.1 percentage points** to **2.0% YoY** for 2026
- Economic growth expected to rebound to **2.2%** in 2026 (some forecasters)
- S&P Global forecasts stable global economic expansion of **3.2%** in 2026
- PCE inflation expected to increase to **2.7%** in 2026
- Unemployment rate expected to rise to **4.5%**

## The "Two Faces" Divergence

- Robust GDP growth and relatively low unemployment coexist with deeply pessimistic consumer sentiment
- Conference Board Consumer Confidence Index fell to **84.5** -- lowest since May 2014
- Expectations Index at **65.1** -- well below the 80 recession-signal threshold
- Gap between hard data (GDP, employment) and soft data (confidence, surveys) is historically wide

## Key Risk Factors

- Tariff-driven inflation pressures
- Geopolitical uncertainty
- Consumer pessimism on prices, inflation, food costs
- Housing affordability constraints
- Immigration policy impacts on labor markets

## Implications for Construction

- The two-speed economy creates a complex environment for construction planning
- Public-sector construction (IIJA, CHIPS) remains strong regardless of consumer sentiment
- Private-sector residential construction more vulnerable to consumer confidence shifts
- Commercial construction demand mixed: data centers booming, office struggling
- The divergence between hard and soft data makes forecasting unusually difficult
