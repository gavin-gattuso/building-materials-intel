# ConstructConnect: The State of the Construction Economy -- What to Expect in 2026

## Construction Spending Outlook

- Overall U.S. construction market expected to grow **5.6%** annually to reach **$1.27 trillion** in 2026
- Investment in structures expected to pivot from 2025 decline to modest growth of **~1.8%**
- Transportation construction market expected to grow nearly 3% to a record **$209.1 billion**

## Bidding and Project Pipeline

- 40% YoY increase in Heavy Civil solicitations vs 2024
- Data center and energy infrastructure projects driving a significant share of new project starts
- Commercial and institutional construction planning accelerating

## Labor Shortage Impact on Bidding

- Labor costs projected to increase 6-8% annually in 2026
- Shortage pushing contractors to bid higher, reducing competitive pressure in some markets
- Specialty trades (electricians, pipe fitters) commanding premium rates
- Some projects being deferred due to inability to staff them

## Material Cost Outlook

- Tariff-affected metals remaining elevated throughout 2026
- Non-tariffed materials (lumber, concrete) relatively stable
- Overall construction cost inflation driven primarily by tariffs, not underlying commodity movements
- Material volatility stabilized since 2023 peaks but risk remains in energy-intensive materials

## Key Themes

- Public infrastructure spending (IIJA peak phase) providing stable demand floor
- Private construction more uncertain due to interest rates, consumer confidence
- Technology adoption accelerating as firms seek productivity gains to offset cost/labor pressures
- Regional variation significant: Sun Belt markets showing more resilience than Midwest/Northeast
