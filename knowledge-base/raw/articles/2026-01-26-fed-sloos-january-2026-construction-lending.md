# Federal Reserve January 2026 Senior Loan Officer Opinion Survey (SLOOS)

## Key Findings (Q4 2025 Changes)

### Construction & Land Development Loans
- Banks reported **generally unchanged** standards for commercial real estate loans
- **Stronger demand** reported for commercial real estate loans
- Banks expect to **modestly tighten** standards for construction and land development loans over 2026
- Large banks reported net **easing** (-5.6%)
- Smaller banks still reported some **tightening** (+5.3%), but at a slowing pace

### Lending Spreads
- Construction loan spreads tightened to **237.5 basis points** by Q3 2025
- Down from a peak near **310 basis points** in late 2024
- Near the lowest level since **2021**

### Overall Credit Conditions
- Standards approaching **neutral** across the board
- Construction lending demand turned **positive** in Q4 2025, led by large banks
- Banks expect standards to remain basically unchanged for most loan categories over 2026
- Modest net shares expecting to ease standards for auto loans

## Implications for Building Products & Construction

- Improved lending conditions support new construction project starts
- Tighter spreads reduce financing costs, potentially improving project feasibility
- Large bank easing is a leading indicator for broader credit availability
- However, smaller bank caution reflects ongoing concerns about CRE credit quality, particularly office
- The divide between large and small bank lending posture suggests uneven access to construction financing
