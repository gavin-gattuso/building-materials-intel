# AGC Survey: Two-Thirds of Contractors Report Projects Postponed or Cancelled Due to Tariffs

## Key Details
- AGC/Sage survey: nearly **two-thirds (65%)** of contractors had at least one project postponed, scaled back, or cancelled in the past six months
- **16%** of firms specifically cited tariffs as the reason for project disruption
- **41%** of contractors have raised prices in response to tariffs
- **43% of general contractors** (AGC-NCCER survey) reported at least one project cancelled, postponed, or scaled back due to **higher material costs driven by tariffs**
- Many projects saw updated quotes for steel, aluminum, or switchgear exceed original budgets by **10-15%**

## Material Cost Escalation
- Steel: up **13% year-over-year**
- Aluminum mill shapes: up **33% year-over-year**
- Copper wire: significant increases
- Switchgear and electrical panels: major cost overruns reported

## Industry Response
- AGC urging contractors to update contract language with **escalation clauses**
- Recommending pass-through provisions for tariff-related cost increases
- Many contractors absorbing some costs or sharing with suppliers
- Some firms shifting to alternative materials where possible

## Analysis
- The survey data provides ground-level evidence that tariffs are not just raising costs -- they are actively killing projects
- A 10-15% budget overrun on steel/aluminum-heavy projects is enough to make many projects financially unviable
- For building materials: the tariff-driven project cancellation wave reduces overall construction volume, partially offsetting the higher per-unit material prices
- The dynamic creates a "price up, volume down" scenario for the industry
- Contract escalation clauses are becoming essential, fundamentally changing how the construction industry manages risk
- Companies that can offer value engineering (alternative materials, lighter designs) may capture share from traditional approaches
