# Construction Lending Standards Ease for First Time Since Rate Hikes Began

## Federal Reserve SLOOS Data (January 2026)

- Banks eased underwriting standards for CRE loans for the **first time since interest rates started rising in 2022**
- Net tightening measure: **-3.16%** (negative numbers reflect net easing)
- Applies across combined CRE loan types: construction & land development, nonfarm nonresidential, and multi-family

## Significance

- Suggests lenders are gaining confidence that market conditions have stabilized
- Risks are becoming more manageable in lenders' assessment
- Marks a potential turning point after years of progressive tightening

## 2026 Refinancing Environment

- Despite easing, 2026 is described as a "sorting year" for CRE
- Banks, CMBS, and private credit applying tougher underwriting standards
- Marginal assets face stricter debt service coverage requirements and heightened scrutiny
- Key question: whether an asset can service current debt with today's rate coupons

## Regulatory Developments (March 2026)

- White House executive action: "Promoting Access to Mortgage Credit"
- Federal regulators directed to consider revising supervisory guidance to:
  - **Exclude 1-4 family residential development and construction lending** from commercial real estate concentration guidance
  - Ensure supervisory expectations support responsible construction lending by community banks
- This could significantly ease constraints on residential construction lending

## Impact on Construction

- Easing lending standards should gradually support new construction activity
- Community bank construction lending may increase if regulatory changes are implemented
- However, elevated interest rates (3.50-3.75% fed funds rate) continue to make project financing expensive
- Commercial real estate refinancing remains challenging for assets underwritten at lower rates
