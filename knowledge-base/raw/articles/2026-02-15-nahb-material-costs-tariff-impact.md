# NAHB: Material Costs and Tariff Impact on Housing Affordability

## Tariff Cost Impact on Housing
- NAHB estimates typical tariff cost effect: $10,900 per home
- Material costs represent ~50% of total home construction costs
- Tariffs on steel, aluminum, lumber, copper, and cement all adding to builder costs

## Key Material Tariffs Affecting Home Building
- Steel: up to 50% tariff (structural steel, rebar, fasteners, HVAC ductwork)
- Aluminum: up to 50% tariff (windows, siding, electrical wiring)
- Copper: 50% tariff (plumbing, electrical wiring)
- Canadian/Mexican cement: 25% tariff
- Lumber: countervailing and anti-dumping duties on Canadian softwood lumber
- Section 122 global surcharge: 10-15% on remaining imported materials

## Material Price Trends (Early 2026)
- Building material price growth remains elevated despite sluggish market
- Framing lumber: ~$872/MBF (up ~13% YoY)
- Concrete/cement: 4-6% price increases from EPA regulations + 25% tariffs
- Steel bars, plates, structural shapes: PPI up 12.1%
- Aluminum: PPI up 39.1% YoY
- Overall nonresidential construction input prices: up 12.6% annualized

## Senate Response
- Senate bill proposed in late February 2026 to exclude building materials from tariffs
- Bill aims to reduce housing cost burden from trade policy
- Status: proposed but uncertain legislative path

## Broader Affordability Context
- High housing price-to-income ratios persist
- Construction costs combining with elevated home prices and mortgage rates
- Builder confidence at 36 (well below 50 breakeven)
- 36% of builders cutting prices; average reduction of 6%
