# Carrier Reports 2025 Results and 2026 Outlook: Data Center Revenue Hit $1 Billion

## Key Details
- Q4 2025 sales of $4.8 billion, down 6% (organic sales down 9%, 3% FX tailwind)
- Q4 revenue missed analyst expectations by 3%
- Full-year 2025 revenues of $22 billion, in line with forecasts
- Statutory EPS of $1.72, missing estimates by 7.4%
- Q4 commercial HVAC orders up nearly 50% driven by data center wins
- Data center revenue reached approximately $1 billion in 2025
- Data center orders up 400% in Q4 in North America
- 2026 guidance: data center revenue of approximately $1.5 billion (50% growth)
- Expected per-share earnings of $2.40 for 2026 (38% leap)
- Fifth consecutive year of double-digit commercial HVAC aftermarket growth
- Sixth consecutive year of double-digit commercial HVAC growth expected in 2026
- Completed $10B+ in divestitures (Security to Honeywell $4.95B, Industrial Fire to Sentinel $1.425B, Commercial Refrigeration to Haier $775M)
- Reduced net debt by over $5 billion using divestiture proceeds

## Analysis
- Carrier's transformation from a diversified conglomerate to a pure-play climate company is now essentially complete. The data center cooling business is the standout growth engine, with 400% Q4 order growth driven by hyperscaler demand for AI compute infrastructure. The $1.5B 2026 data center revenue target (6.8% of total sales) growing at 50% annually is remarkable for a company of Carrier's size. However, the Q4 organic sales decline of 9% highlights that the residential HVAC market remains weak, creating a two-speed business.
