# Lumber Futures Climb to $618.50 as Mortgage Rates Hit 3-Year Low; Spring Outlook Mixed

## Key Details
- Lumber futures rallied 17.5% to a high of $618.50 per 1,000 board feet on January 21, 2026
- Highest level in nearly three months
- Rally driven by 30-year mortgage rates falling to 6.06% on January 15, the lowest in 3+ years
- Lower rates lifted purchase and refinance activity
- Framing lumber at $872.03/MBF (NAHB metric), down 3.44% from Q4 2025 but up YoY
- March physical lumber futures at $598.50 (Jan 29); May 2026 delivery at $629
- By late February, lumber futures retreated toward $580-590 as housing data weakened and restocking faded
- By March 2026, lumber hit 17-month low of $508.50

## Analysis
- The January rally was a false start driven by mortgage rate optimism that quickly faded as underlying housing demand failed to materialize.
- The subsequent decline from $618 to $508 (an 18% drop in ~2 months) illustrates the extreme volatility that makes lumber one of the most difficult commodities to trade.
- For lumber producers (Canfor, Interfor, West Fraser, Weyerhaeuser), the price trajectory from $618 to $508 means Q1 2026 margins likely deteriorated significantly from the brief January improvement.
- The $508 low in March suggests lumber may be approaching or at the cyclical bottom, but previous "bottom" calls have been premature in this extended downcycle.
