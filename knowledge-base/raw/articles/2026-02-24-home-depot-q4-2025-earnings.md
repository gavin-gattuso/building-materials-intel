# Home Depot Q4 2025: Revenue Beats but Declines 3.8% YoY

## Q4 2025 Results (Quarter ended February 1, 2026)
- Revenue: $38.2 billion (down ~3.8% YoY, but above analyst expectations)
- Beat Wall Street's earnings and revenue expectations
- Comparable sales: +0.4% overall, +0.3% in U.S.
- Conservative full-year guidance maintained

## Key Themes
- Housing market described as "frozen" by management
- Home improvement demand remains tepid due to high borrowing costs
- Mortgage rate lock-in effect limiting housing turnover and renovation spending
- Pro customer segment showing relative strength
- Online channel continuing to grow

## 2026 Outlook
- Conservative guidance reflecting ongoing macroeconomic uncertainty
- Management cited frozen housing market as primary reason
- Home improvement spending expected to remain constrained
- Potential upside if mortgage rates continue declining below 6%

## Industry Context
- Home Depot and Lowe's together represent majority of home improvement retail market
- Both reporting in same week (Feb 24-25) with similar themes
- Home Depot's larger Pro exposure gives it slight advantage in current environment
- Neither retailer signaling meaningful housing market recovery in near term
