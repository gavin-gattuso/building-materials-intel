# USA: Disrupting Industry Dynamics

## Key Points
- US cement market entering a period of profound structural tension
- Falling consumption, rising imports, and rapid shift to Type IL cement have disrupted long-standing industry dynamics
- Type IL cement (portland-limestone cement) adoption creating conflict between cement producers and ready-mix/concrete products manufacturers
- US remains the most attractive market for global producers due to infrastructure stimulus, structural under-supply, and resilient pricing
- US end-markets expected to deliver strongest margin expansion globally in 2026
- Rare alignment of disciplined pricing, favorable cost dynamics, and structurally constrained supply supporting margins
- Import dynamics from Turkey and Asia could test coastal pricing discipline in second half of 2026
- Up to 27% of US cement imports come from Mexico and Canada (7% of total US consumption), exposed to 25% tariffs
