# Fitch Ratings: Modest Recovery in Global Steel Market in 2026 Amid High Protectionism

## Key Data Points
- Fitch maintains **neutral sector outlook** for global steel in 2026
- China steel output expected to decline **4.5% year-over-year** in 2026 due to tighter production controls and trade barriers
- U.S. steel demand expected to improve by **low single digits** in 2026 vs. 2025
- European steelmakers supported by tighter import quotas and CBAM introduction

## U.S. Steel Market Outlook
- Infrastructure investment (IIJA), improved demand, lower interest rates, and limited imports support U.S. producers
- Data center and energy sector construction driving robust demand less sensitive to interest rate fluctuations
- Tariffs and trade protectionism creating "protectionism premiums" that benefit domestic producers

## Company-Specific Insights
- **Nucor:** Record order backlogs entering 2026; shipments expected to rise 5%; leveraging low-carbon EAF fleet for aggressive pricing
- **Steel Dynamics:** Robust demand from data center and energy sectors; aluminum flat-rolled mill ramp-up progressing
- **ArcelorMittal:** Expected earnings growth of 22.1% for 2026; Calvert EAF investment positioning for tariff-protected market

## Construction Industry Implications
- Modest steel demand recovery benefits steel-intensive construction (commercial, industrial, data centers)
- Tariff-driven price premiums keep U.S. steel costs elevated, pressuring construction budgets
- China's production decline could ease global oversupply but tariffs largely insulate U.S. market
- Infrastructure and data center spending are the key demand drivers, not residential construction
