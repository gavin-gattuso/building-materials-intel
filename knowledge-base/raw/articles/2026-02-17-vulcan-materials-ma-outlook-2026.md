# Vulcan Materials Signals Active 2026 M&A Year; Healthy Pipeline of Acquisition Targets

## Key Details
- Management stated 2026 will be "a very active year on the strategy side and the M&A front"
- M&A approach described as "aggregates-led" and "very disciplined"
- Pipeline described as "very healthy" with "really good conversations with some potential sellers"
- Aim to achieve #1 or #2 position in fastest-growing U.S. markets
- Will pursue downstream asphalt and concrete businesses complementary to aggregates in select markets
- **CalPortland/Vulcan California ready-mix deal**: Vulcan is divesting California ready-mix assets to CalPortland (Taiheiyo Cement subsidiary) for $712 million
  - Delayed to H1 2026 after DOJ Second Request under HSR Act
  - Subject to antitrust review
- Recent acquisitions include Superior Ready Mix, Wake Stone, and Syar Industries
- Strong cash generation and balance sheet support continued deal activity

## Analysis
- Vulcan's M&A signals are bullish: "very active" and "healthy pipeline" suggest multiple transactions could close in 2026
- The emphasis on discipline and avoiding overpayment shows management is not chasing deals at inflated valuations
- The California ready-mix divestiture to CalPortland is strategically consistent with Vulcan's aggregates-first approach
- The DOJ's Second Request on the CalPortland deal indicates regulatory scrutiny of market concentration in California ready-mix
- Vulcan's combination of organic growth (17 greenfield projects) and inorganic growth (bolt-on acquisitions) creates multiple pathways to the $4.5-$5.0B EBITDA target
