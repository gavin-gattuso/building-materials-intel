# Construction Costs Rise at Fastest Rate Since January 2023 in November, Outpacing Increases in Contractors' Bid Prices

## Key Data Points

- The producer price index (PPI) for materials and services used in **nonresidential construction** rose 0.4% in November (not seasonally adjusted) and **3.6% year-over-year** -- the largest YoY increase since January 2023
- Contractor bid prices for new nonresidential buildings rose only 2.1% YoY, meaning input costs are rising faster than what contractors can charge
- The PPI for **aluminum mill shapes** (subject to 50% tariff) soared **28% from November 2024 to November 2025**
- Primary and secondary nonferrous metals prices up **more than 25%** over the past year
- **53% of contractors** list materials costs as one of their top concerns for 2026
- Only **24% of contractors** said they had not been affected by tariffs

## Tariff Impact on Specific Materials

- Aluminum mill shapes: 50% tariff, prices up 28% YoY
- Steel and aluminum imports: 50% tariff rates
- Copper: 50% tariff on semi-finished copper and derivatives
- Softwood lumber: 10% tariff (Section 232, effective Oct 14, 2025)
- Kitchen cabinets and vanities: 25% tariff rising to 50% on Jan 1, 2026
- Upholstered furniture: 25% tariff rising to 30% on Jan 1, 2026

## Analysis

AGC reports that construction input cost inflation is outpacing what contractors can pass through in bid prices, creating margin compression across the industry. The tariff regime is the primary driver of accelerating costs in metals, with aluminum and steel bearing the heaviest impact. AGC urged federal officials to consider the construction cost implications of trade policy and to quickly renew key infrastructure measures like the surface transportation bill.
