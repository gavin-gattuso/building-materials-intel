# NAHB HMI February 2026: Builder Sentiment Falls to 36 on Affordability Concerns

## Key Details
- NAHB/Wells Fargo Housing Market Index: **36** in February
- Down **1 point** from January (37)
- Below market expectations of **37**
- Below the **50** breakeven threshold for every month since mid-2024
- Key concern cited: **affordability**
- Mortgage rates at approximately **6.1%** during the survey period

## HMI Component Breakdown
- Current single-family home sales conditions: weakening
- Sales expectations for the next six months: mixed
- Prospective buyer traffic: remained soft

## Builder Response to Market Conditions
- Builders continuing to use heavy incentive packages to drive sales
- Mortgage rate buydowns remain the most common incentive
- Price cuts averaging approximately **5%** across reporting builders
- Lot development slowing in response to demand uncertainty

## Analysis
- February's HMI at 36 is the lowest reading since 2023, signaling deep pessimism among homebuilders
- The persistent sub-50 readings indicate builders see more challenges than opportunities in the current market
- Affordability is the core issue: the combination of ~$400K+ median home prices and 6%+ mortgage rates puts homeownership out of reach for many first-time buyers
- For building materials: the weak HMI suggests builders will remain cautious with new project starts, focusing on finishing existing inventory rather than breaking ground on new developments
- The incentive-heavy environment means builders are prioritizing volume over margin, which can pressure material suppliers for price concessions
