# Aluminum Prices Surge 40% on 50% Tariffs, Hitting Construction Budgets

## Key Details
- Aluminum prices: **$3,113.70 USD/ton** (global benchmark, February 2026), up **16.77%** year-over-year
- US consumer aluminum prices: above **$5,200/metric ton**, up approximately **40%** since tariffs doubled to 50%
- PPI for aluminum mill shapes: up **33.0%** year-over-year (January 2025 to January 2026)
- Tariff history: 25% implemented February 2025, doubled to **50%** in June 2025
- Largest year-over-year PPI increase since early 2022 supply chain disruptions

## Construction Applications Affected
- Aluminum curtain wall and window framing systems
- Commercial roofing and cladding
- Structural aluminum components
- HVAC ductwork
- Architectural metalwork and trim

## Industry Response
- **Two in five contractors** have raised prices to offset tariffs
- **16%** absorbed costs or shared them with suppliers
- Many contractors report projects cancelled or scaled back due to aluminum cost overruns
- AGC urging contract language updates to include escalation clauses

## Analysis
- The 40% domestic price increase is among the most severe material cost shocks facing the construction industry
- Aluminum is particularly important for commercial construction (curtain walls, storefronts) -- making commercial projects disproportionately affected
- Unlike steel, aluminum has fewer domestic substitutes, and US primary aluminum production has been declining for decades
- The 33% PPI increase for aluminum mill shapes directly impacts glazing contractors, metal building manufacturers, and architectural metal fabricators
- For building materials companies: firms with aluminum-intensive product lines (windows, doors, metal roofing) face the most acute margin pressure unless they can pass through costs
