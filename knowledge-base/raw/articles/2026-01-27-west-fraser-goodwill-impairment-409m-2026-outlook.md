# West Fraser Announces $409 Million Goodwill Impairment in Lumber Segment; Issues 2026 Operational Outlook

## Key Details
- US$409 million non-cash goodwill impairment recorded in Q4 2025
- Represents the entire amount of goodwill associated with U.S. lumber operations
- Drivers: species-specific pricing trends, lower demand/pricing for wood chip residuals, depth and duration of current downcycle
- 2026 operational guidance:
  - SPF shipments: 2.4-2.7 billion board feet
  - SYP shipments: 2.4-2.7 billion board feet
  - N.A. OSB: 5.9-6.3 billion sq ft (3/8-inch basis)
  - EU/UK OSB: 1.0-1.25 billion sq ft (3/8-inch basis)
  - Capital expenditures: $300-$350 million
- EPS projected to remain negative in H1 2026 before slight H2 improvement
- Announced separately from Q4 results (which came February 12)

## Analysis
- Writing off the entire U.S. lumber goodwill is a decisive accounting acknowledgment that the Norbord and other U.S. lumber acquisitions have not delivered expected returns at current market conditions.
- The $300-350M capex range is roughly maintenance-level for West Fraser's asset base, indicating no growth spending planned for 2026.
- The guidance for negative H1 2026 EPS with only "slight" H2 improvement suggests management does not expect a meaningful lumber price recovery in 2026.
- At 2.4-2.7B board feet each for SPF and SYP, West Fraser is guiding to reduced production volumes that reflect both curtailments and permanent closures.
