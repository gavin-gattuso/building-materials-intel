# West Fraser Reports Q4 2025 Results: Massive Impairment Charges; Full-Year Loss of $937 Million

## Key Details
- Q4 2025 Adjusted EBITDA: negative $79 million (improved from negative $144M in Q3)
- Full-year 2025 net loss of $937 million, or $(12.08) per diluted share, vs. $(5) million or $(0.07)/share in 2024
- Full-year 2025 Adjusted EBITDA of $56 million, down from $673 million in 2024
- $409 million non-cash goodwill impairment in Lumber segment (represents entire U.S. lumber goodwill)
- $473 million restructuring/impairment charges in Lumber segment (Q4)
- $239 million restructuring/impairment charges in N.A. Engineered Wood Products segment (Q4)
- Lumber segment Adjusted EBITDA: $(57) million in Q4 (excluding charges)
- N.A. EWP segment Adjusted EBITDA: $(24) million in Q4 (excluding charges)
- Europe EWP segment Adjusted EBITDA: $4 million (sole positive segment)
- 2026 guidance: SPF 2.4-2.7B board feet, SYP 2.4-2.7B board feet, N.A. OSB 5.9-6.3B sq ft
- 2026 capex: $300-$350 million

## Analysis
- West Fraser's full-year loss of $937 million represents a catastrophic deterioration from 2024, driven by historically weak lumber prices, high Canadian operating costs, and cumulative tariff impacts.
- The $409M goodwill writedown of the entire U.S. lumber segment goodwill signals that management no longer expects the near-term recovery needed to justify prior acquisition valuations.
- The European EWP business is the sole bright spot, reflecting stronger demand dynamics in Europe's engineered wood market.
- At $56M full-year Adjusted EBITDA on a multi-billion-dollar asset base, West Fraser is effectively running at near-breakeven, underscoring the severity of the lumber downcycle.
