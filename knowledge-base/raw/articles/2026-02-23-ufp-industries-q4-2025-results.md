# UFP Industries Reports Q4 2025: Sales Down 9%, EPS $0.70; Deckorators Surges 44%

## Key Details
- Q4 2025 net sales of $1.33 billion, down 9% YoY (2% price decline + 7% organic unit decline)
- Diluted EPS of $0.70 vs. $1.12 a year ago (37% decline)
- Net earnings of $40 million vs. $68 million prior year
- Adjusted EBITDA of $107.2 million (8.1% of net sales vs. 9.1% prior year)
- Deckorators Surestone sales up 44% YoY; wood plastic composite up 35%
- Deckorators organic unit sales grew 17% in Q4
- Packaging segment: structural packaging volume turned positive for first time since 2021
- $443 million in share repurchases in 2025 (7% of outstanding shares); $82 million in dividends
- Selma plant fully operational; Buffalo facility ($77M investment) expected online by end Q1 2026
- Deckorators projected to add $100 million in incremental sales in 2026
- $35 million SG&A cuts achieved in 2025, exceeding target; on track for $60M total by end of 2026
- Reinvesting $20 million in Deckorators brand marketing
- 2026 capital investments: $300-$325 million
- $2.2 billion in liquidity (>$900M cash + $1.3B unused credit)

## Analysis
- UFP Industries' story is a tale of two businesses: legacy lumber/site-built housing (declining) vs. Deckorators/composites (surging). The 44% Surestone growth rate is exceptional.
- The Deckorators brand is becoming the company's primary growth engine, with $100M incremental sales projected for 2026 -- a bet on the composite decking market gaining share from treated lumber.
- The $77M Buffalo plant investment and broader $250M five-year Deckorators expansion plan represent a strategic pivot toward value-added composite products.
- At $2.2B in liquidity, UFP has one of the strongest balance sheets in building materials, providing significant flexibility for M&A in the fragmented packaging industry.
- The 9% revenue decline masks underlying business mix improvement as high-margin Deckorators grows while lower-margin commodity lumber shrinks.
