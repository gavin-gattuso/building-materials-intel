# Canfor Reports Q4 2025 Results: $390.5 Million Net Loss; Lumber and Pulp Segments Under Pressure

## Key Details
- Q4 2025 operating loss of $415.9 million; net loss of $390.5 million ($3.35 per share)
- EPS of -$1.09 (USD), missing forecast of -$0.92
- Revenue of $1.28 billion USD, exceeding forecast of $1.18 billion by 8.5%
- Lumber segment Adjusted EBITDA: loss of CAD $8 million (CAD $6M worse than Q3)
- Pulp segment Adjusted EBITDA: loss of $17 million (CAD $14M worse than Q3)
- Total levies on Canfor's US-bound shipments: 57.59% (including Section 232 tariffs)
- Stock fell 6.11% following earnings release
- 2026 outlook: North American lumber markets face continued uncertainty; conditions projected to remain subdued through at least H1 2026
- Unresolved trade issues and sustained higher softwood lumber duty rates cited as key headwinds

## Analysis
- Canfor's 57.59% total tariff burden is the highest among the three major Canadian lumber producers (vs. West Fraser at 36.47% and Interfor at ~35%), making it the most exposed to US trade policy.
- The Q4 operating loss of nearly $416M on $1.28B in revenue demonstrates that Canfor is losing roughly 33 cents on every dollar of sales at current pricing and duty levels.
- Revenue beat expectations despite losses, suggesting volume resilience even as pricing destroys profitability.
- The company's multi-year effort to diversify toward U.S. operations is critical given the punitive duty rates on Canadian-sourced lumber.
