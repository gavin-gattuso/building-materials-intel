# Hot Rolled Coil Steel Prices Breach $1,000 Milestone as Tariff Rally Accelerates

## Key Details
- **January 2026 (early)**: HRC at $874/ton (lowest since Nov 2025)
- **January 13**: Average spot HRC at $935/short ton
- **Late January**: HRC rose to $970/ton (highest since Jan 2024)
- **February 9**: Nucor CSP at $975/ton
- **February Monthly Gain**: ~2.63% increase
- **Early March**: Nucor raised CSP to $960/ton ($10/ton weekly increase)
- **Late March/Early April**: HRC prices breached $1,000/ton
- **April 2 (Futures)**: Open price at $1,051/ton
- **April (Spot)**: HRC reached as high as $1,074/short ton
- **Q1 2026 Trend**: Steady upward trajectory from $874 to $1,000+
- **Key Driver**: 50% Section 232 tariffs reducing import competition
- **Import Share**: Down from 25% to ~14% of U.S. finished steel market

## Analysis
- The 23% price increase from January lows ($874) to April highs ($1,074) represents one of the sharpest Q1 rallies in recent HRC history
- Nucor's weekly CSP price adjustments have served as a leading indicator for the broader market, with the company acting as the de facto price leader
- The breach of $1,000/ton is psychologically significant and represents a level at which domestic producers generate exceptional margins
- For building materials consumers (construction, infrastructure, manufacturing), steel costs are now a major headwind that will pressure margins or require price pass-throughs
- The tariff-driven price environment creates a structural floor, but the ceiling will be determined by domestic capacity additions (Nucor Apple Grove, STLD Sinton ramp) and demand conditions
