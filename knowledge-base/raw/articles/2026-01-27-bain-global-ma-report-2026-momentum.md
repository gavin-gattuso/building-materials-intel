# Bain & Company: Global M&A Poised to Sustain Momentum in 2026 After Great Rebound

## Key Findings

- Global M&A positioned to continue momentum in 2026 after rising **40% to $4.9 trillion in 2025** -- the second-highest deal value on record
- Bain survey of **300 M&A executives**: 80% expect to sustain or increase deal activity in 2026
- Improving macro conditions and a growing backlog of private equity and venture capital assets ready for exit

## Building Products Context

- M&A activity in construction and building products **remained mostly stable in 2025** after a brief rebound in 2024
- Underscores still-fragile construction demand and uncertain outlook
- Companies that make **frequent and material acquisitions outpace inactive companies** in TSR: 9.6% vs. 2.7%

## Regional Performance (First 9 Months 2025)

- **North America**: +33% increase in deal value, bulk of largest deals
- **EMEA**: -48% drop in deal value
- **Asia-Pacific**: -44% drop in deal value

## Strategic Implications

- Geopolitics and post-globalization will continue to drive M&A strategy in 2026 and beyond
- Tariff shocks in 2025 drove a deeper understanding of how fragmentation will reshape flows of goods, capital, IP, and labor
- Winners invest through the cycle, benefiting from lower multiples now
- Due diligence must clearly separate temporary cyclical softness from structural weakness
