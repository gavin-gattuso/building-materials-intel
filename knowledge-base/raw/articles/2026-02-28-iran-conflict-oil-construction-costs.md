# Iran Conflict Begins February 28: Oil Nears $100, Construction Costs Face New Shock

## Event
- Joint US-Israeli airstrikes targeting Iranian leadership and military infrastructure began February 28, 2026
- Strait of Hormuz disrupted, forcing tanker reroutes around Africa
- IEA characterized as "largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market"

## Oil & Energy Price Impact
- Oil prices surged toward $100/barrel
- Diesel prices jumped over 20% in early weeks of conflict
- Natural gas prices already up 10.9% month-over-month in February (pre-conflict)
- Diesel hit $5.45/gallon (per AAA), up 45% since start of war
- A 10% rise in diesel pushes headline CPI up by 0.1%

## Construction Industry Impact
- PPI data for February does NOT yet capture full Iran war impact (conflict began last day of month)
- Already elevated material costs expected to accelerate further
- Aluminum mill shapes: already up 39.1% YoY before conflict
- Steel mill products: already up 20.9% YoY before conflict
- Disruption of oil, natural gas, and aluminum supplies from Middle East pushing up costs
- Project owners beginning to delay projects due to cost uncertainty

## Supply Chain Disruption
- Tanker rerouting adds weeks to transit times
- Extended shipping times increasing costs for all imported materials
- Middle East aluminum and petrochemical supplies directly affected
- Cascading impact through construction supply chains

## Industry Response
- Contractors pulling back on fixed-price bids
- Escalation clauses becoming standard in new contracts
- Some owners delaying project starts to assess cost trajectory
- AGC warning of further price increases ahead
