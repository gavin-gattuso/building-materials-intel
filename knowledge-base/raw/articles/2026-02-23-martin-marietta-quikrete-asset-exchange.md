# Martin Marietta Completes Landmark Asset Exchange with Quikrete: Divests Cement, Acquires 20M Tons of Aggregates

## Key Details
- **Completion date**: February 23, 2026
- **What MLM acquired**: Aggregates operations producing ~20 million tons annually in Virginia, Missouri, Kansas, and Vancouver, BC; plus $450 million in cash
- **What MLM divested**: Midlothian cement plant, related cement terminals, and Texas ready-mixed concrete assets plus certain nonoperating land
- **Transaction structure**: Tax-efficient exchange
- **Updated 2026 guidance**: Revenue raised to $7.16 billion (from initial $6.89B)
- **Updated aggregates volume**: 222 million tons projected for 2026 (12% volume growth, 2% organic)
- **Strategic rationale**: Enhanced earnings durability by exchanging cyclical cement/concrete for aggregates

## Analysis
- This is a transformative transaction that dramatically increases Martin Marietta's pure-play aggregates exposure
- The Midlothian cement plant divestiture removes one of MLM's most capital-intensive and cyclical assets
- Adding 20 million tons of annual aggregates capacity is the largest aggregates acquisition in MLM's history
- The $450 million cash component sweetens the deal and partially compensates for the cement asset value
- The tax-efficient structure preserves value for shareholders, a hallmark of MLM's disciplined deal-making
- The updated guidance (revenue up $270M) reflects immediate accretion from the Quikrete aggregates portfolio
- This deal reinforces MLM's competitive positioning vs. Vulcan Materials in the race for pure-play aggregates dominance
