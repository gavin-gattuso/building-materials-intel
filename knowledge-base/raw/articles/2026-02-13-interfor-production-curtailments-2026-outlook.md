# Interfor Earnings Call: Maintenance-Only Capex, Cautious 2026 Outlook Amid Trade Uncertainty

## Key Details
- September 2025: announced 145 million board feet reduction (12% of normal) through year-end
- Later expanded Q4 2025 curtailments to 250 million board feet (26% of normal)
- Curtailments included reduced hours, extended holiday breaks, reconfigured shifts, extended maintenance
- Impacted all operating regions: BC, Ontario, US Pacific Northwest, US South
- Permanently closed two high-cost U.S. South facilities (previously curtailed in 2024)
- 2026 capex: CAD $75-80M; 2027: ~CAD $60M -- "almost entirely on maintenance"
- Interfor operates 13 U.S. sawmills (majority of output) vs. Canadian mills
- 2026 outlook "remains uncertain, with trade and geopolitical developments adding risk and potentially slowing interest rate easing and broader economic activity"
- Geographic diversification strategy: more U.S. production to avoid Canadian tariff exposure

## Analysis
- Interfor's systematic shift to U.S.-based production mirrors the broader Canadian lumber industry trend of moving operations south of the border to avoid the cumulative 35%+ tariff burden.
- The reduction to maintenance-only capex is the most conservative capital stance in Interfor's recent history, preserving cash for a potentially prolonged downturn.
- With 13 U.S. mills vs. Canadian operations, Interfor is increasingly a U.S. lumber producer listed on the TSX, which changes the tariff risk profile significantly.
- The permanent closure of two U.S. South facilities suggests even domestic U.S. production is uneconomic at current lumber prices for higher-cost mills.
