# Oil Price Forecast: 2026 Expected to Be Cheapest Year for Gas Since COVID

## January 2026 Oil Prices

- **Brent crude** jumped by $6/bbl to around **$66/bbl** in early January before easing to **$64/bbl**
- Volatility driven by geopolitical tensions around Iran and Venezuela
- IEA Oil Market Report for January 2026 published with updated global supply/demand data

## 2026 Forecasts

- U.S. oil prices expected to average **$51 per barrel** in 2026, down from $65 in 2025
- Forecasters predicted relatively low oil prices for 2026 at the start of the year
- EIA Short-Term Energy Outlook projected moderate supply growth outpacing demand

## Impact on Construction Costs

- Annual increases for overall construction inputs climbed from **2.3%** in January to **3.1% YoY**
- In January 2026, tariffs enabled domestic producers to push up costs
- Aluminum and steel price indexes were accelerating every month since tariffs took effect
- Construction price inputs would later rise at a **12.6% annualized rate** in the first two months of 2026 (per subsequent data)

## Energy-Intensive Material Implications

- Cement/concrete production is highly energy-intensive; stable oil helps moderate cement costs
- Concrete and cement seeing **4-6% price increases** due to new EPA kiln regulations (separate from energy costs)
- Asphalt, diesel for equipment, and transportation costs all tied to energy prices
- Lower oil prices provide some offset to tariff-driven metal cost increases
- Overall construction cost inflation driven more by tariffs than energy in January 2026
