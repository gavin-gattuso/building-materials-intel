# Lowe's Reports Q4 2025 Earnings: Revenue Beats, Comp Sales Up 1.3%

## Q4 2025 Results (Quarter ended January 30, 2026)
- Revenue: ~$20.58 billion (up ~11% YoY)
- Beat Wall Street expectations for both revenue and earnings
- Comparable sales: +1.3% (driven by Pro, online, and home services sales, plus strong holiday performance)
- Adjusted diluted EPS: $1.98 (up 2.6% vs. prior year adjusted diluted EPS)

## Full-Year 2026 Guidance
- Total sales: $92 billion to $94 billion (up ~7%-9% over prior year)
- Guidance reflects integration of recent acquisitions and moderate market recovery expectations

## Key Growth Drivers
- Professional (Pro) customer segment continuing to grow
- Online sales channel expanding
- Home services sales gaining traction
- Holiday season performance stronger than expected

## Market Context
- Housing market remains "frozen" per management
- High borrowing costs limiting home improvement demand
- Home improvement demand remains tepid due to mortgage rate lock-in effect
- Conservative guidance reflects ongoing macroeconomic uncertainty
- Both Lowe's and Home Depot cited frozen housing market as primary constraint

## Comparison to Home Depot (Also Reported Feb 24-25, 2026)
- Home Depot Q4 revenue: $38.2 billion (down ~3.8% YoY, but above analyst expectations)
- Home Depot Q4 comps: +0.4% overall, +0.3% in U.S.
- Both retailers beat expectations but provided conservative forward guidance
- Both management teams cited ongoing housing market challenges
