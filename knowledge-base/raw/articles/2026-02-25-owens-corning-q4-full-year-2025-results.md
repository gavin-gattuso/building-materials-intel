# Owens Corning Reports Fourth-Quarter and Full-Year 2025 Results; $1.2B Doors Impairment Charge

## Key Details
- Full-year 2025 net sales from continuing operations of $10.1 billion, up 3% YoY
- Full-year diluted EPS from continuing operations of $(2.24); adjusted diluted EPS of $12.05
- Full-year adjusted EBITDA margin from continuing operations of 22%
- $1.2 billion in non-cash, pre-tax impairment charges related to the Doors business (Masonite acquisition) in H2 2025
- Operating cash flow of $1.8 billion; free cash flow of $1.0 billion
- Returned $1.0 billion to shareholders via dividends and share repurchases
- Q1 2026 guidance: revenue from continuing operations ~$2.1-2.2 billion; enterprise adjusted EBITDA margin from continuing operations in the mid-teens
- Doors segment Q1 revenue expected down mid-teens, reflecting weaker volumes and strategic sales of Oregon components facility and company-owned distribution business (~$150M combined annual revenues)
- Masonite integration synergies of $125 million achieved ahead of schedule

## Analysis
- The $1.2 billion impairment on the Doors segment, acquired just 18 months earlier for $3.9 billion, signals that the high-interest-rate environment has significantly depressed door demand and the near-term value of the Masonite acquisition. This is a major red flag for the residential building products sector broadly.
- Despite the impairment, underlying operations remain strong with $1.0B in free cash flow and cost synergies running ahead of plan.
- Owens Corning's strategic pivot toward a pure-play building products company (roofing, insulation, doors) is accelerating with the pending glass reinforcements divestiture.
- The mid-teens EBITDA margin guidance for Q1 2026 is well below the full-year 22% adjusted EBITDA margin, reflecting seasonal weakness and Doors headwinds.
