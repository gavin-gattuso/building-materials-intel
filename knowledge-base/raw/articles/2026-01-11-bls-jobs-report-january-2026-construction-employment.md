# BLS: Construction Employment Jumps by 33,000 Jobs in January 2026

## Headline Numbers

- Total nonfarm payroll employment increased by **130,000** in January 2026 (later revised to **160,000**)
- Above Dow Jones consensus estimate of **55,000**
- **Construction** added **33,000 jobs** in January

## Construction Employment Breakdown

- Gain concentrated in **nonresidential specialty trade contractors**: +25,000
- Follows a year in which the construction sector saw little employment increase
- January gain represents strongest monthly construction employment gain in several months

## Other Key Sectors (January 2026)

- **Health care**: +82,000 (largest gainer)
- **Social assistance**: +42,000
- **Construction**: +33,000

## Context for Building Materials Sector

- Construction employment gains signal continued demand for building products
- Nonresidential specialty trade contractor growth aligns with IIJA infrastructure execution
- Data center construction driving demand for electricians and specialized trades
- Wage growth in construction continuing to outpace broader private-sector averages at 6-8% annually
- Total construction industry employment approaching pre-pandemic levels but still constrained by demographics
