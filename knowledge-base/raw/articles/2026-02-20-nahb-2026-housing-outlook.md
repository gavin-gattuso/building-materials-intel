# NAHB 2026 Housing Outlook: Ongoing Challenges, Cautious Optimism

## January 2026 Housing Data (Most Recent Available)
### Housing Starts
- Seasonally adjusted annual rate: 1,487,000 (January 2026)
- Up 7.2% from revised December estimate of 1,387,000
- Up 9.5% from January 2025 rate of 1,358,000

### Building Permits
- Seasonally adjusted annual rate: 1,376,000 (January 2026)
- Down 5.4% from revised December rate of 1,455,000
- Down 5.8% from January 2025 rate of 1,460,000
- Declining permits signal potential weakness ahead (permits lead construction activity)

### New Home Sales (January 2026)
- Seasonally adjusted annual rate: 587,000
- Down 17.6% from December 2025 rate of 712,000
- Down 11.3% from January 2025 rate of 662,000
- Median sales price: $400,500
- Inventory of new homes for sale: 476,000

## Key Themes
- Mixed picture: housing starts rising but permits and sales declining
- Affordability remains primary constraint on housing demand
- Mortgage rates hovering near 6% providing some relief vs. 2023-2024 levels
- Tariff uncertainty adding cost pressure for builders
- Builder confidence at 36 (well below breakeven of 50)

## Outlook Challenges
- High housing price-to-income ratios
- Elevated land and construction costs
- Material cost inflation (12.6% annualized in early 2026)
- Labor cost inflation (6-8% annual wage increases)
- Tariff uncertainty (steel, aluminum, lumber, cement all affected)

## Note on Data Delays
- February 2026 housing starts/permits data: rescheduled from March 17 to April 29, 2026
- February 2026 new home sales data: rescheduled from March 24 to May 5, 2026
- Census Bureau data releases experiencing significant delays
