# Interfor Reports Q4 2025 Results: Net Loss of $104.6 Million; Significant Improvement from Q3

## Key Details
- Q4 2025 net loss of $104.6 million ($1.59/share), improved from Q3 loss of $215.8 million ($4.19/share)
- EPS of -$0.33 USD, beating forecast of -$1.29 by 74%
- Revenue of $600.6 million USD, exceeding forecast by 9%
- Adjusted EBITDA: loss of $29.2 million (improved from Q3 loss of $183.8 million)
- Lumber production of 753 million board feet, down 159 million from Q3 due to curtailments
- October 2025 bought deal equity offering: $143.8 million gross proceeds from 14.3 million shares
- Net debt to invested capital: 36.5%; available liquidity: $481.6 million (proforma)
- 2026 capex: CAD $75-80 million (primarily maintenance)
- 2027 preliminary capex: ~CAD $60 million
- Anti-dumping duty rate: 20.56%; countervailing duty rate: 14.63%
- Permanently closed two high-cost U.S. South facilities that were indefinitely curtailed in 2024
- Q4 2025 production curtailments of ~250 million board feet (26% of normal operating stance)

## Analysis
- Interfor's Q4 improvement from Q3 suggests the worst of the production disruptions may be passing, but the company remains deeply unprofitable at current lumber prices.
- The 26% production curtailment in Q4 represents one of the most aggressive supply-side responses in the industry, which should eventually support price recovery.
- The October equity raise ($143.8M) was a critical liquidity measure, suggesting the balance sheet was under stress; the 36.5% net debt ratio remains elevated for a cyclical commodity producer.
- Maintenance-only capex of CAD $75-80M for 2026 signals no growth investments until market conditions improve materially.
- Interfor is North America's third-largest softwood lumber producer, making its decisions a bellwether for the broader industry.
