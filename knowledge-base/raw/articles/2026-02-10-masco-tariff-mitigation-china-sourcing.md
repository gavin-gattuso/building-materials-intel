# Masco Outlines $200M Tariff Headwind and Aggressive China De-Sourcing Strategy

## Key Details
- Total annualized tariff cost impact estimated at $200 million before mitigation (down from $270M as of Q3 call)
- Reduction driven by 10% decline in China tariff rates and proactive sourcing changes
- Breakdown: ~$80M from 20% China tariffs; remainder from other country tariffs, 50% tariffs on steel/aluminum/copper, and glass anti-dumping duties
- Full $200M expected to impact 2026, with mitigation actions to offset direct cost impact
- China imports expected at ~$400M in 2026 (down from $450M in 2025), subject to reciprocal tariffs
- Target: China exposure below $300M by end of 2026 -- a 60%+ reduction from 2018 peak
- Mitigation will lean more heavily on sourcing footprint changes than on pricing or cost reductions
- Sourcing de-risking journey started in 2018-2019

## Analysis
- Masco's tariff mitigation strategy is one of the most detailed and advanced in building materials. The systematic reduction from peak China exposure since 2018 gives Masco a significant structural advantage over competitors who delayed their supply chain diversification. The $200M headwind is manageable given Masco's $1.3B operating profit base, and the shift toward sourcing changes over pricing preserves market share. This is a template for how building materials companies should approach tariff risk management.
