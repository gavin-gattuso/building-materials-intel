# Bain M&A Report 2026: Building Products -- Making the Right Bets in a Cyclical Industry

## Market Overview

- Building products M&A activity remained mostly stable in 2025 after a brief rebound in 2024
- Deal volume in building products sector fell **20.8% YoY** (265 transactions YTD 2024 to 210 YTD 2025, per Capstone Partners data)
- However, deal value surged on the back of several mega-transactions

## Regional Deal Activity (First 9 months of 2025)

- **North America**: +33% increase in deal value, bulk of largest deals
- **EMEA**: -48% drop in deal value
- **Asia-Pacific**: -44% drop in deal value

## Marquee 2025 Transactions

| Deal | Value | Strategic Rationale |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Lowe's / Foundation Building Materials | $8.8B | Pro customer channel expansion, 370+ locations |
| Home Depot / GMS | Undisclosed | Professional channel continuation (after SRS) |
| CRH / Eco Material Technologies | $2.1B | Fly ash and pozzolans scale |
| QXO / Apollo investment | $1.2B+ | Building products distribution rollup |

## Strategic Shift: Scale to Scope

- Center of gravity in building products M&A shifting from **scale** toward **scope and capability plays**
- Major retailers expanding professional contractor channels through strategic acquisitions
- U.S.-targeted M&A surged **66%** in H2 as firms adjusted to Build America, Buy America provisions

## 2026 Outlook

- A 30% increase in deal volume expected, per some forecasts
- Acceleration in commercial and institutional construction planning expected to prop up deal flow
- Interest rate cuts, if they materialize, would make project financing cheaper for developers
- Data center construction and energy infrastructure are key demand drivers
- Tariff uncertainty tempering valuations but structural demand drivers remain intact
