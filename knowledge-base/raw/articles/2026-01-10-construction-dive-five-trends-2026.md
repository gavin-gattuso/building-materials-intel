# 5 Construction Trends to Watch in 2026

## Trend 1: Tariff-Driven Cost Volatility

- 53% of contractors list materials costs as top concern for 2026
- Only 24% of contractors unaffected by tariffs
- Forward-looking firms integrating data analytics, cloud-based estimating, and ML forecasting models
- Companies anticipating raw material pricing changes before they hit project budgets

## Trend 2: Alternative Building Methods

- Rising costs boosted interest in **modular and offsite construction**
- Concentrating production in controlled environments where materials can be purchased in bulk
- **3D concrete printing** can build projects up to **50% faster** vs traditional construction
- Reduces labor requirements by **50-70%** on suitable scopes
- Cuts material waste by up to **40%** through precise, additive processes

## Trend 3: AI and Automation Integration

- **71%** of businesses will integrate AI agents into departments by 2026
- AI agent networks operating across design, engineering, and construction
- Streamlining schedules, resolving conflicts, tracking progress, managing resources
- Digital Project Delivery (DPD) becoming contractually expected standard

## Trend 4: Data Center Construction Boom

- AI driving unprecedented demand for data center construction
- Surge in demand for electricians capable of precision wiring
- Energy infrastructure and grid modernization creating parallel demand
- Record demand for power-related construction

## Trend 5: Safety Technology Adoption

- Construction robotics market valued at **$1.30 billion** in 2026, projected to reach **$11.14 billion** by 2040
- Smart helmets and wearable sensors delivering **35% reduction** in workplace accidents
- **20% boost** in worker efficiency from safety tech adoption
- Robotics moving beyond limited trials to active job site deployment

## Market Context

- Construction spending expected to grow 5.6% annually to $1.27 trillion in 2026
- Persistent labor shortage (349,000 workers needed) accelerating technology adoption
- Sustainability driving adoption of biochar and carbon-sequestering materials
