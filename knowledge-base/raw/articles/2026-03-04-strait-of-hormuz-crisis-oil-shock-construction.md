# Strait of Hormuz Crisis Triggers Largest Oil Supply Disruption Since 1970s, Devastating Construction Costs

## Crisis Timeline

- **February 28, 2026**: Military conflict with Iran begins (Operation Epic Fury)
- **March 4, 2026**: Iranian forces declare the Strait of Hormuz "closed" and begin attacking ships
- **By March 12, 2026**: Iran has made 21 confirmed attacks on merchant ships
- Tanker traffic dropped approximately **70%**; over 150 ships anchored outside the strait

## Oil Price Impact

- Brent crude surpassed **$100/barrel** on March 8, 2026 -- first time in four years
- Brent peaked near **$126/barrel** before settling to **$111.10/barrel** as of March 30, 2026
- Oil prices surged approximately **50%** from the start of 2026
- Brent surged **47%** in less than a month at peak
- Described as the largest disruption to energy supply since the 1970s energy crisis

## Diesel Price Shock

- Diesel reached **$5.40/gallon** by late March, up more than **$1.90/gallon** since start of 2026
- March 2: diesel at **$3.89/gallon**
- March 5: diesel at **$4.17/gallon** (AAA daily report)
- Massive cost implications for construction equipment operation and material transport

## Scale of Disruption

- The Strait of Hormuz normally carries approximately **20%** of the world's crude oil and LNG supplies
- Blockade also disrupted export of petrochemicals, fertilizer, and raw materials used in plastic manufacturing
- Transit times extended by weeks as ships forced to reroute around Africa

## Construction Cost Impact

- Input prices for nonresidential construction surged at a **12.6% annualized rate** in the first two months of 2026
- Aluminum jumped nearly **40%** in a single year
- Rising oil prices directly elevate fuel costs, asphalt prices, and transportation costs for all building materials
- A cascading failure across multiple supply chains driven by spikes in oil, copper, aluminum, and steel

## Industry Response

- Firms prioritizing increased US sourcing
- Investment in cloud-based supply chain visibility
- Use of formal indexed pricing tied to published cost benchmarks
- AGC urging contract updates amid rising steel, copper, and lumber costs
