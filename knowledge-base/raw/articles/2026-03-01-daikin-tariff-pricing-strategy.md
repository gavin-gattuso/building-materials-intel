# Daikin Faces US Tariff Pressures but Maintains Profit Outlook Through Price Hikes

## Key Details
- Direct tariff impact estimated at JPY 18 billion on operating profit
- Original estimate of JPY 47 billion ($320M) has been recalculated lower
- Tariff rates: 54% on Chinese goods, 30-35% on Mexico/Canada parts
- Finished goods from Mexico meeting USMCA requirements are exempt
- Daikin implementing up to 7% price increases on select products
- Ductless, VRV, and light commercial products most affected
- Price increases implemented as of March 2026
- DNA (Daikin North America) faces higher costs on units imported from Japan, Asia, and Europe
- Locally produced units still rely on Asian parts
- Mitigation through price increases, cost-cutting, and flexible sourcing

## Analysis
- Daikin's tariff exposure is complex: as a Japanese manufacturer, it faces tariffs on finished units imported to the US and on components used in US-assembled products. The 7% price increases on select products will test demand elasticity in the residential HVAC market. Unlike Carrier and Trane (which manufacture more domestically), Daikin's import-heavy model creates a structural disadvantage in a high-tariff environment. The USMCA exemption for Mexican production provides some relief.
