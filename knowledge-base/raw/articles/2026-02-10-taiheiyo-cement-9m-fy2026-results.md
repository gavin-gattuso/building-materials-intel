# Taiheiyo Cement Reports Mixed 9-Month Results; Revises FY2026 Guidance Down on Impairment Losses

## Key Details
- **Fiscal year**: Ending March 31, 2026
- **Q3 FY2026 revenue**: JPY 233.1 billion (down from JPY 238.2 billion in prior year)
- **Q3 FY2026 net loss (excl. extraordinary items)**: JPY 6.7 billion
- **Revised full-year guidance**: Net sales JPY 906 billion; Operating profit JPY 70 billion; Net income JPY 17 billion
- **Impairment losses**: JPY 24.4 billion recorded as extraordinary losses (triggering guidance revision)
- **Trailing 12-month EPS**: JPY 204.34
- **TTM revenue**: JPY 885.7 billion
- **Net margin**: 2.6% (thin)
- **Medium-Term Management Plan**: "26 Plan" focused on carbon neutrality and circular economy

## Analysis
- The JPY 24.4 billion impairment charge is a significant headwind and reflects asset write-downs likely related to aging domestic capacity
- Japan's cement market continues to face structural headwinds from demographic decline and reduced domestic construction activity
- The 2.6% net margin is thin compared to Western peers like CRH (10.1%) and Holcim (18.3% EBIT margin)
- The company's high debt load adds financial risk in a low-margin environment
- Taiheiyo's growth strategy increasingly depends on international operations, particularly CalPortland in the U.S.
- The fiscal year timing (ending March 2026) means full-year results will be reported in May 2026
