# BEA GDP Q4 2025 Third Estimate: Scheduled for April 9, 2026

## Key Data Points
- **Release date:** April 9, 2026 at 8:30 a.m. EDT
- Originally scheduled for March 27 but delayed due to government data disruptions
- Will include: GDP (Third Estimate), Industries, Corporate Profits, State GDP, and State Personal Income
- This is the final estimate for Q4 2025 GDP

## Prior Estimates
- **Advance estimate** (January 30): **1.4%** annualized growth
- **Second estimate** (March 13): **0.7%** annualized growth (revised down 0.7 ppt)
- The second estimate was the weakest quarterly growth since early 2022
- Q3 2025 had been **4.4%**, making the Q4 slowdown dramatic

## Market Expectations
- Consensus expects the third estimate to confirm growth near **0.7-0.9%**
- Further downward revision would increase recession probability estimates
- Treasury yields fell on the second estimate, briefly supporting lower mortgage rates
- Fed futures market increased probability of a June 2026 rate cut after the second estimate

## Construction Industry Implications
- Weak Q4 GDP confirms the demand slowdown hitting construction activity
- Sub-1% growth typically correlates with declining construction starts within 2-3 quarters
- Corporate profits data (included in this release) will show building materials sector profitability trends
- State GDP breakdowns will reveal regional construction demand patterns
- Combined with Atlanta Fed GDPNow at 1.6% for Q1, the economy appears in a sustained low-growth trajectory
