# Canfor Vanderhoof Mill Closure Impact: $580K Tax Revenue Loss, 11% of District's Tax Base

## Key Details
- Canfor closed Plateau Mill in Vanderhoof, BC in December 2024; community impacts being felt in 2026
- District of Vanderhoof facing $580,000 annual tax revenue loss (11% of total tax base)
- Also closed Fort St. John, BC sawmill in December 2024
- Earlier in 2024: permanent closure of Polar sawmill in Bear Lake, BC (~180 employees affected)
- Suspended planned reinvestment in Houston, BC mill
- Closures cited: "increasing regulatory complexity, high operating costs and the inability to reliably access economically viable timber"
- Canfor's South Carolina closures (Darlington and Estill) added in 2025, eliminating 290 U.S. jobs and 350 million board feet of annual capacity
- Canfor now operates 11 mills in Georgia, North Carolina, Mississippi and neighbouring states

## Analysis
- Canfor's systematic closure of BC mills reflects a structural shift in Canada's forestry sector: declining timber supply (mountain pine beetle aftermath), rising regulatory costs, and punitive US tariffs make BC production increasingly uneconomic.
- The community-level devastation (11% tax base loss in Vanderhoof) illustrates the concentrated economic impact of mill closures in resource-dependent communities.
- Canfor's simultaneous closure of US mills (South Carolina) suggests the distress extends beyond Canadian-specific issues to a broader lumber market crisis.
- The company's remaining 11 US Southern mills represent the core of its future production strategy, as the US South offers lower costs, better timber access, and avoidance of Canadian tariff burdens.
