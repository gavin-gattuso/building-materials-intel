# Immigration Policy Impacts on Construction Labor: H-2B Expansion Amid Deportation Push

## Key Details
- DHS and DOL announced temporary H-2B visa cap increase: up to **64,716 additional visas** for FY2026
- H-2B program hit its FY2026 cap, reflecting strong employer demand
- Administration deportation target: **1,000,000 removals per year**
- DHS requesting funding "to support the Administration's strategy of 1,000,000 removals per year"
- Work permits and protective immigration statuses being cancelled

## Construction Workforce Impact
- NAHB position: "mass deportation is not the answer" -- any shock to labor force would adversely impact housing costs and supply
- Immigrant workers comprise an estimated **25-30%** of the construction workforce nationally
- Higher percentages in certain trades (concrete finishing, roofing, framing) and regions (Texas, California, Florida)
- Deportation of construction workers would reduce housing supply and increase costs

## Policy Tensions
- H-2B expansion (adding workers) vs. deportation campaign (removing workers) creates policy contradiction
- Employers increasingly viewing H-2B as critical given shrinking pool of available workers
- Construction is among the industries most dependent on immigrant labor

## Analysis
- The immigration policy environment creates significant labor supply uncertainty for the construction industry
- If deportation efforts accelerate, construction labor shortages could worsen dramatically in labor-intensive trades
- For building materials companies: labor constraints already limit how fast the industry can consume materials; immigration enforcement could tighten this further
- The H-2B visa expansion helps at the margin but is insufficient to offset the combined effects of retirement, deportation, and demand growth
- Regions with high immigrant construction workforce percentages (Texas, Florida, California) face the greatest risk
- This dynamic adds upward pressure to construction wages, which ultimately gets passed through in higher project costs and potentially reduced volume
