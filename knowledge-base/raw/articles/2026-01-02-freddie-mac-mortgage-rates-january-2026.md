# Mortgage Rates Hover Near 6% to Start 2026

## Weekly 30-Year Fixed Rate Averages (Freddie Mac PMMS)

| Week Ending | 30-Year Fixed | 15-Year Fixed |
|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Dec 31, 2025 | 6.15% | ~5.38% |
| Jan 2, 2026 | 5.99% | 5.38% |
| Jan 8, 2026 | 6.16% | -- |
| Jan 29, 2026 | 6.10% | -- |

- Year-ago comparison: January 2025 30-year FRM averaged **6.93%**, showing approximately **80 basis points** of decline over the year

## 2026 Forecasts

- Mortgage rates expected to hover near **6.3%** in 2026 overall
- Gradual decline expected but short-term rate increases possible
- Major forecasters: MBA, Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac all project rates staying above 6% for most of 2026

## Housing Affordability

- Median monthly housing payments fell to **$2,365** during the four weeks ending January 4, 2026
- This is down **4.7% year-over-year** -- the lowest level in **two years**
- Despite improved affordability metrics, no immediate surge in buyer activity
- Redfin attributes lack of surge to seasonal factors and broader economic headwinds

## Context

- The Fed delivered three consecutive rate cuts in late 2025 totaling **75 basis points** (September, November, December)
- However, long-term rates (which drive mortgages) have not declined as much as the short-term fed funds rate
- The Fed held rates steady at the January 28 FOMC meeting
- Homes remain unaffordable for many, especially younger buyers
