# Bain Building Products M&A Report 2026: Making the Right Bets in a Cyclical Industry

## 2025 Deal Activity Summary

- North America deal value increased **33%** through September 2025
- EMEA deal value decreased **48%**
- Asia-Pacific deal value decreased **44%**
- Total 2025 deal activity declined approximately **30%** year-over-year in count
- Strategic buyers represented **63%** of total transactions

## Major 2025 Transactions Shaping 2026 Landscape

| Deal | Value | Status |
|------|-------|--------|
| Lowe's acquired Foundation Building Materials | **$8.8 billion** | Completed Oct 2025 |
| Home Depot/SRS acquired GMS | **$5.5 billion** (enterprise value incl. debt) | Completed Sep 2025 |
| CRH acquired Eco Material Technologies | **$2.1 billion** | Completed Sep 2025 |

## Market Concentration Data

- **Cement sector (US)**: Top six players control approximately **65-70%** of capacity
- **Sanitary & Plumbing (Europe)**: Top five players hold approximately **25%** market share

## Key Strategic Trends

### Shift from Scale to Scope
- Transactions increasingly target expanding product categories and capabilities rather than traditional consolidation
- Revenue-focused due diligence now emphasizes commercial levers for top-line growth including cross-selling and diversification

### Performance Metrics
- Active acquirers delivered **9.6%** total shareholder returns
- Inactive peers delivered only **2.7%** total shareholder returns
- Gap demonstrates the value creation potential of disciplined M&A

### Due Diligence Focus
- Winners distinguish temporary cyclical softness from structural weakness
- Compare target performance against peer benchmarks
- Evaluate customer satisfaction metrics as leading indicators

## 2026 Outlook

- Building products M&A poised for continued growth in 2026
- Robust liquidity, strengthening housing outlook, and pent-up demand expected to drive activity
- Transaction volume expected to outpace 2025 levels
- Private equity remains disciplined, focused on targeted opportunities
- Data center construction remains a notable growth area, offsetting softness in commercial segments
