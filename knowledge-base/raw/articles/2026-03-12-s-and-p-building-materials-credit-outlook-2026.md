# S&P Global Industry Credit Outlook 2026: Building Materials

## Overall Assessment

- Building materials sector enters 2026 with mixed credit outlook
- Infrastructure spending (IIJA) provides a floor for demand
- Rising input costs and tariffs pressure margins
- Interest rate environment keeps financing costs elevated

## Sector Performance Metrics

- Building materials sector Q4 2025 missed revenue estimates by **1.2%** on average
- Share prices down **10.2%** since latest earnings results across the sector
- Cyclical pressures from interest rates weighing on volumes

## Key Companies

### Armstrong World Industries
- Q4 2025 revenues: **$388.3 million**, up 5.6% YoY
- Fell short of analyst expectations by 3%

### Carlisle Companies
- Q4 2025 revenues: **$1.13 billion**, flat YoY
- Outperformed analyst expectations by 1.4%
- Impressive beat of adjusted operating income estimates

### Trex Company
- Q4 2025 revenues: **$161.1 million**, down 3.9% YoY
- Outperformed analyst expectations by 11.3%
- Beat EPS and EBITDA estimates

### Doman Building Materials
- Delivered strong full-year 2025 results despite construction-material price volatility
- Challenging macro backdrop including elevated interest and mortgage rates

## Credit Considerations

- Companies at the whim of cyclical construction volumes
- Interest rates heavily impact construction volumes
- Raw material costs greatly influence profitability
- Active M&A (CRH, Lowe's, Home Depot) affects leverage metrics
- Infrastructure tailwinds provide demand stability
