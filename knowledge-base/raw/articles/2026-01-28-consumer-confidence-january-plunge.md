# Consumer Confidence Dives 9.7 Points in January to 84.5

## Key Details
- Consumer Confidence Index: **84.5** in January (1985=100)
- Decline: **-9.7 points** from December
- Present Situation Index: **113.7** (down 9.9 points)
- Expectations Index: **65.1** (down 9.5 points)
- The Expectations Index below **80** has historically been associated with recession risk

## Context
- This was the sharpest monthly decline in consumer confidence since early 2024
- The drop occurred amid uncertainty about tariff impacts, geopolitical tensions, and mixed economic data
- The Expectations Index at 65.1 is well below the 80-point recession warning threshold

## Analysis
- The January confidence plunge is a significant warning signal for the construction and building materials industry
- Consumer confidence directly correlates with home-buying intentions and renovation spending
- The steep drop in the Present Situation Index (-9.9 points) suggests consumers are feeling the pinch of higher prices now, not just worrying about the future
- For building materials: weak consumer confidence typically translates to softer demand for home improvement products (Home Depot, Lowe's exposure) and slower new home sales
- The Expectations Index at 65.1 is deeply pessimistic and suggests consumers expect conditions to worsen -- a headwind for the spring housing market
