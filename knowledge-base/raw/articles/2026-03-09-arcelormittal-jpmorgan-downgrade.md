# JPMorgan Downgrades ArcelorMittal to Underweight; Broader Analyst Sentiment Remains Bullish

## Key Details
- **JPMorgan Downgrade**: Analyst Dominic O'Kane reduced ArcelorMittal from "Overweight" to "Underweight" on March 9, 2026
- **JPMorgan Price Target Cut**: From EUR 53.50 to EUR 40 (25% reduction)
- **Rationale**: More pessimistic base case for copper and iron ore; Middle East risks not reflected in European metals equities
- **KeyBanc Initiation**: Started coverage with "Sector Weight" rating on March 25, 2026
- **Consensus Price Target**: $63.70 (28.38% upside), with 55% of analysts bullish
- **Median Target**: $43.00 (range $33.00 to $49.60) from 13 analysts
- **Overall Consensus**: 3 Buy, 4 Hold, 0 Sell

## Analysis
- JPMorgan's downgrade reflects growing macro uncertainty rather than company-specific concerns
- The wide range of analyst price targets ($33-$68) indicates significant uncertainty around global steel demand and commodity pricing
- ArcelorMittal's diversified global footprint creates both opportunity and risk exposure vs. U.S.-focused peers that benefit more directly from Section 232 tariffs
- Despite the downgrade, the majority of covering analysts remain constructive, reflecting confidence in ArcelorMittal's strategic transformation toward higher-value products
