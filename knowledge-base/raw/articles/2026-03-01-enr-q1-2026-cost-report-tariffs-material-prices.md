# ENR Q1 2026 Cost Report: Tariffs Drive Material Price Hikes Into 2026

## Key Price Data

### Steel
- ENR 20-city average yearly price for steel rose **11.9%** by end of 2025
- Steel remains one of the most unpredictable materials based on late-2025 BLS and AGC data
- Section 232 tariffs on steel reaching up to **50%**

### Lumber
- National average for framing lumber: approximately **$872 per thousand board feet (MBF)** as of early 2026
- Year-over-year lumber costs up nearly **13%** despite seasonal dips
- 10% tariff on softwood lumber; 25% tariff on lumber derivatives

### Concrete & Cement
- Concrete and cement seeing **4-6%** price increases due to new EPA kiln regulations
- **25%** tariffs on Mexican and Canadian cement contributing to higher costs

### Overall Input Costs
- Input prices for nonresidential construction surged at a **12.6% annualized rate** in the first two months of 2026
- US construction materials prices overall rose **3.5%** (ENR headline figure)

## Key Cost Drivers

1. **Federal tariffs**: Section 232 tariffs on steel and aluminum expanded significantly, with rates reaching up to 50%
2. **Energy costs**: Oil price shock from Strait of Hormuz crisis driving fuel and asphalt costs higher
3. **Regulatory changes**: New EPA kiln regulations affecting cement production costs
4. **Data center demand**: Voracious appetite of tech industry's data centers creating material competition
5. **Supply chain disruption**: Geopolitical trade wars amplifying volatility

## Outlook

- Steel, asphalt, and diesel remain the most unpredictable materials
- Contractors facing 4-6% increases in overall project expenses
- Industry moving toward indexed pricing tied to published cost benchmarks
- Material cost escalation clauses becoming standard in contracts
