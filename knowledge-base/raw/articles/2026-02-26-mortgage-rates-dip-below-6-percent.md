# Mortgage Rates Briefly Dip Below 6% in Late February -- 2026 Low Point

## Key Details
- 30-year fixed-rate mortgage: approximately **5.98%** in late February / early March 2026
- This represented the **lowest level since early 2023** (over three years)
- Brief dip driven by:
  - Weak Q4 2025 GDP data (advance estimate 1.4%, later revised to 0.7%)
  - Flight to safety in Treasury markets
  - Market expectations for Fed rate cuts
- March 5: rates ticked back up to **6.00%**
- By March 19: **6.22%**
- By April 2: **6.46%** (Iran conflict driving rates higher)

## Rate Journey in 2026
| Period | 30-Year Rate | Trend |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Jan 2 | 5.99% | Start of year |
| Jan 8 | 6.16% | Ticking up |
| Jan 15 | 6.06% | Brief dip |
| Late Feb | ~5.98% | 2026 low |
| Mar 5 | 6.00% | Stabilizing |
| Mar 19 | 6.22% | Rising on Iran |
| Apr 2 | 6.46% | 5th week up |

## Missed Opportunity
- The sub-6% window was brief (approximately 1-2 weeks)
- Purchase applications showed a modest uptick during the dip but insufficient to drive meaningful volume
- The rapid reversal back above 6% dashed hopes for a sustained rate decline

## Analysis
- The brief sub-6% window in late February was the year's best opportunity for housing demand, but it was too short-lived to generate sustained momentum
- The subsequent 50-basis-point increase (5.98% to 6.46%) through early April is a significant headwind for the spring selling season
- For building materials: mortgage rate volatility makes it difficult for builders and buyers to plan, contributing to the "wait and see" mentality reflected in weak NAHB HMI readings
- Freddie Mac's forecast of rates averaging ~6.3% for 2026 appears on track, but with wider-than-expected swings driven by geopolitical events
