# Nucor Apple Grove Sheet Mill: $4 Billion Investment On Track for Late 2026 Operations

## Key Details
- **Total Investment**: $4 billion (largest economic development investment in West Virginia and Nucor history)
  - Original commitment: $2.7 billion
  - Additional investment: $800 million (approved by WV Economic Development Authority with $75 million state support)
- **Location**: Apple Grove, Mason County, West Virginia (45 miles NW of Charleston)
- **Production Capacity**: 3 million tons/year of sheet steel
- **Target Markets**: Automotive and sustainable energy
- **Timeline**: Mid- to late 2026 for initial operations
- **Construction Progress**: ~50% complete as of mid-2025; ~1,100 construction workers on site (peak: 2,000)
- **Permanent Employment**: 800 full-time workers when operational (300 already employed + 100 additional from expansion)
- **Technology**: Electric arc furnace (EAF) -- low-carbon steelmaking

## Analysis
- The Apple Grove mill represents Nucor's largest-ever single investment and will meaningfully expand its flat-rolled steel capacity
- At 3 million tons/year, this facility will be among the largest EAF sheet mills in North America, competing directly with ArcelorMittal's Calvert operations
- The automotive and clean energy focus positions Nucor in high-margin, growing end markets that also benefit from U.S. industrial policy (IRA, EV incentives)
- The $800 million additional investment signals scope expansion beyond the original plan, likely including value-added coating and processing capabilities
- Apple Grove's commissioning in late 2026 will be a major catalyst for Nucor's earnings power in 2027+
