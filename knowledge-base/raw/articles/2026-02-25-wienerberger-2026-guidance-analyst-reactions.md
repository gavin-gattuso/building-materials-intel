# Wienerberger 2026 Guidance: No Housing Recovery Expected; Analysts Divided

## Key Details
- **2026 EBITDA Guidance (ex-Italcer)**: EUR 760 million (slight increase from EUR 754 million in 2025)
- **Self-Help Measures**: EUR 36 million in optimization measures planned
- **Energy Headwind**: EUR 30 million expected negative impact
- **Pricing Strategy**: Price increases of up to 2% to offset ~2.5% inflation
- **Market Assumptions**: No structural recovery in residential construction; flat infrastructure and renovation markets
- **Interest Rate View**: No decline in long-term interest rates expected
- **Italcer 2026 Contribution**: ~EUR 50 million EBITDA (8 months)
- **Analyst Reactions**:
  - Berenberg Bank: Maintained Buy, lowered PT to EUR 34 from EUR 35 (Feb 26)
  - Morgan Stanley: Lowered PT to EUR 29 from EUR 32 (Feb 25)
  - Barclays: Lowered PT to EUR 32 from EUR 35 (Feb 25)
  - UBS: Initiated with Sell, PT EUR 24 (citing housing concerns)
- **UBS Estimates**: 10% below Street EBITDA and 16% below EPS consensus for FY 2026
- **Consensus Analyst Net Profit**: EUR 250 million for 2026 (21.75% above TTM)

## Analysis
- Wienerberger's guidance reflects a cautious macro outlook for European construction, with management explicitly ruling out a housing recovery in 2026
- The UBS initiation with a Sell rating is notably bearish, suggesting the market may be underestimating headwinds from weak European housing starts and sticky interest rates
- The company's self-help measures (EUR 36 million) partially offset energy cost headwinds, but organic growth is essentially flat
- The Italcer acquisition becomes the primary growth driver in 2026, highlighting the importance of M&A to the investment case
- European building materials companies face a more challenging macro backdrop than their U.S. counterparts, who benefit from tariff-driven pricing power
