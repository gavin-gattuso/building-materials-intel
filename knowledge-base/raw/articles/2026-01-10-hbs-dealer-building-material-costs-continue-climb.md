# HBS Dealer: Building Material Costs Continue to Climb

## Current Price Environment

- Building material costs continue to climb driven by tariff impacts on metals
- Residential construction input prices up **4.2% YoY** as of November 2025
- Metal molding and trim: up **48.2% YoY** -- single largest category increase
- PPI for final demand rose 0.2% in November; final demand energy jumped **4.6%**

## Material Price Bifurcation

### Rising (Tariff-Affected)
- Aluminum mill shapes: +28% to +33% YoY (depending on measurement period)
- Steel mill products: +20.7% YoY
- Copper wire and cable: significant increases
- Metal products broadly: double-digit increases
- Kitchen cabinets/vanities facing 50% tariff (up from 25%)

### Stable or Declining
- Softwood lumber: **-8.0% YoY** (demand weakness)
- Ready-mix concrete: **-0.7% YoY**
- Concrete overall: modest increases due to EPA kiln regulations

## Dealer Channel Impact

- LBM dealers facing unprecedented price volatility in metals
- Inventory management complicated by rapid price changes
- Forward pricing and hedging becoming more important for dealers
- Customers increasingly seeking alternatives to tariff-affected materials
- Pro contractor segment remains active but price-sensitive

## Contractor Response

- Firms integrating data analytics and cloud-based estimating platforms
- ML forecasting models being adopted to anticipate raw material pricing changes
- Growing interest in modular and offsite construction to reduce material exposure
- Some contractors stockpiling certain materials ahead of anticipated tariff increases
