# Owens Corning to Sell Glass Reinforcements Business to Praana Group for $755 Million

## Key Details
- Enterprise value of $755 million for the glass reinforcements business
- Business generated approximately $1.1 billion in revenue in 2024
- Employs approximately 4,000 people across 18 operations in 12 countries
- Buyer is India-based Praana Group
- Transaction expected to close in 2025; as of Feb 2026 earnings call, deal progressing through regulatory approvals
- Owens Corning retains glass nonwovens business (supports Roofing segment) and structural lumber business within Composites segment
- Post-sale, retained Composites assets will be integrated into Roofing and Insulation segments

## Analysis
- This divestiture marks the final step in Owens Corning's transformation from a diversified industrial company to a focused building products platform (Roofing, Insulation, Doors).
- At ~0.7x revenue, the $755M price for a $1.1B revenue business suggests moderate valuation in the composites space.
- The deal streamlines OC's portfolio but reduces geographic diversification, concentrating the company more heavily on North American and European residential/commercial construction cycles.
- Combined with the Masonite acquisition ($3.9B) completed in May 2024, Owens Corning has undergone a dramatic portfolio reshaping in under two years.
