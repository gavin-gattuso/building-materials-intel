# White House Executive Action: Promoting Access to Mortgage Credit -- March 2026

## Executive Action Summary

The administration directed multiple federal financial regulators to consider revising supervisory guidance related to residential construction lending.

## Key Regulatory Changes Directed

### CRE Concentration Guidance
- Federal regulators directed to consider **excluding 1-4 family residential development and construction lending** from commercial real estate concentration guidance
- This would reduce regulatory burden on community banks that finance homebuilding

### Construction Lending Support
- Ensure supervisory expectations support **responsible construction lending** by community banks
- Aimed at expanding credit availability for residential development

## Agencies Involved

- Vice Chairman for Supervision of the Federal Reserve
- Director of the CFPB
- Chairman of the NCUA Board
- Chairperson of the Board of Directors of the FDIC
- Comptroller of the Currency (OCC)

## Potential Impact on Construction

- Community banks are significant lenders for residential construction, especially in smaller markets
- Easing CRE concentration rules could unlock additional lending capacity
- May support housing supply at a time when permits are declining (down 5.8% YoY)
- NAHB and home builders have long advocated for these regulatory changes

## Context

- Comes as Federal Reserve SLOOS data (January 2026) shows banks easing CRE lending standards for the first time since 2022
- Mortgage rates remain elevated at 6.25-6.43% in March
- Builder sentiment (HMI) at 38, well below 50-point optimism threshold
- Housing affordability remains the dominant market challenge
