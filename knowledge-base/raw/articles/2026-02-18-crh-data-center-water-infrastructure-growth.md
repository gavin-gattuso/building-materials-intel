# CRH Stock Outlook for 2026: IIJA, Water, Data Centers Drive Growth

## Key Details
- **Data center exposure**: CRH is active on more than 100 U.S. data center projects
- **Proximity advantage**: ~80% of U.S. data centers are within 25 miles of a CRH facility
- **Water infrastructure**: Management expects high single-digit growth in water quality and flow control in 2026
- U.S. data centers consumed 17 billion gallons of water directly in 2023, with demand projected to rise significantly
- CRH Ventures invested in VODA.ai; Oldcastle Infrastructure partnered through CivilSense platform
- Water demand is less dependent on the housing cycle and tied to essential replacement and regulatory-driven upgrades
- IIJA infrastructure spending continues to accelerate through peak execution phase in 2026

## Analysis
- CRH's strategic positioning near data center hubs creates a durable competitive moat in an AI-driven construction boom
- The data center buildout represents a multi-year demand driver for aggregates, cement, and precast concrete
- Water infrastructure provides a secular growth driver that is recession-resistant and government-funded
- CRH's diversified exposure across infrastructure, data centers, and water differentiates it from pure-play aggregates peers like Vulcan Materials and Martin Marietta
- The convergence of IIJA spending, data center construction, and water infrastructure replacement creates a multi-pronged demand environment for CRH through 2026 and beyond
