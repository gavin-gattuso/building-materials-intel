# Weyerhaeuser Remains A Likely Value Trap

## Key Points
- Weyerhaeuser rated Sell as housing construction weakness continues to pressure lumber demand, pricing, and earnings
- WY owns or controls more than 10 million acres of timberlands in the U.S. with 35 mills or wood products manufacturing facilities
- Lumber prices remain subdued though 2026 could see volatility and upside if mortgage rates decline and tariffs disrupt supply
- REIT structure limits capital allocation flexibility compared to C-corp lumber peers
- Citi maintained Buy rating in February 2026 with raised price target from $30 to $32, contrasting bearish Seeking Alpha view
- Housing starts outlook remains the key swing factor for Weyerhaeuser's 2026 earnings trajectory
- Structural underinvestment in new housing supply could eventually benefit WY but timing remains uncertain
