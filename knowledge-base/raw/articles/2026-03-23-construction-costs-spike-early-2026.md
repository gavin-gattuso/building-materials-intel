# Construction Costs Spike Early in 2026, Signaling Trouble Ahead

## Key Data Points

- Input prices for nonresidential construction surged at **12.6% annualized rate** in first two months of 2026
- This represents one of the sharpest acceleration rates in recent years
- Driven by compounding effects of tariffs, energy crisis, and supply chain disruptions

## Material-Specific Price Movements

### Steel
- Prices rose **11.9%** by end of 2025, with further increases into 2026
- Section 232 tariffs up to **50%** on imports

### Lumber
- National average: **$872/MBF** as of early 2026
- Year-over-year costs up **13%**

### Aluminum
- Jumped nearly **40%** in a single year
- Catastrophic impact on project budgets

### Concrete & Cement
- **4-6%** price increases from EPA kiln regulations
- **25%** tariffs on Mexican and Canadian cement

### Diesel
- Reached **$5.40/gallon** by late March
- Up more than **$1.90/gallon** since start of 2026

## Drivers of Cost Escalation

1. **Strait of Hormuz crisis**: Oil price shock from Iran conflict
2. **Tariffs**: Steel at 50%, aluminum at 50%, copper at 50%, lumber at 10-25%
3. **EPA regulations**: New kiln rules affecting cement production
4. **Data center demand**: Competing for materials and labor
5. **Labor costs**: 8-12% wage growth in construction sector

## Real Estate Development Impact

- Overall project expenses increased **4-6%** for US construction firms
- Aggregate construction costs estimated to rise **8%** under current tariff conditions
- Tariffs alone add approximately **$10,900** to cost of a typical new home (NAHB estimate)
- Project feasibility threatened for marginal developments
