# ENR: Section 122 Tariffs Leave Construction Cost Exposure Largely Intact

## Section 122 Tariff Details
- Invoked within hours of SCOTUS IEEPA ruling on February 20, 2026
- 10% ad valorem surcharge on most imports, effective February 24, 2026
- Later increased to 15%
- Statute limits to 150 days unless Congress acts
- Replaces voided IEEPA tariffs but at lower overall rate for many products

## Remaining Materials-Specific Tariffs (Unaffected by SCOTUS Ruling)
- Steel: up to 50% (Section 232)
- Aluminum: up to 50% (Section 232)
- Copper products: 50%
- Canadian lumber: countervailing and anti-dumping duties
- Canadian/Mexican cement: 25%
- These materials account for major cost drivers in infrastructure, industrial, and multifamily construction

## Net Impact on Construction
- Overall relief limited because materials-specific tariffs remain intact
- Section 122 at 10-15% is lower than the IEEPA reciprocal rates were for many countries
- Specialty equipment, HVAC, electrical systems, and fixtures see modest relief
- Core structural materials (steel, aluminum, lumber, copper, cement) see NO relief
- Construction industry cost exposure "largely intact" per ENR analysis

## Key Tariff Cost Estimates
- NAHB: typical cost effect of tariff actions = $10,900 per home
- Canada supplies ~5 million metric tons of cement to U.S.
- Mexico supplies ~2 million metric tons of cement to U.S.
- Together = over 25% of all U.S. cement imports
- 25% tariff on these imports directly impacts concrete pricing nationwide
