# Weyerhaeuser Completes $459M Timberland Acquisitions, Advances $410M+ Divestitures

## Key Details
- Completed two high-quality acquisitions totaling $459 million in Q3 2025, including North Carolina and Virginia timberlands
- Divestiture #1: 28,000 acres in coastal Oregon sold for $190 million (closed October 2025)
- Divestiture #2: ~86,000 acres in Georgia and Alabama for $220 million (closed December 2025)
- Divestiture #3: ~108,000 acres in Virginia, anticipated to close in early 2026
- Total divestiture proceeds expected to exceed acquisition cash outlay
- Minimal tax liability anticipated on all transactions
- Part of broader strategy to enhance quality and value of timberland holdings

## Analysis
- Weyerhaeuser's portfolio rotation strategy is net cash positive, meaning the company is upgrading its timberland quality without spending incremental capital.
- The Virginia divestiture closing in early 2026 provides a near-term cash infusion that supports the ambitious 2030 growth plan.
- This buy-high-quality/sell-non-core approach is a disciplined capital allocation model that other timberland REITs may seek to emulate.
- The transactions also reflect the growing market for timberland assets driven by carbon credit potential, ESG mandates, and natural capital investment themes.
