# Housing Starts and Building Permits: January 2026 Data Release (Most Recent Available)

## Note on Data Availability

The February 2026 release (originally scheduled for March 17, 2026) and the March 2026 release (originally scheduled for April 17, 2026) have both been **rescheduled for release on April 29, 2026**. This article covers the most recent available data.

## January 2026 Housing Starts

- Privately-owned housing starts: seasonally adjusted annual rate of **1,487,000**
- This is **7.2% above** the revised December estimate of 1,387,000
- This is **9.5% above** the January 2025 rate of 1,358,000
- Strong month-over-month and year-over-year gains

## January 2026 Building Permits

- Building permits: seasonally adjusted annual rate of **1,376,000**
- This is **5.4% below** the revised December rate of 1,455,000
- This is **5.8% below** the January 2025 rate of 1,460,000
- Declining permits suggest future softening in starts

## Market Outlook

- Combined with persistent affordability constraints and mortgage rates firming around 6.1%-6.4%, momentum in homebuilding activity is likely to ease near-term
- The divergence between starts (up) and permits (down) signals a potential turning point
- Builder confidence (NAHB HMI) at 38 in March -- well below the 50-point optimism threshold

## Context

- January spike likely reflects seasonal factors and pull-forward of activity
- Permit declines are a forward-looking indicator suggesting caution ahead
- Tariff-driven material cost increases adding to affordability pressures
- Labor constraints remain a structural brake on housing production
