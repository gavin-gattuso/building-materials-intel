# Holcim Plans 15 Acquisitions in 2026; Xella Deal and European Consolidation Drive Strategy

## Key Details
- **2026 acquisition target**: ~15 deals planned, per CEO Miljan Gutovic
- **Xella acquisition** (signed Oct 2025): European leader in sustainable walling systems (Ytong, Silka, Hebel, Multipor brands)
  - Projected 2026 net sales: EUR 1 billion
  - Adds 4,000 employees across 21 European markets
  - Expected closing: H2 2026, subject to regulatory approvals
- **Recent European bolt-ons**: Thames Materials (UK), A&S Recycling GmbH (Germany), recycler in Northwest France
- **Q1 2026**: Stronger-than-expected quarterly results reported March 22, 2026; solid revenue and profit expansion
- **European consolidation thesis**: Holcim expects smaller players to struggle with sustainability investment costs, creating M&A opportunities
- **UBS upgrade**: Upgraded Holcim to Buy with CHF 78 target (from Neutral/CHF 60), citing "start of a new European cement regime"
- **CFRA**: Raised target to CHF 100; JPMorgan raised to CHF 84

## Analysis
- Holcim is pursuing a dual strategy: inorganic growth through M&A and organic margin expansion post-Amrize
- The Xella acquisition represents a strategic pivot toward higher-value building solutions beyond traditional cement/aggregates
- The European consolidation play is differentiated: Holcim is using its scale and sustainability investment capacity as competitive advantages
- With 15 planned acquisitions, 2026 will be Holcim's most active M&A year in years
- UBS's "new European cement regime" thesis reflects expectations for European volume recovery after years of decline
- The combination of Xella, Pacasmayo, and CEMEX Colombia assets could add >EUR 2B in annual revenue
