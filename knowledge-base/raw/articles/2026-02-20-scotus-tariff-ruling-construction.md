# Supreme Court Strikes Down IEEPA Tariffs; Construction Impact Mixed

## The Ruling (February 20, 2026)
- U.S. Supreme Court issued 6-3 decision in Learning Resources Inc. v. Trump
- Court held the President is NOT authorized to impose tariffs under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA)
- Rejected Trump's claim to authority for reciprocal tariffs against the rest of the world
- Removed legal basis for much of the administration's 2025 trade program

## Immediate Impact on Construction
- Provides "modest but meaningful reduction in materials price escalation" for specialty equipment, HVAC and electrical systems and fixtures (per Anirban Basu, ABC chief economist)
- However, tariffs on lumber, steel, aluminum, and copper remain in place and were NOT affected by the court's decision
- Those materials account for major cost drivers in infrastructure, industrial, and multifamily construction
- Net relief to the construction industry is limited

## Administration Response
- Within hours of the ruling, White House invoked Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974
- Imposed 10% ad valorem surcharge on most imports effective February 24, 2026
- Statute authorizes surcharge of up to 15% and limits it to 150 days unless Congress acts
- Later increased to 15%
- All existing tariffs (steel 50%, aluminum 50%, copper 50%, lumber tariffs) remain in place

## Key Tariff Rates Still in Effect Post-Ruling
- Steel: up to 50% tariff
- Aluminum: up to 50% tariff
- Copper products: 50% tariff
- Canadian/Mexican cement: 25% tariff
- Section 122 global surcharge: 10-15% on most imports

## Industry Reaction
- AGC warned members they are unlikely to see refunds for materials purchased during the past year
- Brian Turmail (AGC VP of Public Affairs): "We have been cautioning our members for some time now that it is unlikely they will see any refunds"
- Builders estimate typical cost effect from recent tariff actions at $10,900 per home (NAHB estimate)
- Ongoing uncertainty remains the primary concern for contractors

## Budget Impact
- Administration's tariffs projected to raise ~$1.3 trillion over 2026-2035
- Net dynamic revenue (accounting for slower growth): ~$1.1 trillion
- Yale Budget Lab tracked tariff state as of February 20-21, 2026
