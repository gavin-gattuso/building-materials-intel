# February 2026 PPI: Building Material Prices Entrenched Above 3% Growth

## Key Details
- Price index for inputs to new residential construction rose **0.7%** in February
- Year-over-year increase: **3.4%**
- Goods used in new residential construction: **+1.1% month-over-month**, +3.0% year-over-year
- Services component: **+0.1% month-over-month**, +4.2% year-over-year
- Building material prices: **+0.6% month-over-month**, +3.5% year-over-year
- Construction input prices climbed at a **12.6% annualized rate** during January-February 2026 combined

## Notable Category Movements
- Metal products showed the largest year-over-year increases
- Metal molding and trim: **+61.7%** year-over-year
- All metals and metal products: **+16.6%** year-over-year
- Lumber: relatively flat, providing some offset
- Concrete/cement: up approximately **6%** from two years ago but minimal monthly change

## Analysis
- The 12.6% annualized rate for the first two months of 2026 is described as "staggering" by Construction Dive
- Building material price growth has been "entrenched above 3%" per NAHB analysis, creating a structural cost floor
- The divergence between metal prices (soaring on tariffs) and wood products (stabilizing) is widening
- Builders face difficulty accurately pricing homes when material costs are volatile and unpredictable
- NAHB data shows builders adding an estimated **$10,900** to the cost of a new home due to tariff-driven material increases
- March PPI data scheduled for release April 14, 2026
