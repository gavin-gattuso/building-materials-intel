# Deloitte 2026 Engineering and Construction Industry Outlook

## Market Outlook

- E&C industry enters 2026 confronting rising material costs, persistent labor shortages, and shifting project demand
- Investment in structures expected to pivot from 2025 decline to modest growth of **~1.8%** in 2026
- Industry faces persistent inflation, high interest rates, rising material costs, supply-chain disruptions, and acute labor shortages

## Growth Drivers

### Data Centers & AI Infrastructure
- Surge in demand for AI-driven data center projects providing powerful new momentum
- Energy infrastructure demand tied to grid modernization
- Record demand for power-related construction

### Federal Spending
- IIJA and CHIPS Act continuing to drive public-sector construction
- Bipartisan support for infrastructure investment remains intact

## Key Challenges

### Labor Crisis
- By **2031**, **41% of construction workers** expected to retire
- Only **10%** of current workers are **under 25**
- Projections indicate shortage of over **2 million skilled craft professionals** by 2028 if trends continue
- Wages rising 6-8% annually, compressing margins

### Cost Pressures
- Elevated tariff rates on key materials raising costs
- Supply disruption prompting more strategic procurement and contract risk management

## Technology Transformation

- Firms must transition from traditional, labor-intensive models to digitally-enabled ecosystems
- Key technologies: **agentic AI**, building information modeling (BIM), robotics
- **71%** of businesses will integrate AI agents into departments by 2026
- Digital Project Delivery (DPD) moving from innovation strategy to contractually expected standard
- Companies adopting smart helmets and wearable sensors reported up to **35% reduction** in workplace accidents and **20% boost** in worker efficiency
- 3D concrete printing can reduce labor requirements by **50-70%** and cut material waste by up to **40%**

## Strategic M&A

- E&C companies using M&A to modernize operations, capture opportunity, build domestic capacity
- Deal activity expected to increase as firms tackle labor shortages, manage tariffs, capture data center growth
