# Consumer Confidence Rises Marginally to 91.8 in March 2026, But Expectations Signal Recession Risk

## Conference Board Consumer Confidence Index -- March 2026

- Overall CCI: **91.8** (up 0.8 points from 91.0 in February)
- Present Situation Index: **123.3** (up 4.6 points)
- Expectations Index: **70.9** (down 1.7 points)

## Recession Warning Signal

- A level of **80 or below** for the Expectations Index historically signals a recession within the next year
- The Expectations Index has been **below 80 since February 2025** -- over 13 consecutive months
- This is a critical warning sign for construction demand outlook

## University of Michigan Consumer Sentiment

- Michigan Consumer Sentiment Index (MCSI): **53.3** (dropped sharply)
- The divergence between CCI (91.8) and MCSI (53.3) reflects different measurement methodologies

## GDP Outlook

- Conference Board revised GDP growth down by **0.1 ppt to 2.0% y/y** for 2026
- This will be lower than growth in 2025
- Geopolitical conflict (Iran/Strait of Hormuz) adding significant uncertainty

## Consumer Spending Patterns

- Improved financial sentiment did NOT translate to higher spending intent in March
- Discretionary spending intent pulled back well below the 2021 baseline
- Nondiscretionary intent softened month over month
- This signals potential headwinds for residential construction and renovation demand

## Deloitte State of the US Consumer (March 2026)

- Consumer pulse shows growing economic pressures
- Critical maritime chokepoint disruption (Strait of Hormuz) causing global anxiety
- Brent crude skyrocketed 47% in less than a month to peak near $116/barrel
