# NAHB/Wells Fargo Housing Market Index: Builder Sentiment Inches Higher to 38 in March 2026

## March 2026 HMI Results

- Overall HMI: **38** (up 1 point from revised February reading)
- This marks the **23rd consecutive month** below the 50-point threshold indicating favorable conditions

### Component Indices
- Current Sales Conditions: **42** (up 1 point)
- Future Sales Expectations: **49** (up 2 points)
- Traffic of Prospective Buyers: **25** (up 3 points)

## Price Reductions and Incentives

- **37%** of builders cut prices in March (up from 36% in February)
- Average price reduction remained stable at **6%**
- **64%** of builders using sales incentives in March (down 1 point from February)
- **12th consecutive month** the incentive share has exceeded 60%

## Key Concerns

- Elevated construction costs remain a primary drag on sentiment
- Shortages of buildable lots persist
- Labor shortages continue to constrain activity
- Affordability remains the dominant challenge

## Regional Data (3-month moving averages)

- Northeast: 47
- Midwest: 37
- South: 39
- West: 33

## Context

- Builder sentiment entered 2026 at 42 in January before declining
- The index has been below 50 since mid-2024
- Mortgage rates averaging 6.25%-6.43% through March continue to pressure demand
- Tariff-driven material cost increases adding to builder cost concerns
