# Building Materials Q4 2025 Earnings: Mixed Results Amid Economic Pressures

## Sector Overview

- 9 building materials stocks tracked reported a slower Q4
- Revenues missed analysts consensus estimates by **1.2%**
- Next quarter revenue guidance was in line with expectations
- Average share prices **down 10.2%** since latest earnings results

## Individual Company Results

### Armstrong World Industries
- Q4 Revenue: **$388.3 million** (up 5.6% YoY)
- **Missed** analyst estimates by 3%

### Carlisle Companies
- Q4 Revenue: **$1.13 billion** (flat YoY)
- **Beat** analyst estimates by 1.4%
- Strong quarter with impressive beat on adjusted operating income

### Martin Marietta Materials
- Revenue increased YoY but **below** analyst expectations
- Full-year revenue guidance **significantly missed** analyst expectations

### Vulcan Materials
- Q4 Revenue: **$1.91 billion** (up 3.2% YoY)
- **Missed** analyst estimates by 1.7%

### Doman Building Materials
- Full-year 2025 revenue: **$3.1 billion** (up 17.1% from $2.7 billion in 2024)

### Lowe's (Reported Feb 25, 2026)
- Q4 results to be released alongside FBM integration update

## Key Takeaways

- Revenue misses concentrated in aggregates/heavy materials (Martin Marietta, Vulcan)
- Specialty/value-added companies (Carlisle) outperforming
- Stock price declines of 10%+ suggest market pricing in continued headwinds
- Tariff costs and slowing residential demand weighing on forward guidance
- Q1 2026 guidance generally in-line, suggesting cautious but not deteriorating outlook
