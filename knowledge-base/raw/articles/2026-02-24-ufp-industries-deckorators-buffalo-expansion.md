# UFP Industries Targets $100M Deckorators Sales Jump in 2026; Buffalo Plant Coming Online

## Key Details
- $77 million investment in 30-acre site in Buffalo, New York for Deckorators manufacturing
- Buffalo facility expected online by end of Q1 2026; full optimization by H2 2026
- Doubles production capacity for Surestone composite decking product
- Part of $250 million five-year plan to expand Deckorators product line
- Selma, Alabama plant already fully operational
- Deckorators projected to add $100 million in incremental sales for 2026
- Initial stocking orders, upgraded manufacturing capacity, and expanded distribution support 2026 momentum
- Company completed two acquisitions totaling ~$24 million in historical sales in 2025
- Active M&A pipeline, especially in fragmented packaging industry
- DA Davidson maintains Buy rating with $110 price target (down from $112)

## Analysis
- The Buffalo facility opening in Q1 2026 is a major capacity catalyst that positions UFP to capitalize on the secular shift from treated lumber decking to composite alternatives.
- The $250M five-year investment plan signals management's conviction that composite decking is a multi-decade growth opportunity, not a cyclical trend.
- UFP's asset-light model (no foreign sawmills) provides natural insulation from the Canadian lumber tariff issues plaguing competitors.
- The company's strategy of pairing organic Deckorators growth with bolt-on acquisitions in packaging creates a diversified growth profile less dependent on housing starts.
