# S&P Global Industry Credit Outlook 2026: Building Materials

## Key Assessment

- S&P anticipates a **modest recovery** in building materials, skewed toward the **second half** of the year
- Steady rebound in residential markets expected with some pickup in renovation
- Lagging new build activity in most regions

## Regional Outlook

### United States
- High home prices and higher-than-expected mortgage rates pricing many buyers out
- Housing shortage of 3.7 million units provides long-term demand floor
- Tariff-driven cost increases creating margin pressure for producers

### Europe/EMEA
- Prices expected to increase **low-single-digit percent**
- Supported by resilient job market, improving consumer confidence, stable but elevated mortgage rates

### China
- Primary property sales will **decline further** in 2026
- Nationwide sales dropping amid soft demand
- Negative demand outlook for cement, steel, glass producers serving Chinese market

## Broader Economic Context

- S&P forecasts stable global economic expansion of **3.2%** in 2026
- Growth slows in U.S. and China
- Eurozone continues to recover
- Emerging markets prolong resilient streak

## Credit Implications

- Building materials sector credit quality stable overall
- Companies with strong balance sheets and diversified geographic exposure best positioned
- Tariff-exposed subsectors (metals, imported goods) face greatest margin pressure
- R&R spending durability supports credit profiles of renovation-oriented companies
