# Recession Probability Estimates for 2026: Range from 20% to 42%

## Key Details - Probability Estimates by Institution
| Institution | Recession Probability (Next 12 Months) | Change |
|------------|---------------------------------------|--------|
| Goldman Sachs | **20%** | Down from 30% |
| RSM | **30%** | Down from 40% |
| Polymarket (prediction market) | **29%** | -- |
| J.P. Morgan | **35%** | -- |
| Moody's | **42%** | -- |

## GDP Growth Forecasts
- Goldman Sachs: **2.5% Q4/Q4** growth (above consensus of 2.1%)
- Full-year 2026: **2.8%** on a full-year basis (Goldman)
- Consensus estimate: **2.2%** real GDP growth
- Morgan Stanley: US resilience expected to lead global growth in 2026

## Key Risk Factors
- **Iran conflict and oil shock:** stagflation risk from sustained energy price increases
- **Tariff impacts:** 50% tariffs on metals creating inflationary pressure and demand destruction
- **Labor market softening:** unemployment ticking up to 4.3%
- **Consumer confidence weakness:** Expectations Index below 80 for three consecutive months
- **Q4 2025 GDP:** revised down to 0.7% -- sharp deceleration
- **Geopolitical uncertainty:** "fat tail" distribution of outcomes per Mohamed El-Erian

## Positive Factors
- Consumer spending still expanding, though moderating
- Fiscal policy providing some tailwind
- Fed has room to cut rates if recession risk materializes
- Construction spending at $2.19 trillion SAAR

## Analysis
- The wide range of estimates (20-42%) reflects genuine uncertainty about the economic path
- The Moody's estimate at 42% is approaching coin-flip territory -- the highest among major forecasters
- For building materials: a recession would hit residential construction hardest (new homes and remodeling), while infrastructure spending (IIJA-backed) would provide a floor
- The Iran conflict adds a tail risk that could push recession probability higher if the oil shock persists or deepens
- Companies with diversified end-market exposure (both residential and infrastructure) are best positioned to weather a potential downturn
- Monitoring: Q1 2026 GDP advance estimate (expected late April) will be critical for assessing whether recession risks are materializing
