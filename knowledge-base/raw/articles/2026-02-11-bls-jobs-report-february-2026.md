# February 2026 Employment Situation: Construction Adds 33,000 Jobs in January

## Key Details
- **Construction employment:** +33,000 jobs in January 2026
- Nonresidential specialty trade contractors drove the bulk of gains: **+25,000**
- Total nonfarm payroll employment showed continued moderate growth
- Unemployment rate held steady
- Average hourly earnings for construction workers continued to outpace broader economy

## Construction Sector Breakdown
- Nonresidential specialty trade contractors: **+25,000** (strongest subsector)
- Residential building and specialty trade: modest gains
- Heavy and civil engineering construction: relatively flat
- Construction employment showed little net change over the prior 12 months despite monthly volatility

## Wage Trends
- Average hourly earnings for all employees rose modestly
- Construction sector wages continued to grow above the national average, reflecting persistent labor shortages
- Annual wage growth in construction remained elevated above **4%**

## Analysis
- The 33,000-job gain in construction was solid and showed particular strength in commercial/nonresidential work
- Data center construction and infrastructure projects are driving demand for specialty trade contractors (electricians, HVAC, etc.)
- The ABC workforce shortage report (349,000 workers needed in 2026) underscores that job gains are supply-constrained, not demand-constrained
- For building materials companies, healthy construction employment signals continued demand for materials, though labor costs continue to pressure contractor margins
