# PwC: Engineering and Construction US Deals 2026 Outlook

## Deal Activity Expectations

- Deal activity expected to increase in 2026
- E&C M&A increasingly anchored in **infrastructure, clean energy, and energy security**
- Investors positioning for long-duration demand tied to **grid modernization**, **data center buildouts**, and **AI-enabled infrastructure**
- **U.S.-targeted M&A surged 66%** in H2 2025 as firms adjusted to Build America, Buy America provisions

## Strategic Drivers

### Domestic Capacity Building
- Buyers adapting to U.S. trade and industrial policy by using M&A to strengthen domestic capacity
- Reducing supply chain risk through vertical integration and reshoring

### Labor & Technology
- M&A used as a tool to tackle labor shortages (acquiring firms with specialized workforces)
- Technology acquisitions to modernize operations and improve productivity

### Growth Sectors
- AI driving surge in **data center and digital infrastructure** projects
- Record demand for **power-related construction** and **grid modernization**
- Infrastructure, clean energy, and energy security forming core investment thesis

## Market Challenges

- Rising material costs and shortages of skilled labor compressing margins
- M&A emerging as both a **defensive** and **strategic** tool
- Tariff uncertainty affecting deal timing and valuations

## 2026 Outlook

- Expected to be a **growth year** for global E&C M&A
- Deal flow supported by strong infrastructure pipeline, data center demand, and energy transition
- Companies using M&A not just to withstand volatility, but to capture structural opportunities
