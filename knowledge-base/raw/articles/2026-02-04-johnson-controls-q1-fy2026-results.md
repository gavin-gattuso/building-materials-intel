# Johnson Controls Reports Strong Q1 FY2026: EPS Up 40%, Raises Full-Year Guidance

## Key Details
- Q1 FY2026 sales of $5.8 billion, up 7% (organic +6%)
- GAAP EPS of $0.90; adjusted EPS of $0.89, up nearly 40% YoY
- Adjusted EBIT margins expanded 190 basis points to 12.4%
- Record backlog of $18 billion, up 20% YoY
- Orders increased nearly 40%, building on 16% growth prior year
- Americas adjusted segment EBITDA margin: 16.4% (up 20 bps)
- EMEA EBITDA margin: 13.5% (up 120 bps)
- APAC EBITDA margin: 16.9% (up 290 bps)
- Americas led by 56% data center growth from large-scale projects
- More than 1,000 employees engaged in business system transformation
- Over 80 kaizens completed; 350 senior leaders trained
- Net debt at 2.2x, within long-term target range
- Cash available: approximately $600 million
- Raised FY2026 adjusted EPS guidance to approximately $4.70 (up ~25% YoY)

## Analysis
- Johnson Controls' Q1 is the strongest evidence yet that its transformation is working. The 40% EPS growth, 190 bps margin expansion, and record $18B backlog represent a fundamental inflection in the company's trajectory. The post-Bosch divestiture portfolio (60% commercial HVAC, 40% fire and security) is proving to be a higher-quality, faster-growth business. The data center opportunity (56% Americas growth) is a transformative demand driver. The guidance raise to $4.70 EPS reflects management's high confidence level.
