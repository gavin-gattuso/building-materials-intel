# IIJA Infrastructure Spending Reaches Peak Execution Phase in 2026

## IIJA Funding Status

- Total IIJA allocation: approximately **$568 billion** across **68,000+ projects**
- Agencies have obligated **$275 billion** (47% of available funds)
- Outlayed: **$119 billion**
- Remaining for FY2026: **$131.2 billion** (18%) available for new obligations

## 2026: Year of Shovels in the Ground

- 2026 characterized as peak disbursement year for IIJA funds
- **40% year-over-year increase** in Heavy Civil solicitations compared to 2024
- FHWA notes IIJA provides approximately **$350 billion** for federal highway programs across FY2022-FY2026

## Primary Funding Categories

| Category | Allocation |
|----------|-----------|
| Road/Bridge repair | **$110 billion** |
| Water Infrastructure | **$55 billion** |
| Public Transit | ~$39 billion |
| Broadband | ~$65 billion |
| Clean Energy | ~$62 billion |

## Distribution Model

- **90%** of funding distributed via state formula grants (not direct federal contracts)
- Contractors should focus on State DOT lettings for opportunities
- Peak construction activity expected through 2026-2027

## CHIPS Act and Data Centers

- CHIPS Act megaprojects and data center construction redefining US construction investments
- Manufacturing construction spending driven by semiconductor and data center facilities
- Combined government-incentivized construction creating significant demand for materials and labor

## Key Challenge

- High construction cost inflation has reduced the effective purchasing power of IIJA investments
- The ability to build projects has been limited by faster-than-inflation increases in labor and materials costs
- Net impact of infrastructure spending partially offset by rising project costs
