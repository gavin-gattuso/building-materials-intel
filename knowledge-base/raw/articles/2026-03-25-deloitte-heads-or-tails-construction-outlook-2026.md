# Deloitte: Heads or Tails -- 2026 Construction Industry Outlook

## Key Findings

- E&C industry enters 2026 confronting **rising material costs, persistent labor shortages, and shifting project demand**
- Recent tariffs on steel and aluminum reaching up to **50%** have sharply raised construction material costs
- Effective tariff rate for construction goods climbed to a **40-year high of 25-30%** in 2025

## Material Cost Impact

- Longer-term tariff impacts expected to range from **5% to 25%** depending on material type
- Aggregate construction costs estimated to rise roughly **8%** under current policy conditions
- Input prices for nonresidential construction surged at a **12.6% annualized rate** during the first two months of 2026
- Construction input prices increased **0.7% month-to-month** in January due to tariff-affected materials (copper wire, cable, iron, steel, industrial controls)

## Project Abandonment

- **88.2% year-over-year increase** in project abandonment activity in August 2025 due to elevated costs
- Tariffs squeezing smaller contractors disproportionately

## Supply Chain Strategies

- Firms prioritizing **increasing U.S. sourcing** to reduce tariff exposure
- Investing in **cloud-based supply chain visibility**
- Using **formal indexed pricing tied to published cost benchmarks**
- Outsourcing procurement functions via engineering, procurement, and construction contracts or hybrid models
- Adopting **advanced digital platforms** integrating tariff data, freight information, and material forecasts for predictive purchasing

## Growth Bright Spots

- Investment in structures projected to pivot from a 2025 decline to **modest growth (+1.8%)** in 2026
- **AI-driven data center construction** and associated energy infrastructure driving segment growth
- Firms increasingly deploying advanced digital tools (AI, BIM, digital twins, IoT) to improve planning, safety, and project delivery
