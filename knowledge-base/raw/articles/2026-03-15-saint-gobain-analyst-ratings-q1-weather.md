# BofA Trims Saint-Gobain Forecasts on Q1 Weather Headwinds; Mixed Analyst Action

## Key Details
- BofA Global Research trimmed Q1 2026 forecasts, citing bad weather impact in first two months of 2026
- Management warned H1 2026 margins and volumes will be weaker, particularly in North America
- Normalization expected from Q2 2026, with positive momentum in Europe and strong growth in Asia/LatAm
- Citi raised price target to EUR 120 from EUR 112, maintaining Buy rating
- Jefferies maintained Buy with EUR 126 target
- RBC Capital lowered target to EUR 93 from EUR 96
- Morgan Stanley lowered target to EUR 100 from EUR 105
- Berenberg lowered target to EUR 105 from EUR 110
- Consensus: Buy from 19 analysts; 13 Buy, 5 Hold, 1 Sell
- Average 12-month price target: EUR 104.28 (range: EUR 78 - EUR 137)
- Implied upside of ~21% from current levels

## Analysis
- The divergent analyst actions (Citi and Jefferies raising targets while RBC/Morgan Stanley/Berenberg cut) reflect the tension between near-term weather/macro headwinds and Saint-Gobain's compelling long-term strategic positioning.
- Q1 2026 will likely be the trough quarter given weather impacts, creating a potential entry point for investors ahead of the expected H2 improvement.
- The 21% implied upside to consensus targets suggests the market may be overly discounting the near-term weakness.
