# AIA Consensus Construction Forecast January 2026: Modest 1% Growth Expected

## Key Projections
- Overall nonresidential building spending growth: **+1.0%** in 2026, **+2.2%** in 2027
- These figures are nominal (unadjusted for inflation), implying flat or declining real spending
- Many firms expected to be "doing more with less -- if they're growing at all"

## Sector Breakdown

### Commercial Facilities
- 2026: **+3.0%** growth
- 2027: **+3.5%** growth
- Data centers expected to experience strong growth over the next two years
- Traditional office spending (excluding data centers) projected to decline sharply

### Manufacturing
- 2026: **-3.9%** decline
- 2027: **-0.9%** decline
- Post-CHIPS Act initial investment wave fading

### Institutional Facilities
- 2026: **+2.7%** growth
- 2027: **+2.8%** growth
- Education and healthcare driving modest growth

## Key Challenges Identified
- Tariffs and cost of goods remain top concern
- Labor availability constraints persist
- High interest rates suppressing activity
- Federal spending cutbacks creating uncertainty
- Nonresidential construction faces prolonged weakness through 2027

## Building Materials Implications
- Flat real spending growth limits volume upside for aggregates, cement, and steel suppliers
- Data center construction is the primary bright spot -- benefits CRH, Vulcan, Martin Marietta (aggregates), Nucor, Steel Dynamics (steel)
- Manufacturing decline offsets some infrastructure gains
- Companies with institutional exposure (schools, hospitals) positioned for steady if unspectacular demand
