# West Fraser to Indefinitely Curtail High Level, Alberta OSB Mill; $200M Impairment

## Key Details
- Indefinite curtailment of oriented strand board (OSB) mill in High Level, Alberta in spring 2026
- Reduces production capacity by 860 million square feet (3/8-inch basis)
- Approximately 190 employees affected; company to offer positions at other operations
- $200 million asset impairment loss recorded in Q4 2025
- Additionally, Cordele, Georgia OSB facility one production line (440 million sq ft capacity) remains indefinitely idled since late 2023
- Total curtailed OSB capacity: ~1.3 billion square feet
- Previously announced permanent closures: Augusta, GA and 100 Mile House, BC lumber mills by end of 2025
- Huttig, AR and Lake Butler, FL lumber mill curtailments made permanent; mills to be dismantled

## Analysis
- West Fraser's cascade of mill closures and curtailments reflects the most severe capacity rationalization in the Canadian lumber industry in years.
- The 1.3B sq ft of curtailed OSB capacity represents a significant supply withdrawal that, if sustained, could eventually support price recovery in the OSB market.
- The permanent nature of several closures (Augusta, 100 Mile House, Huttig, Lake Butler) signals that management views some capacity as structurally uneconomic, not just cyclically challenged.
- The High Level curtailment is particularly significant as a Canadian OSB mill closure, reflecting the compound impact of low OSB prices, carbon taxes, and distance-to-market costs.
