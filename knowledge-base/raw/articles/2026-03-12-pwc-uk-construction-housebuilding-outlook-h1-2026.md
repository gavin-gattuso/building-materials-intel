# PwC UK: Construction and Housebuilding Outlook H1 2026

## Key Findings

- PwC's sixth edition of the Construction and Housebuilding Outlook provides a quantified view on the UK's spend outlook by segment
- After **two years of contraction**, the sector returned to modest growth in 2025, primarily driven by public investment in health, education, and infrastructure
- Private housing and commercial activity remained subdued

## Growth Forecasts

- **2026**: 1.7% output growth
- **2027**: 2.9% output growth
- **2028**: 3.5% output growth
- Growth in 2026 expected to remain measured as private sector confidence continues to recover

## Sector Breakdown

### Infrastructure (Key Growth Driver)
- Infrastructure, led by activity in the **energy and water sectors**, set to be a key driver for UK growth over the coming three-year period
- Transport activity remains subdued

### Residential
- Residential construction should strengthen gradually
- **Affordability constraints** will limit the pace of expansion

### Commercial
- Commercial activity is likely to improve more slowly
- Reflects cautious investor sentiment and soft development pipelines

## Key Themes

- Efficiencies and digital enablement critical to leverage infrastructure growth
- Public investment continues to be the primary engine of sector recovery
- Private sector recovery remains gradual and uneven
