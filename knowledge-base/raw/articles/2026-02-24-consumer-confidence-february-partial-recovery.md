# Consumer Confidence February 2026: Inches Up 2.2 Points to 91.2

## Key Details
- Consumer Confidence Index: **91.2** in February (up from revised 89.0 in January)
- Month-over-month change: **+2.2 points**
- Present Situation Index: **120.0** (down 1.8 points from January)
- Expectations Index: **72.0** (up 4.8 points from January)
- January revised up from 84.5 to **89.0**

## Trend Analysis
- The January revision (from 84.5 to 89.0) moderates the severity of the January plunge
- February's 91.2 shows a partial recovery but remains well below the December level
- Expectations Index at 72.0 still below the **80-point recession warning threshold**
- Present Situation Index continued to decline even as Expectations improved

## Analysis
- The mixed picture -- improving expectations but deteriorating present situation -- suggests consumers believe current conditions are tough but may improve
- The partial recovery is encouraging but the index remains historically weak
- For building materials: the slight improvement could support spring renovation activity but the overall reading is still suppressed
- Home improvement retailers (Home Depot, Lowe's) are sensitive to consumer confidence trends
- The key question is whether the March data (released March 25) shows continued improvement or a reversal due to the Iran conflict
