# Strengthening Global Materials Supply Chains Amid New Risks -- March 2026

## Current Supply Chain Challenges

The construction industry faces a cascading failure across multiple supply chains in early 2026, driven by:

1. **Strait of Hormuz closure**: 20% of world's oil and LNG supply disrupted
2. **Tariff escalation**: Steel/aluminum at 50%, copper at 50%, lumber at 10-25%
3. **Geopolitical trade wars**: US-China, US-Canada, US-Mexico tensions
4. **Energy price shock**: Diesel from $3.49 to $5.40/gallon in Q1 2026

## Material Price Impacts

| Material | Price Change | Driver |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Steel | +11.9% (2025) + further 2026 increases | 50% tariff + demand |
| Aluminum | +40% YoY | Tariffs + energy costs |
| Lumber | +13% YoY ($872/MBF) | 10-25% tariffs |
| Cement | +4-6% | EPA regs + 25% tariff on imports |
| Diesel | +$1.90/gal since Jan 2026 | Strait of Hormuz |
| Copper | Significant increases | 50% tariff |

## Industry Response Strategies

### Near-Term
- Increased US sourcing to reduce tariff exposure
- Formal indexed pricing tied to published cost benchmarks
- Contract escalation clauses for material cost pass-through
- Cloud-based supply chain visibility tools

### Medium-Term
- Diversification of supplier base beyond tariff-affected regions
- Investment in domestic manufacturing capacity
- Strategic stockpiling of critical materials
- Prefabrication to reduce on-site material waste

## Deloitte 2026 E&C Industry Outlook

- The E&C industry enters 2026 confronting rising material costs, persistent labor shortages, and shifting project demand
- Firms prioritizing supply chain resilience and digital transformation
- Input cost management is the #1 operational challenge for contractors
