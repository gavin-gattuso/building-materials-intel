# Capstone Partners Building Products M&A Update: Poised for Growth in 2026

## 2025 Deal Activity

- Building products M&A deal volume fell **20.8% YoY**: from 265 transactions (YTD 2024) to 210 (YTD 2025)
- Decline driven by tariff-based uncertainty reducing overall transaction volume
- However, 2025 included several marquee strategic transactions and strong PE platform formation
- A **30% increase in deal volume** expected for 2026

## Market Drivers for 2026

- Lower interest rates expected to boost activity and valuations
- Acceleration in **commercial and institutional construction planning** expected to further prop up deal flow
- Booming **data center construction** likely to uphold momentum
- Pent-up demand for new residential construction
- Accelerated planning within institutional and commercial end-markets

## Key Themes

- Large-scale building products M&A expected to persist despite trade policy volatility
- Private equity platform formation remains active in building products
- Distribution sector undergoing rapid consolidation (QXO, Home Depot/SRS, Lowe's/FBM)
- Specialty building products companies commanding premium valuations
- Mid-market activity (enterprise values $50M-$500M) expected to accelerate

## Valuation Trends

- Building products multiples remained healthy despite market uncertainty
- Companies with strong recurring revenue (R&R-exposed), proprietary products, and technology capabilities commanding highest valuations
- Tariff uncertainty creating some buyer-seller valuation gaps in import-exposed businesses

## Sector Outlook

Capstone expects building products M&A to be one of the most active industrial subsectors in 2026, driven by structural housing demand, infrastructure investment, and the ongoing digital transformation of the construction value chain.
