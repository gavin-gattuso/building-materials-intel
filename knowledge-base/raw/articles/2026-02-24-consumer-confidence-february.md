# Consumer Confidence Inches Up to 91.2 in February 2026

## February 2026 Index Data
- Consumer Confidence Index: 91.2 (up modestly from January)
- Expectations Index: 72.0 (up 4.8 points from January)
- Level below 80 historically signals recession within the next year
- Expectations Index has been below 80 since February 2025 (full 12 months)

## Key Findings
- Confidence ticked up after falling in January
- Consumers' pessimistic expectations for the future eased somewhat
- However, improvements were modest and readings remain historically weak
- Buying plans rose in February despite weak Expectations Index
  - Suggests households separating near-term purchase decisions from longer-term economic worry

## Concern Areas
- Persistent concerns about inflation
- Job security worries
- Broader economic outlook pessimism
- Sentiment indicators notably weaker over past year
- Confidence remains well below levels associated with strong consumption growth

## Labor Market Signals
- Job growth has slowed
- Hiring intentions have softened
- Layoffs picked up in technology, financial services, and parts of manufacturing
- Labor market cooling is reducing source of consumer resilience

## Implications for Construction/Housing
- Weak consumer confidence weighs on home purchase decisions
- Sub-80 Expectations Index is a recession warning signal
- However, rising buying plans could support near-term housing demand
- Real GDP growth expected to accelerate in spring
- Overall economic picture presents challenges for residential construction demand
