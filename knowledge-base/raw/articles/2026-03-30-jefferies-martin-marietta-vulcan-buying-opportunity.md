# Jefferies Calls Martin Marietta and Vulcan Materials Buying Opportunities Amid Energy Spike

## Key Details
- **Date**: Late March 2026
- **Analyst**: Jefferies
- **Recommendation**: Both Martin Marietta (MLM) and Vulcan Materials (VMC) identified as buying opportunities
- **Catalyst**: Temporary energy price spike creating short-term stock weakness
- **MLM consensus**: Moderate Buy; average price target $689.40
- **MLM price target actions**: Morgan Stanley $702 (Overweight); Citigroup raised to $804
- **VMC consensus**: Buy (12 analysts as of Feb 2026)
- **VMC average price target**: $334.67 (high $348, low $316)

## Analysis
- Jefferies' call reflects a view that energy cost spikes create temporary margin pressure but do not impair the long-term structural story
- Aggregates producers can typically pass through energy cost increases with a lag, preserving margin over time
- The temporary stock weakness creates an entry point for long-term investors in high-quality aggregates assets
- Both MLM and VMC benefit from the same structural tailwinds: IIJA spending, limited new supply, and pricing power
- The wide spread between Citigroup's $804 target and Morgan Stanley's $702 for MLM reflects differing views on the Quikrete deal's accretion
- VMC's Investor Day targets ($20/ton, $4.5-$5.0B EBITDA) provide a clear long-term framework for valuation
