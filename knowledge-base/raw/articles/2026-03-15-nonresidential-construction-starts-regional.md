# Nonresidential Construction Starts Regional Analysis -- March 2026

## National Overview

- Nonresidential construction spending at seasonally adjusted annual rate of **$728.2 billion** (January 2026)
- Down **0.4%** from revised December estimate
- Nonresidential building spending expected to remain sluggish through 2026
- Public sector lifting nonresidential spending as private projects lag

## Sector Dynamics

### Data Centers -- The Dominant Story
- Nonresidential construction spending is "all about the 1s and 0s"
- Data center and technology-related construction driving manufacturing numbers
- Data center construction remains the standout bright spot

### Public Sector
- Government construction buoyed by IIJA peak disbursement
- Heavy Civil solicitations up **40% YoY** vs 2024
- State DOT lettings leading highway construction activity

### Private Sector Weakness
- Office construction continues post-pandemic adjustment
- Retail construction subdued
- Hotel/hospitality sector cautious

## AIA Consensus Construction Forecast

- AIA forecast reflects nonresidential construction faces slowing growth
- Widening sector imbalances between public infrastructure (strong) and private commercial (weak)
- Continued economic uncertainty dampening private investment

## Census Bureau Data Note

- January 2026 construction spending data released
- March 2026 construction spending report rescheduled to May 7, 2026
- Data delays creating additional uncertainty for market participants
