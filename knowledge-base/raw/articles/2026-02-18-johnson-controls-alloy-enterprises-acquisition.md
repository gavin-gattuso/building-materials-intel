# Johnson Controls Signs Agreement to Acquire Alloy Enterprises for Data Center Thermal Management

## Key Details
- Agreement signed February 18, 2026 to acquire Alloy Enterprises (Boston-based)
- Alloy specializes in next-generation thermal management platform for high-performance data centers
- Founded in 2020
- Proprietary platform with advanced direct liquid cooling components
- Up to 35% improvement in thermal management efficiency
- Up to 75% reduction in pressure drop (fluid flows more easily)
- Financial terms not disclosed
- Transaction expected to close in fiscal Q3, subject to regulatory approvals
- Acquisition strengthens JCI's capabilities in fast-growing data center cooling segment

## Analysis
- The Alloy Enterprises acquisition adds proprietary liquid cooling technology to Johnson Controls' data center portfolio, complementing its traditional chiller and air-handling offerings. The 35% efficiency improvement and 75% pressure drop reduction are significant performance claims that would be highly valued by hyperscale operators focused on PUE optimization. Like Carrier's AquaEdge and Trane's LiquidStack, this acquisition confirms that all major HVAC players are racing to secure liquid cooling technology.
