# Federal Reserve Holds Rates Steady in March 2026 Amid Iran Conflict and Oil Crisis

## Key Decision

The Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) voted 11-1 to keep the benchmark federal funds rate anchored in a range between **3.50%-3.75%** at its March 18, 2026 meeting.

## Context and Rationale

- The uncertainty associated with the war with Iran that started nearly three weeks prior, and the fighting and its impact on the Strait of Hormuz, has roiled the global oil market
- Oil price shock threatened to keep inflation above the Fed's 2% target
- Economic data showing a slowdown in the labor market
- Inflation continuing to run hotter than the Fed's 2% target

## Dot Plot Projections

- The closely watched "dot plot" pointed to **one rate reduction in 2026** and **another in 2027**, though timing remains unclear
- This represents a significant downgrade from earlier expectations of multiple cuts in 2026

## Fed Chair Powell's Commentary

- Powell stated the global oil crisis may have only temporary economic effects
- Policymakers continue to pause rate cuts amid dual pressures of slowing growth and persistent inflation

## Construction Industry Impact

- Mortgage rates remained elevated at approximately **6.25%-6.43%** through March 2026
- The 30-year fixed mortgage averaged **6.37%** as of March 31, 2026
- 15-year mortgage rates averaged **5.75%**
- Construction lending standards showed slight easing per January 2026 SLOOS data, with net tightening at -3.16% (indicating net easing)
- Most forecasts anticipate moderation rather than a sharp drop in borrowing costs
- Projected trends suggest gradual improvement but not a return to pre-2022 conditions

## Market Implications

- The rate hold keeps construction financing costs elevated
- Home builders continue to face affordability headwinds
- Commercial real estate refinancing remains challenging with current rate coupons
- The trajectory of short-term rates and Fed balance-sheet policies influence mortgage rates and construction lending with a delay
