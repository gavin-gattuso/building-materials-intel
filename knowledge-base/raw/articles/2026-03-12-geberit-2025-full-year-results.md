# Geberit Reports 2025 Full-Year Results: Profit Flat, Dividend Breaches Payout Ceiling

## Key Details
- Net sales of CHF 3.16 billion, up 2.5% (or 4.8% adjusted for negative currency effects of CHF 72 million)
- Net income of CHF 598 million, up just 0.1% YoY
- Operating profit of CHF 767 million, up 0.7%
- EBITDA margin slightly down from 29.6% to 29.4%
- Net profit margin of 18.9%
- Personnel expenses grew 6.2% to CHF 834 million, outpacing revenue growth by 2+ percentage points
- One-off costs from German ceramics plant closure: EUR 18 million at EBITDA level, EUR 24 million at EBIT level
- Dividend proposed: CHF 12.90/share, up 0.8%, representing 71.1% payout ratio -- above the 50-70% target corridor
- Organic growth of 1.8%, primarily driven by renovation projects (70% of European volumes)
- Morgan Stanley: consensus 5% local-currency growth for 2026 "not out of reach"
- Next earnings report: May 5, 2026

## Analysis
- Geberit's results reflect the tension between solid organic growth (driven by renovation demand) and rising cost pressures. The dividend exceeding the payout ceiling is notable -- it signals the Board's willingness to reward shareholders even when margins are under pressure. The 70% renovation mix provides stability but also caps the upside in a new construction recovery scenario. Geberit trades at a significant premium (30x FY26E) which limits room for further multiple expansion.
