# Holcim Completes $30 Billion Amrize Spinoff, Creating Two Global Construction Giants

## Key Details
- **Spinoff date**: June 23, 2025
- **Structure**: 100% dividend-in-kind; 1 Amrize share per outstanding Holcim share
- **Ticker**: AMRZ (NYSE and SIX Swiss Exchange)
- **Amrize 2024 revenue**: $11.7 billion
- **Amrize 2024 adjusted EBITDA**: $3.7 billion
- **Amrize footprint**: 1,000+ sites, serving all U.S. states and Canadian provinces, 19,000 employees
- **Amrize leadership**: Jan Jenisch (former Holcim CEO 2017-2024) as Chairman and CEO
- **Amrize cost reduction**: $350 million SG&A cost-cutting program announced post-separation
- **Post-spinoff Holcim**: Focused on Europe, Latin America, Asia-Pacific under CEO Miljan Gutovic
- **Jefferies**: Upgraded Holcim to Buy post-spinoff; initiated Amrize at Hold

## Analysis
- The Amrize spinoff is the most significant structural event in the global cement industry in years
- It creates two pure-play entities: Amrize as the largest North American building solutions company, and Holcim as a focused international materials leader
- Jan Jenisch's move to lead Amrize signals the strategic importance of the North American business
- The $350M SG&A cost-cutting program suggests Amrize will pursue aggressive margin optimization as an independent company
- For Holcim shareholders, the spinoff unlocks value by allowing each entity to be valued on its own merits
- Amrize's independence gives it flexibility for North America-specific M&A, which was constrained under the Holcim umbrella
