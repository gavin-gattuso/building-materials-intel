# FOMC March 2026 Summary of Economic Projections: One Cut Projected, Inflation Raised

## Key Projections

### Federal Funds Rate (Dot Plot)
- Median end-of-2026 projection: **3.4%** (unchanged from December 2025 SEP)
- Implies **one 25bp rate cut** during 2026 (to 3.25-3.50%)
- 7 policymakers see rates unchanged in 2026, tied with 7 projecting one cut
- End-of-2027 projection: **3.00-3.25%** (two additional cuts)
- Long-run neutral rate estimate: unchanged

### GDP Growth
- 2026 GDP growth: **2.4%** (up from 2.3% in December projection)
- 2027 GDP growth: modest deceleration expected

### Inflation
- 2026 PCE inflation: **2.7%** (up from 2.4% in December -- significant increase)
- 2026 core PCE inflation: **2.7%** (up from 2.5% in December)
- 2027 PCE inflation: **2.2%** (up from 2.1%)
- Iran conflict oil shock driving upward inflation revision

### Unemployment
- 2026 Q4 average: **4.4%**
- 2027: **4.3%**
- 2028: **4.2%**

## Construction Industry Implications
- Elevated inflation forecast means mortgage rates unlikely to drop significantly in 2026
- Only one projected rate cut this year limits relief for construction financing costs
- Higher unemployment projection signals potential softening in housing demand
- The upward revision to inflation (2.4% to 2.7%) reflects Iran-driven energy costs and tariff pass-through
- Building materials companies face sustained high borrowing costs through at least mid-2026
