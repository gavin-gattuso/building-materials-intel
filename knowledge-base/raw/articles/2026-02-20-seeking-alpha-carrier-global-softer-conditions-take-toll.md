# Carrier Global: Softer Conditions Continue To Take Their Toll

## Key Points
- Carrier Q4 2025 revenue fell 6% year-over-year and missed revised guidance, signaling persistent headwinds
- High residential HVAC exposure leaves Carrier more vulnerable to housing market weakness than peers
- Analysts anticipate higher EPS in 2026 from modest sales growth, share buybacks, and lower effective tax rate
- Balance sheet is healthy following asset sales and debt paydown, allowing increased shareholder returns in 2026
- Carrier targeting organic sales growth rate of 6-8%, annual margin expansion of 50 basis points, and double-digit EPS growth
- Growth catalysts include data center cooling demand and potential policy easing, but residential headwinds and inflation pose risks
- Mixed analyst sentiment with buy ratings maintained but near-term caution warranted
