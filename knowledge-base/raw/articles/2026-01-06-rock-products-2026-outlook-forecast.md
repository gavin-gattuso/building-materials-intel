# 2026 Outlook/Forecast

## Key Points
- 2026 outlook for U.S. aggregates industry defined by a sharp bifurcation in demand
- Public infrastructure and megaprojects hitting peak consumption levels
- Despite overall construction spending declining 4.7% in 2025 in real value, data centers, utilities, and infrastructure demonstrated remarkable resilience
- Infrastructure projects expected to move into full execution in 2026 and 2027, raising aggregate production substantially
- Anticipated tariff increases may range from 5% to 25% depending on the material
- Aggregate tariff increase estimated at around 8% at current policy levels
- Private residential construction remains constrained by elevated borrowing costs
