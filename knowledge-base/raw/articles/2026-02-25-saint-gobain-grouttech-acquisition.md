# Saint-Gobain Acquires Grouttech, Continues Construction Chemicals Expansion

## Key Details
- Saint-Gobain acquired Grouttech, a Netherlands/Belgium-based construction chemicals specialist
- Grouttech solutions include concrete repair systems, grouting, injection resins, and technical flooring
- Completion expected by end of Q1 2026
- Most recent acquisition under Saint-Gobain's strategy; Maturix, Kilwaughter Minerals, and Fosroc were prior recent deals
- Saint-Gobain has completed 52 acquisitions total across Building Materials, Advanced Materials, and Retail Chains
- Construction chemicals sales targeted to reach EUR 9 billion by 2030, up from EUR 6.5 billion in 2024
- Part of broader "Lead & Grow" plan with EUR 14 billion allocated to investments and acquisitions (2026-2030)

## Analysis
- This bolt-on acquisition continues Saint-Gobain's aggressive push into construction chemicals, a market with higher margins and less cyclicality than traditional building materials.
- The Benelux entry strengthens Saint-Gobain's European construction chemicals footprint, complementing the recent Fosroc acquisition which focused on Asia/emerging markets.
- Construction chemicals is a fragmented market ripe for consolidation, and Saint-Gobain's EUR 14B war chest positions it as the dominant acquirer in this space through 2030.
