# Capstone Partners: Merger and Acquisition Outlook 2026

## Key Findings

- Transactions by large, capitalized companies flourished in 2025, private equity buyers mobilized, and CEO sentiments showed improvement, providing a backdrop for a **gradual reawakening of the middle market in 2026**
- **Building products sector**: Large-scale M&A expected to persist despite trade policy volatility pressuring profit margins
- **Construction services**: Sector participants' efforts to acquire scale and market share will likely keep construction M&A activity robust through 2026

## Building Products Outlook

- Ongoing strength from **booming data center construction** will likely uphold M&A momentum near-term
- A recent acceleration in **commercial and institutional construction planning** expected to further support sector deal flow into 2026
- Interest rate cuts, if materialized, and cheaper project financing will be catalysts for developers
- Pent-up demand for **affordable housing** remains a long-term growth lever

## Construction Services Outlook

- Targets serving **high-growth data center and power infrastructure end markets** expected to generate outsized acquisition interest as AI demand surges
- Deal flow upheld by booming data center construction and long-term growth levers

## Key Headwinds

- Profit margin strains accelerated as **new tariff policies drove construction material prices higher**
- Persistent **skilled labor shortages** pushed employee costs up and project timelines out
- Trade policy uncertainty delayed many transaction efforts in 2025
- Input cost inflation softened building products M&A activity through YTD 2025
