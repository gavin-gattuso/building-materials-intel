# ABC: Construction Industry to Face 'Real Risks' in 2026

## Key Warnings from ABC

- The construction industry faces "real risks" in 2026 despite underlying infrastructure demand
- Contractor backlog declining, signaling potential slowdown
- Multiple headwinds converging simultaneously

## Risk Factors Identified

### Cost Pressures
- Material costs surging from tariffs and energy price shock
- Input prices for nonresidential construction at **12.6% annualized** growth rate
- Labor costs rising **8-12%** in construction vs **3.5-4%** economy-wide

### Demand Uncertainty
- Consumer confidence Expectations Index below 80 for 13+ consecutive months (recession signal)
- GDP growth revised down to **2.0%** for 2026
- Housing permits declining -- down **5.8%** YoY in January

### Geopolitical Risk
- Strait of Hormuz crisis creating unprecedented energy cost uncertainty
- Oil price volatility directly impacts construction costs
- Supply chain disruptions from global shipping rerouting

### Financial Conditions
- Fed funds rate at **3.50-3.75%** -- elevated for construction financing
- Mortgage rates at **6.25-6.43%** constraining residential demand
- Only one rate cut projected for remainder of 2026

## Bright Spots

- IIJA infrastructure spending at peak disbursement ($131.2B available for FY2026)
- Data center construction remains robust
- Manufacturing construction supported by CHIPS Act
- CRE lending standards easing for first time since 2022

## Construction Employment

- Industry needs 349,000 net new workers in 2026
- Reflects cyclical slowdown more than structural improvement in labor availability
- More than half of needed workers are retiree replacements
