# Stifel Cuts Builders FirstSource Price Target to $93, Lowest on Street

## Key Details
- **Analyst**: W. Andrew Carter, Stifel
- **Action**: Lowered price target from $115 to $93 (19% cut)
- **Rating**: Maintained Hold
- **Key Concerns**: Continued softness in single-family housing starts; elevated mortgage rates; multi-family construction weakness
- **Context**: BLDR stock had already declined significantly from 2024 highs
- **For Reference**: DA Davidson also lowered PT to $111
- **Contrarian View**: RBC Capital upgraded to Outperform ($119 PT) on March 5

## Analysis
- Stifel's $93 price target is the lowest among major covering analysts and represents a deeply bearish view on the U.S. housing recovery timeline
- The PT cut reflects growing concern that the housing affordability crisis may persist longer than initially expected, with mortgage rates remaining above 6.5%
- BLDR is the most housing-sensitive stock among major building products companies, with revenue directly tied to single-family and multi-family starts
- The divergence between Stifel ($93) and RBC ($119 with Outperform) illustrates the fundamental debate: is the housing cycle bottoming or has it further to fall?
- For investors, BLDR at these levels is a pure play on the direction and timing of the U.S. housing recovery
