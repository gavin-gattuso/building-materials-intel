# HVAC Market Outlook 2026: Data Center Cooling Emerges as Fastest-Growing Segment

## Key Details
- Global HVAC market estimated at $282 billion (2025), growing to $302 billion (2026), +7.1% YoY
- Data center cooling market projected at $12.80 billion (2026), growing to $30.99 billion by 2034 at 16.2% CAGR
- Data center cooling is the fastest-growing HVAC sub-segment at 14.2% CAGR
- Liquid cooling now accounts for over 38% of new high-density installations
- Hybrid air-liquid systems becoming standard in cloud data centers
- Private equity heavily investing in data center-focused HVAC businesses
- Key acquisitions: Trane/LiquidStack, Daikin/Chilldyne, JCI/Alloy Enterprises
- Carrier launched AquaEdge 30CF data center chiller
- Operators prioritizing Water Usage Effectiveness (WUE) alongside PUE metrics
- Wave of HVAC consolidation expected in 2026 as players race for cooling technology

## Analysis
- Data center cooling has become the defining growth theme in HVAC for 2026 and beyond. All four major HVAC companies covered (Carrier, Trane, JCI, Daikin) have made significant data center investments. The shift from air to liquid cooling is a technology inflection point that favors companies with specialized expertise. The $12.8B market growing to $31B by 2034 represents a massive addressable market expansion. PE interest adds a bid floor for HVAC companies with data center exposure, supporting valuations across the sector.
